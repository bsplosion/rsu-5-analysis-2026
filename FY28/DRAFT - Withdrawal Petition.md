## Instructions for Signature Gatherers

This section is for the use of petition circulators only. Do not include these pages when presenting the petition to the Board of Selectpersons.

**What this petition does.** When signed by at least 109 registered Pownal voters (10% of the 1,081 votes cast for Governor in Pownal on November 8, 2022, per the Maine Secretary of State's certified results), this petition requires the Board of Selectpersons to hold a public hearing and call a special election on the question of withdrawing Pownal from RSU 5. The withdrawal process is governed by Title 20-A, §1466 of the Maine Revised Statutes.

**Who may sign.** Any registered voter of the Town of Pownal. Signers do not need to be present at the same time or location.

**How to circulate.** The petition has three parts: (1) a cover page with the full petition text, (2) signature pages, and (3) a statutory reference enclosure. Photocopy the signature pages as many times as needed and distribute copies to multiple gatherers. Each signature page is independently valid. Do not alter the text at the top of any page. Return all completed pages to: **\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_** (designated collection contact). When at least 109 valid signatures have been collected, assemble the cover page, all signed pages, and the statutory reference, and present them to the Board of Selectpersons.

**What happens next.** (1) The Selectpersons hold a posted public hearing at least 10 days before the election. (2) The Selectpersons call a special election. (3) Voters vote by secret ballot on the article described in the petition. (4) If a majority votes "Yes," the Town Clerk notifies RSU 5 and the Commissioner of Education by registered mail. (5) A four-member Withdrawal Committee is formed to negotiate the terms of withdrawal. (6) The final withdrawal agreement is subject to a separate referendum vote before taking effect.

**Important.** This petition initiates the withdrawal *process* — it does not finalize withdrawal. Voters will have a second opportunity to approve or reject the final withdrawal agreement after its terms have been negotiated. Signing indicates support for putting the question to a town vote, not necessarily a commitment to vote "Yes." The withdrawal process is fully reversible until the final referendum is approved.

# Petition to Withdraw the Town of Pownal from Regional School Unit No. 5

**Pursuant to Title 20-A, §1466, Maine Revised Statutes**

We, the undersigned registered voters of the Town of Pownal, County of Cumberland, State of Maine, hereby petition the municipal officers (Board of Selectpersons) of the Town of Pownal to call and hold a special election pursuant to Title 20-A, §1466 of the Maine Revised Statutes on the question of the withdrawal of the Town of Pownal from Regional School Unit No. 5 (RSU 5).

We request that the following article, in the form prescribed by §1466(2), be placed on the ballot at such special election:

> **"Article: Do you favor filing a petition for withdrawal with the board of directors of Regional School Unit No. 5 and with the Commissioner of Education, authorizing the withdrawal committee to expend $50,000 and authorizing the Board of Selectpersons to issue notes in the name of the Town of Pownal or otherwise pledge the credit of the Town of Pownal in an amount not to exceed $50,000 for this purpose?**
>
> **Yes &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; No"**

The Town of Pownal has been a member of Regional School Unit No. 5 since its formation in 2009, satisfying the 30-month minimum membership requirement of §1466(1). Under §1466(1)(A), this petition requires the signatures of at least 10% of the number of Pownal voters who voted in the last gubernatorial election. According to the Maine Secretary of State's certified results for the November 8, 2022 general election, 1,081 votes were cast for Governor in the Town of Pownal, establishing a minimum threshold of 109 signatures.

The signatures of the undersigned registered voters of the Town of Pownal appear on the attached signature pages.

## Statutory Authority

This petition is authorized by Title 20-A, §1466 of the Maine Revised Statutes.

**§1466(1):** "Beginning January 1, 2012, the residents of a municipality that has been a member of a regional school unit for at least 30 months may petition to withdraw from the regional school unit in accordance with this subsection."

**§1466(1)(A):** "Ten percent of the number of voters in the municipality who voted at the last gubernatorial election must sign the petition to withdraw from the regional school unit."

**§1466(1)(B):** "At least 10 days before the special election called pursuant to this paragraph, the municipal officers of the municipality within the regional school unit shall hold a posted or otherwise advertised public hearing on the petition. The municipal officers shall call and hold a special election in the manner provided for the calling and holding of town meetings or city elections to vote on the withdrawal from the regional school unit."

**§1466(1)(C):** "The petition to withdraw from the regional school unit must be approved by secret ballot by a majority vote of the voters present and voting before it may be presented to the regional school unit board and the commissioner."

**§1466(2):** The article to be voted upon must be in substantially the following form: *"Article: Do you favor filing a petition for withdrawal with the board of directors of regional school unit (name of regional school unit) and with the Commissioner of Education, authorizing the withdrawal committee to expend $ (insert amount) and authorizing the (municipal officers; i.e., selectpersons, town council, etc.) to issue notes in the name of the (name of the municipality) or otherwise pledge the credit of the (name of the municipality) in an amount not to exceed $ (insert amount) for this purpose? Yes No"*

RSU 5 is not a reformulated school administrative district; therefore the standard majority-vote threshold under §1466(9) applies to the final withdrawal referendum — not the 2/3 threshold of §1466(9-A).

Full text of §1466: https://www.mainelegislature.org/legis/statutes/20-a/title20-Asec1466.html
