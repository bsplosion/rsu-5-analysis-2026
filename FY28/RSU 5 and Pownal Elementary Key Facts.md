# RSU 5 and Pownal Elementary: Key Facts

**March 2026** | A public reference for residents of Durham, Freeport, and Pownal

All figures are sourced and documented in the companion *RSU 5 Analysis: Budget, Education, and Community Impact* (the "Omnibus"). Section references below point to that document for full data, context, and citations.

---

**"PES costs too much per student."**
PES's direct budget grew **1.3%/year** over the past six years — one-third the RSU rate (3.9%/year). The per-pupil cost increase is driven by enrollment dipping to 89 in FY24, not by spending. As enrollment recovers (now 105), per-pupil costs are falling.
*(Omnibus, Sections 3.1–3.2)*

**"Closing PES saves $2 million."**
After accounting for the cost of educating 104 Pownal students at other schools and 8 new bus routes, closing PES produces a **net annual cost of ~$161,000** — not savings. Risk-adjusted (property values, attrition, transition): **-$560K to -$650K/year**.
*(Omnibus, Sections 2.3, 4.5)*

**"PES enrollment is declining."**
PES is the **only elementary in RSU 5 with consistent enrollment growth**: up from 89 (FY24) to 105 (FY27 projected) — an 18% recovery. MSS (-42) and MLS (-17) are declining.
*(Omnibus, Section 1.4)*

**"PES is overstaffed."**
PES is the **only school whose staffing declined** over the past decade (-8.0%). Every other school grew (+3.9% to +36.5%). Central office grew +34.6%. PES's share of district staff (~4.5%) is roughly half its share of students (~10.5%).
*(Omnibus, Sections 1.5, 3.5)*

**"The budget keeps going up because of PES."**
The RSU budget grew from $29.5M to $47.4M over a decade (+61%). PES's direct budget grew from $1.65M to $1.78M over six years (+8.2%). PES grew at **one-third the district rate**.
*(Omnibus, Section 3.4)*

**"We have to close PES to meet the EC mandate."**
The EC special education classroom can be placed at MSS, which has the largest PreK program (64 students) and strong LRE ratios. All three schools can continue community PreK. No school closure is required.
*(Omnibus, Section 2.2)*

**"There's no alternative to closing PES."**
Path A — middle school grade consolidation — saves **$479K–$804K/year** while preserving all schools. Combined with revenue alternatives, the total offset is **$1.3M–$1.9M**.
*(Omnibus, Sections 2.2, 2.5)*

**"This is a new problem."**
Cost-sharing and PES cost concerns have been documented for **17 years** — since before RSU 5 opened in 2009. Pownal's middle school was closed in 2010. The cost-sharing formula has been debated and left unchanged since 2012.
*(Omnibus, Part VIII)*

**"The cost-sharing formula is fair."**
Pownal pays **$382,000/year more** than a per-student formula would yield. The 85/15 (valuation/enrollment) ALM formula is a Board policy choice, not a state requirement. It can be changed by Board vote.
*(Omnibus, Section 5.2)*

**"What does Pownal actually pay?"**
Pownal's FY27 RSU assessment: **$4,448,225** (58.4% of Pownal's total tax bill). That's **$21,699/student** in local taxes — nearly double Durham's $11,034/student.
*(Omnibus, Sections 5.3–5.4)*

**"What happens next?"**
FY28 baseline: ~$50.8M. EC mandate deadline: July 2027. The Board is commissioning a facilities study. Community forums are expected in 2026. The FY27 budget goes to referendum in June 2026.
*(Omnibus, Sections 2.1, 2.7)*

**"Could Pownal withdraw from RSU 5?"**
Maine law provides a 22-step withdrawal process used by 15+ towns since 2013. Freeport nearly left in 2014 (failed by 76 votes). Financial modeling shows independence could **save Pownal ~$522K/year** (~$821/household) by eliminating the formula penalty.
*(Omnibus, Sections 6.2, 6.5)*
