# DRAFT — FOAA Request to RSU 5 Superintendent

**Town of Pownal Select Board**

---

Superintendent Gray,

Thank you for your response. The Town of Pownal is submitting this request for public records pursuant to Maine's Freedom of Access Act, Title 1, Chapter 13, §408-A. Because of the surprise announcement of a possible "repurposing" of the Pownal Elementary School, and in order to accelerate the information needed to accurately assess the financial and educational implications of the school administrative unit withdrawal process needed to inform the potential withdrawal committee, the Town of Pownal requests the following records and data.

We request that responsive records be provided in electronic format where available (native spreadsheet format preferred for financial data; PDF for meeting minutes and contracts). We respectfully request acknowledgment within 5 business days per §408-A(2) and ask that all responsive records be produced within 15 business days. The Town reserves its rights under §409 in the event of unreasonable delay.

Unless otherwise noted, "current" means the most recent version available, and all requests cover records from January 1, 2020 through the present date.

---

## 1. Financial Obligations and Commitments (§1466(4)(A)(6))

Specify the distribution of all financial commitments extending beyond the proposed effective date. Provide copies of the minutes of Board meetings that document agreement with this item. This shall include, but not be limited to:

a. Outstanding bonds, including amortization schedules and Pownal's share under the cost-sharing formula for each instrument
b. Notes
c. Debt service schedules
d. Leases or lease-purchase agreements
e. Any matters in litigation or pending/threatened claims
f. Any other contractual obligations extending beyond the proposed withdrawal date

## 2. Superintendent and Administrative Financial Commitments (§1466(4)(A)(7))

Specify how the withdrawing town and the RSU will meet any financial commitments to the RSU superintendent that extend beyond the effective date of the withdrawal, including:

a. The superintendent's current employment contract (term, compensation, benefits, severance provisions)
b. Contracts for assistant superintendent, business manager, and any other central office administrators with multi-year terms

## 3. Division of Real and Personal Property (§1466(4)(A)(10))

Specify how all real and all personal property will be disposed of or divided. Provide copies of the minutes of Board meetings that document agreement with this item. This shall include, but not be limited to:

a. Buildings — including current assessed value and any encumbrances
b. Land, including the enforcement of any existing deed restrictions or reversionary clauses
c. Buses and other vehicles — inventory with age, condition, and mileage
d. Equipment (instructional, custodial, kitchen, technology)
e. Supplies
f. Fuel
g. Infrastructure upgrades and capital improvements made at each school
h. Technology assets — hardware, network infrastructure, and software/platform licenses (SIS, LMS, assessment tools)

## 4. Division of Monetary Assets (§1466(4)(A)(10))

Specify how monetary assets will be disposed of or divided and how the new school administrative unit will manage and distribute any such assets. Provide copies of the minutes of Board meetings that document agreement with this item. This shall include, but not be limited to:

a. Year-end fund balances (unassigned, assigned, committed, restricted) for the past 3 fiscal years
b. Trusts, gifts, endowments, or scholarships — including any restricted to Pownal students or PES
c. Accounts receivable
d. Prepaid expenses
e. Investments
f. Uncollected insurance proceeds
g. Locally established retirement programs or post-employment benefit obligations

## 5. Educational Services and Student Data (§1466(4)(A)(1))

The withdrawal agreement must provide educational services for all Pownal students and establish tuition rates. To inform this provision, provide:

a. Current enrollment by grade level, school, and town of residence (FY22 through present)
b. Number of Pownal-resident students attending each RSU 5 school
c. Current and historical per-pupil expenditure data by school (FY22 through present)
d. Tuition rates as published under §5804 and §5805 (elementary and secondary)
e. Special education caseloads: number of students with IEPs, by municipality and disability category, and total expenditures attributable to Pownal-resident students
f. Number of Pownal-resident students with Section 504 plans, receiving ELL services, or receiving gifted/talented services
g. Any existing tuition agreements with other school administrative units

## 6. School Construction and Facilities (§1466(4)(A)(3))

The withdrawal agreement must establish that withdrawal will not create a need for state-eligible school construction within 5 years. Provide:

a. Any facility condition assessments, engineering reports, or architectural studies for any RSU 5 school
b. The comprehensive facility study RFQ issued in 2026, any responses received, and any draft or final deliverables
c. Capital improvement plans and deferred maintenance lists for all schools
d. Any pending or planned school construction projects and their state-funding eligibility status
e. Building capacity data for all schools (designed capacity vs. current enrollment)
f. Any communications with the Maine Department of Education regarding school construction, major capital projects, or the status of Pownal Elementary School since January 1, 2025

## 7. Transportation (§1466(4)(A)(4))

The withdrawal agreement must establish how transportation services will be provided. Provide:

a. Current bus routes serving Pownal-resident students (maps, route descriptions, mileage, ridership by route)
b. All transportation contracts (vendor, term, rates, renewal provisions)
c. District-owned transportation fleet inventory (number, type, age, condition, assigned routes)
d. Transportation budget, broken out by municipality or route where available
e. Any inter-district transportation agreements

## 8. Administration of the New Unit (§1466(4)(A)(5))

The withdrawal agreement must provide for administration of the new unit. Provide:

a. Current central office organizational chart and staffing (all positions, FTEs, compensation)
b. Administrative cost allocation by municipality (or the methodology used to allocate)
c. Any existing shared-services, supervisory union, or cooperative agreements with other districts
d. Current administrative contracts (superintendent, assistant superintendent, business manager, curriculum director, special education director, and other central office positions)

## 9. Collective Bargaining Agreements (§1466(4)(A)(8))

The withdrawal agreement must provide for continuation and assignment of collective bargaining agreements. Provide:

a. All current collective bargaining agreements (teachers' association, educational technicians/support staff, custodial/maintenance, transportation, and any other bargaining units)
b. Duration and expiration dates of each agreement
c. Any pending grievances, arbitrations, or unfair labor practice complaints
d. Number of bargaining unit members currently assigned to PES or regularly serving Pownal-resident students

## 10. Continuing Contract Rights (§1466(4)(A)(9))

The withdrawal agreement must provide for continuing contract rights under §13201. Provide:

a. List of all certified staff currently assigned to PES (name, position, certification area, years of service)
b. List of all non-certified staff currently assigned to PES
c. List of itinerant, specialist, or shared staff who serve Pownal students at PES or other schools
d. Seniority lists by bargaining unit
e. Individual employment contracts for PES-assigned staff

## 11. Child Nutrition Services (§1466(4)(A)(12))

The withdrawal agreement must provide for child nutrition in compliance with state and federal law. Provide:

a. Current food service program structure for PES (in-house or contracted; staffing)
b. USDA program participation status for PES (National School Lunch Program, School Breakfast Program, Community Eligibility Provision, etc.)
c. Food service revenue and expenditure by school
d. Food service equipment inventory at PES
e. Free and reduced-price lunch eligibility rates by school and by municipality

## 12. Budget and Revenue Data (§1466(4)(A)(13))

The withdrawal agreement must include an anticipated first-year budget in the §1485 cost center summary format. To construct this budget, provide:

a. Detailed adopted budgets by cost center for all RSU 5 schools (FY22 through present) — line-item detail, not summary totals only
b. Revenue detail: state subsidy (EPS) allocation by municipality, local assessment by municipality, federal grant allocations (Title I, Title II, Title IV, IDEA, Perkins, rural/small schools, and any others), and all other revenue sources
c. The cost-sharing formula under §1481-A and its current calculations, including the property valuation and enrollment data used
d. Most recent audited financial statements (FY23, FY24, FY25 — or the three most recent available)
e. Current-year (FY27) actuals: year-to-date expenditures and revenues as of the most recent reporting period
f. State EPS model data for Pownal (allocation components, per-pupil rates, adjustments)

## 13. Insurance (operational need)

a. All insurance policies covering RSU 5 operations — property, general liability, professional liability, workers' compensation, student accident, cyber liability — including premiums, coverage limits, and which policies specifically cover PES or its operations
b. Any self-insurance or pooled-insurance arrangements

## 14. Governance and Organizational Documents (§1466(4)(A)(11))

a. The RSU 5 Reorganization Plan as approved under §1461
b. Current Board bylaws
c. Current Board composition, apportionment, and weighted voting structure
d. Any amendments to the Reorganization Plan since its adoption

## 15. Board Actions Related to Pownal Elementary School

In addition to the minutes requested in items 1, 3, and 4 above, provide:

a. All Board meeting minutes, agendas, and supporting materials (including superintendent recommendations, presentations, and exhibits) from January 1, 2025 through the present date that reference or relate to PES, school configuration, school closure, school "repurposing," early childhood or preschool facility planning, or attendance zones
b. Any written communications between the superintendent and the Board (or individual Board members) regarding PES closure, repurposing, or reconfiguration
c. Any executive session agendas that list topics related to PES, school closure, or school reconfiguration (noting that we do not request the content of executive sessions, only the stated topics as required to appear on the agenda)

## Regarding the Characterization of Board Action on PES

Separately from the records requested above, the Town of Pownal requests that the RSU 5 Board and Superintendent clarify the RSU 5 position on whether the action identified as "school repurposing" constitutes a "school closing" as defined by Title 20-A §1512(4): "any action by the regional school unit board that has the effect of providing no instruction for any students at that school." This clarification has material implications for the statutory protections available to the Town and its residents, including the municipal referendum requirement under §1512(1). If the RSU 5 position is that the proposed action is not a "school closing" within the meaning of §1512(4), we request the legal basis for that position in writing. If the RSU 5 position is that the proposed action does not constitute a "school closing" within the meaning of §1512(4), we further request all records, memoranda, or legal opinions in the RSU's possession that support that characterization.

---

## In Summary

According to the Maine Department of Education, "changing the makeup of a school administrative unit has serious implications for the education of students residing in the unit and for the citizens, both local and statewide, who provide support for the unit. For this reason, the process of withdrawal is complex with necessary involvement of many parties."

The Town of Pownal looks forward to working with the RSU 5 Board and Superintendent to help inform Pownal residents in this important decision.
