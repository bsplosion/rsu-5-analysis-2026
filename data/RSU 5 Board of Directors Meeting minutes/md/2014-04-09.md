6. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Board members report out on “Dine and Discuss” conversations — Chair Larkins thanked all
those in attendance tonight. Nelson and Candy Decsipkes reported out on conversations at their
tables which included positives of the RSU such as: professional development; sharing of
services; culture of the school community; and gifted and talented. More immediate concerns
included: athletics and the track; increase volunteerism; withdrawal; and communication on how
to get state funding information out to the public.

B. Withdrawal Update — Michelle Ritcheson reported the Working Group continues to work
through the draft plan from the Freeport Withdrawal Committee. Another joint meeting will be
held on April 16, 2014. Discussions with other districts regarding Central Office services and
tuition students are ongoing. A meeting with MSAD 51 is scheduled for April 14, 2014

10. WORKSHOP:

A. Transition to a Proficiency-Based Diploma Grades 9-12 — Joe Makley, Curriculum Director
presented the third in a series of workshops to provide an overview of Proficiency-Based
Diplomas. Joe reviewed the work that has taken place starting with the 2011-2012 school year
including: professional development in curriculum design for standards; identification of
standards; development of courses/curriculum; piloting cross curricular standards; continued
professional development; development of assessments for power standards and beginning the
revision process for policy IKF and IKE. Community outreach has taken place through such
avenues as the State of the Schools Report, parent public presentation, online discussions,
student meetings with guidance counselors, and 8" grade parent night. The work will continue
in 2014-2015.

B. FY '?15 Budget Review — Kelly Wentworth, Finance Director reviewed the two documents
handed out at the Budget presentation on March 25, 2014. The video of that meeting is available
online. Kelly pointed out we will be looking at declining enrollments with additional students
attending Charter Schools. Two adjustments will need to be made to the budget document due
to an increase in Region 10 and health insurance costs. Kelly also reviewed a new handout that
provided information on Administrative budget requests and an Educational Technician
comparison chart.

Board members asked questions about the following: floating custodian; NWEA testing; and
professional development.
