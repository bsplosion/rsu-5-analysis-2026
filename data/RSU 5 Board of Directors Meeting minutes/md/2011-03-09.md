5. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. The Girls Alpine and Boys Combined State Champion ski teams were recognized for their
accomplishments.

B. Durham Community School Public Service Announcement —Ryan Gleason provided an update
on the work the School Culture Action Team has been doing. This year's major work is on
reducing peer aggression. The school has been awarded a $14,000 grant from the Juvenile Justice
Authority of Maine to implement collaborative problem solving. Eliza Damone, SCAT Teacher
Leader and School Librarian has been working with students in a multimedia class to create
public service announcements. The intended message to bystanders is that stepping in and
stepping up can make a difference in a person's life. The Board watched a public service
announcement created by Bailey Karnes and Grace Bertrand, two of the student ambassadors.

6. WORKSHOP:

A. Fields and Grounds Update:

Craig Sickels, Athletic Director and members of the Fields and Grounds Committee reviewed
plans and provided additional information for the “Campus Complex” as requested by the Board
at the February 16, 2011 meeting. Mr. Sickels reported he attended recent meetings of the
selectmen and town councilors from Durham, Freeport and Pownal to explain the project and
gain a better understanding of the level of support and concern. There was support for the project
but concerns regarding the timing, cost and tax impact. Additional information was also provided
on the annual maintenance costs and equipment. Mr. Sickels reported prior to the Board meeting
this evening he met with representatives from the surrounding neighborhood to listen to their
concerns regarding the project.

Public Comment:
Durham: Norman White commented he is concerned about a large expenditure at this time. The
RSU should be looking at reductions not additions.
Freeport: Kim Lamarre and Michelle Broderick presented information to the Board on a High
School renovation project to improve safety and accessibility of the front of the school,
appearance of the facade and entrance area, and to enhance the interior appearance.
Ethel Wilkerson mentioned at the meeting earlier this evening the neighborhood residents were
able to layout their concerns. She is optimistic that with improvements the plan can work for the
school and neighborhood. She thanked the Board for listening.
Zac Ward spoke of his concerns regarding Friday and Saturday night activities. Other concerns
include quality of life, speed and safety and the need for a consistent message being sent from
Craig Sickels to the neighbors and Board.
Kristen Dorsey mentioned some of the concerns sound like municipal issues.
Nick White spoke of his concern with spending money on the project at this time when we have
decaying schools. Education should come first.
Pownal: Tim Giddinge is supportive of the concept but would first like to see alternative funding
sources before going to bond.

B. Use of Time Committee Report:

Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, reported the draft calendar was sent out for feedback
with two questions. The first question was about a before or after Labor Day start and the
second was the placement of professional learning time. The committee met and made
adjustments to the calendar to show an after Labor Day start and moved the January 3" PLD to
March and adjusted the March 16" day. There was also support for the elimination of early
release and late arrival days.
Public Comment:
Freeport: Students Danielle Perry, Jess Hench, Patrick LaFlamme and Jack Baker feel students
have a lack of voice regarding late arrival. Students use this time constructively for SAT prep,
club meetings, group projects, and catching up on sleep mid-week. They collected 134 signatures
on a petition to keep late arrival. Other students that spoke in favor of late arrival were: Alex
Sturtevant, Charlie Lehmer, Sadie Russell and Alex Campbell.
Mary Lehmer spoke in favor of late arrival for students and asked about the actual number of
feedback responses.
Liza Moore spoke as a member of the committee and thanked Sarah Simmonds for her
leadership. Liza spoke to the importance of late arrival for sleep purposes for students.
Jessica Sturges spoke to the importance of K-12 teachers having the opportunity to meet. The
piece missing with early release and late arrival is the 8" and 9" grade teachers not being able to
meet together.
Kristen Dorsey notes her concerns with loss of professional development time with the calendar.
She also mentioned students need to have a voice.
Hank Ogilby thanked Sarah Simmonds for her work with the committee. Hank noted his concern
with the loss of professional learning time. Also mentioned was the proposed policy on
instructional planning time that doesn't guarantee time during the school day.
Beth Parker spoke in favor of late arrival due to her children having no time after school due to
sports and other activities.
