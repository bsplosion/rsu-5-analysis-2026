7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Pownal 5" and 6" Grade Transition: Principals Lisa Demick, Ray Grogan and Will Pidden have
worked together to create a transition plan for the Pownal 5" and 6" grade students. For the
2011-2012 school year these students will be offered the choice of attending either Durham
Community School or Freeport Middle School. A timeline has been created beginning in
December with the principals meeting with the Pownal students to talk to them about each
school. In January meetings will be held with parents to hear about what each school offers. By
the end of January a decision on the choice of school will be needed from each family. Between
February and June transition activities will be planned for students to help make the transition as
smooth as possible.

B. Work and College Readiness — Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, reported the Strategic
Framework commits to identifying the work and college readiness skills our students need to
learn to be successful in both college and the world of work. Sarah reviewed roles and
responsibilities of the task force and the time line of the work to be completed. The work will
begin on December 2™ with a meeting to solicit essential work readiness skills from business,
non-profit, and municipal employers. Rubrics will be developed first followed by the
assessments. Heidi McGinley, an educational consultant who worked with us last year on the
Use of Time task force will help facilitate this important work.

Dennis Ouellette, Director of Facilities and Transportation reported the lockers for Freeport High
School have been ordered. Total cost for 108 new lockers delivered and installed total $11,286.
Dennis followed up with the committee regarding the Board's decision last year to review
transportation services for the 2011-2012-budget year. The Finance Committee would like the
following three transportation options to be considered: 1) Freeport local, Pownal leased buses,
and contracted Durham; 2)Freeport local, Pownal and Durham contracted; and 3) Freeport,
Pownal, and Durham all contracted. Committee members asked Dennis Ouellette to talk with his
employees last Friday to notify them of the analysis. After the analysis is complete, the Finance
Committee will make a recommendation to the Board. Warrants were signed.

B. Communications — Chair Jane Blais reported the committee met on November 1, 2010. The
committee members gave an update on communications with Parent Groups and municipal
officials. Jane will meet with Durham's PTA in late November and Kristen has done a wonderful
job meeting with Freeport groups. In general, the parent groups welcome communication efforts
regarding program and budget updates. The groups support the pre-budget workshops with
parent groups; the posting of Board and parent group information on the RSU website; and
continued written and personal communications contacts. Jane Blais extended an invitation to
Durham's Selectmen to attend the Board's social on November 3™. An invitation will also be
extended to the Freeport Town Council prior to the December 8" social in Freeport.

The Committee members reviewed the list of possible invitees to the College and Career
Readiness Skills discussions. The Strategic Framework commits to the identification of these
skills and development of assessments tat require students to demonstrate those skills.

E. Negotiations - Nelson Larkins reported teacher negotiations are ongoing. Peter Murry has been
added to the team and is working on spreadsheets with John Green.
