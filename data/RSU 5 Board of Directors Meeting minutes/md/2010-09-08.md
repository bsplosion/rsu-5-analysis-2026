6. WORKSHOP:
A. Goal Setting Linked to Board Self-Evaluation
The Board reviewed the results of the self-evaluation. The overall rating of 79% reflects a
highly effective Board who just completed a very successful year. The seven dimensions on the
evaluation were contextual, educational, finance, interpersonal, analytical, political and strategic.
The Board discussed the following for goals: assigning mentors to new Board members;
communication with parent groups and town leadership on a regular basis to give and get
feedback on upcoming items; community informal round tables in each town; work on budget
priorities through administrators and the community before budget season begins;
communication of budget information; and boardsmanship training. The Board will now set
goals linked to the self-evaluation.
