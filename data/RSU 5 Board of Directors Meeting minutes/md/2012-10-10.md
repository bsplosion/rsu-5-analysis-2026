5"" graders learning casting skills and fly-tying with Geoff Dyhrberg's Fishing Club; swim

6. WORKSHOP:

A. Energy Efficiencies and Capital Planning - Dennis Ouellette, Facilities and Transportation
Director reviewed Capital Budget projects completed in 2012 including: Mast Landing and
Pownal carpet replacement; Morse Street School paving; Pownal kitchen freezer; Pownal
phone and intercom system; High School and Morse Street dishwashers; Freeport Middle
School boiler replacement; Durham and High School Choices Program room renovations;
improvements to Central Office and the Middle School. The district has seen a savings due to
energy efficiencies from the conversion to natural gas in four of the schools and also by
entering into a rate agreement with Maine Power Options to reduce our average rate for
electricity.

Dennis Ouellette also reviewed the 2013-2014 anticipated Capital Budget. Each year Facilities
Committee members tour the schools and review requests from the Principals to rebuild the Five
Year Capital Plan and reprioritize. Items for 2013-2014 include: one handicapped equipped bus;
pick-up truck for field work; Pownal trim repair; Morse Street carpet and roof in wing D;
Freeport Middle school elevator upgrade; and Mast Landing dishwasher.

B. Durham, Freeport, Pownal Municipal Leadership - Selectmen/Town Council and Budget
Advisory members from our three towns were invited to the meeting with a goal of
increasing communication and build relationships. Those in attendance broke out into small
groups to discuss with Board members what their hopes, fears, questions, and needs for more
information are as we prepare for development of the RSU's FY14 budget. Board members
will report out on their groups feedback at the October 24" Board meeting.
