5. WORKSHOP:

A. Freeport High School Master Plan Update - Lyndon Keck, PDT Architects reported on work
completed to date on the Master Plan. Meetings have been held in each of the three towns with
municipal officials and the communities. A meeting was also held with the neighbors to the north
of the school. The study is three-quarters of the way done and has 8 components including: new
entry front door with security controls, new finishes in 1961 hallways, and new fiber optic
backbone; north science wing with one small classroom; classroom expansion on south side of
science wing, two classrooms; new two-story addition with new cafeteria and kitchen, new choral
room, two new art rooms, new alternative education rooms, new robotics classroom, and theatre
workshop with dressing rooms and add new athletic storage; wellness center with weight room and
aerobic movement room; repurposing administration and student services in old cafeteria and old
administrative wing; renovating six classrooms in 1961 wing; and site work. Mr. Keck reported
there are opportunities to expand on the current site and the building is a candidate for renovations.
Plans were reviewed showing a second and third story expansion, which could be built in stages.
Mr. Keck also explained how he arrived at the square footage and capacity of the current building.
A previous study completed in 2004 by a different group arrived at a higher capacity. Projected
enrollments at the high school going out to 2021-22 were provided by NESDEC. Next steps
include a traffic and parking study. The Master Plan study is expected to be completed by the end
of April or early May.

B. Use of Time Update ~ Joe Makley reported the committee met on March 7, 2012 to review public
feedback from the draft calendar proposal and make a final recommendation to the Board. As a
result of the survey, 270+ responses with significant individual comments were received. Many
parents spoke in support of teachers' professional learning time, and for their ability to have a voice
in the scheduling of flex-time. There was a statistical lack of support for requesting an additional

flex-day, so the recommendation is to continue with 5 professional workdays and two comp days.
The other topic of consideration was a proposed professional learning day in May for school-based
teams to have an opportunity for data analysis, reflection, and setting targets, etc. for their strategic
plans. Some survey comments indicated that having this in May would be difficult, because the
schools are involved in end-of year testing, and the data from the spring testing would not be
available to the planning groups. The committee recommended moving one professional learning
day to the end of the school year in June.
After a discussion by Board members it was asked that the Use of Time Committee meet again and
bring back calendar options for the Board to review and to include additional information such as
how the teachers used their flex-time this year and to separate out the parent and teacher survey
data.

C. State of the Schools Update — Superintendent Welsh reported the State of Schools report will be
reviewed by the Strategic Communications Committee tomorrow evening and a print date,
depending on the committee's feedback, has been set. The report will be mailed to all homes in the
RSU beginning March 22™. The report includes an invitation to the April 2"! Education Forum
with Commissioner Stephen Bowen. We will begin advertising the Forum with blurbs in
newsletters, Delivering on the Mission, Cable television. The Board and staff members will receive
a copy of the report before it is mailed homes.
