5. WORKSHOP: PRESENTATION OF RSU NO. 5 FY'11 BUDGET:
Superintendent Welsh reported this will be a continuation of last evening's presentation on the
Superintendent's Recommended 2010-2011 RSU No. 5 budget. The Budget Goal is to continue to
increase educational excellence while demonstrating a greater efficiency in the use of our resources.
Staff and administration have spent months restructuring, reallocating, and reducing the budget with
the goal of continuing to provide a high quality education to our students and retaining staff
positions.
There is a budget decrease of ($596,965) or -2.7%. This results in a projected tax impact (due to
offsetting local revenues) as follows:
¢ Durham — a reduction of — $21,825
¢ Freeport — an increase of $12,738
¢ Pownal —a reduction of — $4,730
A lengthy discussion took place on the recommendation in the budget is to replace Pownal's current
middle school program with the choice of attending either Durham or Freeport middle schools. This
recommendation provides for a reduction of three teaching positions and a restructured
administrative position for a budget reduction of approximately $180,000.
Continued budget deliberations will occur over the next two months ending May 26" with a three-
town Annual Budget Meeting followed by a Budget Validation Referendum on June 8". All citizens
are encouraged to participate in the process.

6. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Legislative Update: Superintendent Welsh reported on a Resolution Seeking Fair, Equitable, and
Transparent Funding of Education in Maine adopted by area Boards. The Resolution outlines
the lack of state funding for education, cuts during the school year on an emergency basis, and
the lack of clear information from the Department of Education.

VOTED: (1) To adopt the Resolution as presented. (Dube — Parker) (11 — 0)

B. Strategic Framework Indicators: Superintendent Welsh reported the Financial Indicator Task
Force met last week and again this evening. The Performance Indicators Task Force will meet
tomorrow. Dr. Welsh has been in contact with David Silvernail, Director of the Center for
Education Policy, Applied Research, and Evaluation at USM to discuss his research on how
high performing schools spend their money and the direct impact on student performance. The
report will be released after David Silvernail reports out to the Education Committee. His
research ties directly into the work we are doing.
