4. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Cheryl White — Technology Presentation: Cheryl White, Principal of Morse Street School,

introduced Kristy Johnson a second grade teacher. Kristy demonstrated how she integrates

technology into her classroom. This year the class is using a Wiki so students can record what they
learn each week then upload it to the Wiki for parents to hear what their child learned that week. The
class has also joined the online Community Collaborative Rain, Hail and Snow Network and

Climate Connections to work with mapping and math skills.

6. REPORTS FROM DIRECTORS:

A. Russell Packett — Opening of School: Russell Packett, Director of Freeport Recreation and
Community Education explained the services his department provides to the communities
including: Adult GED with 12-15 students participating per year; ESOL with 35-40 students
participating per year; youth and adult recreation; Teen Center; scheduling for all school
facilities in Freeport; and the child care program with 91 total families participating.

B. Dennis Ouellette - Opening of School: Dennis Ouellette, Director of Facilities and
Transportation extended a thank you to the custodians for their hard work over the summer.
Projects completed this summer include: Mast Landing School roof; Freeport High School roof
over the gym and storage area, the master clock system, and some electrical wiring; insulation
replacement and new energy efficient light bulbs at Pownal Elementary; field work at Durham
Elementary; two new buses are on order and expected to arrive in November.

C. Judy Goodenow — Opening of School: Judy Goodenow, Director of Food Service mentioned a
$6,000 grant was received from the Maine Dairy Council to paint, put up new signs and purchase
a new milk cooler for the High School cafeteria. Maine Harvest Day was September 17" all food
was purchased locally. 334 students in the district or 20% receive free or reduced lunch.
Currently we are looking at inviting the elderly to lunch once a month.

D. Joan Nason — Opening of School: Joan Nason, Director of Student Services reported there are
231 students identified and 8 out of district placements. Two of the RSU towns, Pownal and
Freeport, have pre-school programs. Freeport's program was revamped into an integrated
program with some students with disabilities and some students typically developing. One of the
areas being looked at is unifying how the three towns identify Gifted and Talented. There are
currently 21 ESL students in the district. H1N1 clinics have been set up in the three communities
for October 7", 12™ and 20".

E. Diane Boucher — Opening of School: Diane Boucher, Director of Finance and Human Resources
reported the business office is now fully staffed, the Accounts Payable position has been filled.
There are just under 400 employees and 90% have direct deposit. On October 9" advices will be
e-mailed to all staff that have direct deposit. The business office is currently working on having
purchase orders online.

F. Craig Sickels — Opening of School: Craig Sickels, Director of Athletics reported pre-season
started on August 17" and Fall Kick-Off was September 11'". Coastal Orthopedic & Sports
Medicine is working with us to provide athletic trainer services at 2 or 3 games/practices per
week at no cost to the RSU. Participation in High School sports is at 190 and 223 for the three
middle schools.

7. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Community Forum on Strategic Plan: Superintendent Welsh reported she has received positive
feedback on the forum. Several people have thanked us for holding a well-organized and
informative event. Great Schools will report back to the Board at the October 14" Board
meeting.

B. DOE Financial Update: Superintendent Welsh reported there is no concrete information at this
time from the DOE. A revenue shortfall for the current year is still anticipated of approximately
$40 million dollars. The Education Committee is looking at the possibility of shutdown days for
schools, special education costs, salary freezes, retirement incentives. We are working locally
and at the State level. Locally we are working on a salary analysis to see if there are savings due
to staff changes.

C. Naming the RSU: Superintendent Welsh reported that we will begin the process for naming the
RSU. The Student Mentor group at the high school will be asked to help educate and facilitate
student discussions. Students will initially research the history and geography of the three towns.
A summary of the information will be created and shared with all students. Each of the schools
will recommend a name for the RSU. The Board will then establish a panel of five Board
members to survey the citizens of the three towns to gather input about the recommended names
and make a final recommendation to the Board in November/December. Following the naming

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications: Jen Kaplan reported the Communications Sub-Committee met on September
17". The two items discussed were the Community Forum and Naming the RSU. The sub-
committee felt the approach used to inform and invite the public to the forum was effective and a
similar plan will be used for the next forum in October. A communications plan for the work
related to the naming of the RSU was considered by the sub-committee. The next meetings of the
sub-committee will be held on October 8" at 7:30AM and October 22" at 7:30AM.

B. Finance: Eric Dube reported the Finance Sub-Committee met on September 16". Discussions
around Pownal Road field were held. Seacost is looking for the town to make a decision by
November 30". A stakeholders group meting took place on September 17". The sub-committee
heard an update on the 3-5 year financial plan. Jim Rier from the Department of Education and
his team are working on numbers. Jim will be asked to attend the October 14" Finance Sub-
Committee meeting to provide information for review and decisions about our next steps. A
process for capital and facility planning is being worked on and a council member or selectmen
from each town will be invited to join the committee. The Warrant was signed.

C. Negotiations: Nelson Larkins reported negotiations still continue to meet.

D. Policy: Cori Holt reported the Policy Sub-Committee met on September 18'". No feedback was
received on policy packet 10-1. The sub-committee prepared packet 10-2 for feedback. The
committee is recommending that the Board include the “Pledge of Allegiance” as part of their
meeting and add to the agenda template for policy BEDB. The committee is also recommending
adding “Good News and Recognition” to the agenda template for student and staff recognition
and presentations. Policy BEDFA on abstentions is included in packet 10-2 and the committee
encourages all Board members review it. The committee discussed the creation of a policy to
include three student advisory members with representation from each town. Students would not
