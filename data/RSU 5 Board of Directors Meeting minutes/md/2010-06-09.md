6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government — Sam Skold reported on what's happening at Freeport High
School including: Graduation was held on Sunday; graduated seniors went to Boston to see the
Blue Man Group and took a cruise around Boston; Student Government and Student Council
elections were held; yesterday was the first day of finals; the official last day of school is
Monday.

B. FHS Writing Award Recipients — Rich Robinson reported Freeport High School alignes itself
with two writing contests in Maine. The first contest is the Merriconeag Poetry Festival, in our
three years of participating Freeport has had more participants picked as finalists than any other
high school, except Scarborough. The second contest is the Governor's Young Writer of the
Year Award. This year Bennett Wade and Anna Titova placed in the top six. We are very proud
of our young writers.

C. Recognition of Retirees — Retiring staff members were recognized for their many years of
service to the students of RSU No. 5, they are: Walter Libby, Deborah Wescott, Sandra
Lemmerman, Laura Arsenault, Karin Gamache, Anita Wakely, Mark Wakely, Donna Wilhelm,
Clayton Carkin, Joan Nason and Cheryl White.

D. Health Initiatives Grant Funding — Anne-Marie Davee, School Health Coordinator, reported
this year there were 25 initiatives related to health, nutrition, physical activity and leadership.
Total grant funding received was $23,975 to help support the initiatives. There are 16 members
on the district Health Leadership Team.

7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Professional Development — Sarah Simmonds reported on plans for professional development for
the summer that include: a team of 18 will be attending a summer literacy institute; two Every
Day Math sessions, one for new users, one for teachers currently using the program to focus on
differentiation; NWEA assessment work.

B. Morse Street School Building Update — Cheryl White reported on the following: Laugh and
Learn preschool held their graduation tonight; 83 children were screened during Kindergarten
screening in May; two teachers worked on assessment kits this year for each item in each unit so
all teachers had shared terms and benchmarks; six teachers took the Technology for Education
course and have updated their blogs and Wiki.

C. Mast Landing School Building Update — Beth Willhoite reported on the following: Over 50% of
the students participated in the Memorial Day parade; 65% of the students participate in band;
55% of the 4" and 5" grade students are in band and chorus; 60% of students participated in the
Maine Student Book Awards and read over 1,000 books; partnered with Wolf Neck Farm and the
historical society; the 5" grade graduation ceremony will be held Monday.

D. Pownal Elementary School Building Update — Diane Mosher reported on the following: the
students have planted a salsa garden with the assistance of Anne-Marie Davee that will be
harvested in the fall; a partnership with Tommy Hilfiger to help with fundraising, this is the first
ever community partnership; last gn grade celebration for the school will be held Friday;
Monday is the last student day and as the children leave on the busses they staff will give them
the customary wave.

E. Durham Elementary School Building Update — Will Pidden reported on the following: students
read 25% more books this year, this information is tracked each year; feedback on the new
science units this year has been good; staff development this year focused on cognative coaching
with teachers observing each other; staff and students stayed focused on teaching and learning
this year even though a new building was being build right outside the windows; and August 24"
is the dedication of the new building.

F. Freeport Middle School Building Update — Ray Grogan reported on the following: one of the
building goals this year was interdisciplinary units, staff were energized to go in this direction;
conducted a pre and post survey on bullying, FMS is headed in the right direction; staff worked
with the literacy strategist this year; 6" graders attended Space Camp in Alabama; the ropes
course has updated equipment thanks to an Aspirations Grant and teachers have been recertified;
8" grade promotion is Friday evening.

G. Freeport High School Building Update — Bob Strong reported on the following: past six weeks
have been busy with senior activities; over 80% of the seniors participated in senior projects;
graduation was Sunday; schedules for next year are completed and distributed to students; over
33 students used Plato this year; Fishing Club is taking Pownal Elementary students on an outing
on Friday; professional learning communities have been established this year; FHS is losing a lot
of experienced teachers due to retirements.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Budget FY 11 — Superintendent Welsh reported there was a strong turnout at the polls on
Tuesday and thanked the Clerks for all their work. The Budget passed with 2, 345 voting in
favor and 1,607 voting no. The Budget Validation Referendum process will continue for the next
three years with 2,508 voting yes and 1,382 voting no.

Superintendent Welsh reported the budget includes an additional $245,400. The administrative
team met to recommend allocation of those funds to most effectively meet the community's
goals of reducing class size and providing academic support and enrichment for our students
across the RSU. Board members discussed the list of expenditures presented by administration
and the best use of the funds. Administrators spoke to the needs at their buildings.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications — Betsy Peters reported the committee met on May 20" and June 3™ with much
of that time spent on design of a template for building level websites. Work on these templates
will continue over the summer with new sites up in the fall. The committee also worked on
getting out the vote on the budget.

B. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on May 12" and June 9". Discussions took
place on the FY 11 Budget and the 2008-2009 Audit/Management letter review which indicated
all accounts are in good standing. The committee also discussed auditor selection. Proposals
were reviewed and Berry, Talbot, and Royer were selected. Insurance bids were also reviewed.

C. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported we are in mediation on one support staff issue. Teacher
negotiations are ongoing with the next session scheduled for tomorrow evening.

D. Policy — Cori Holt reported the committee has completed three year's worth of work in one year.
No feedback was received on packet 10-7 and the packet was finalized for adoption tonight.
There was also no feedback received on packet 10-8 and it was finalized for 1" read tonight. The
committee included the Superintendent's evaluation instrument in this packet. It does not contain
a merit pay component at this time.
