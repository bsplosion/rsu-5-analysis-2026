6. WORKSHOP: PRESENTATION OF RSUS5 FY'15 BUDGET:
A. Superintendent Welsh presented the recommended 2014-2015 RSU5 budget to the Board and
public. The purpose of this initial budget presentation is to educate the Board and public about what
is contained in the recommended budget. The budget has been developed to help RSUS realize the
goal of educational excellence while demonstrating a greater efficiency in the use of our resources.
Board budget priorities for FY' 15 include the following: instructional programs/student supports;
professional development time and training; technology investment; and capital investments.
Superintendent Welsh noted educational choices for students have changed in the past two years due
to charter schools and Superintendent agreements, which are now more of a school choice option.
Kelly Wentworth, Director of Finance reviewed the budget specifics and tax impact to each town.
There has been a change in the way state valuation is calculated. A blended average is now being
used. The recommended budget reflects a net increase of 5.17% ($1,337,243) with the following
anticipated impact to each community:
Durham — an increase of — $315,016 or a mil rate increase of $0.95 (7.15%)
Freeport — an increase of — $715,533 or a mil rate increase of $0.52 (3.35%)
Pownal — an increase of — $188,939 or a mil rate increase of $2.04 (6.06%)
Continued budget deliberations will occur over the next two months ending May 28, 2014 with the
three-town Annual Budget Meeting followed by a Budget Validation Referendum on June 10, 2014.
