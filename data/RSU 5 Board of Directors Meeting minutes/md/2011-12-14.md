5. ADJUSTMENTS TO THE AGENDA:
6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government — Charlie Baker, Student Council President shared with the
Board with his experiences at Freeport High School. Charlie talked about the need and
importance of AP courses; suggested an AP History course; compassion of the teachers during
his difficult junior year; laptops have made learning easier for the most part; and suggested aid
be given to freshmen on the college process, not wait until junior year.

7. REPORTS FROM Superintendent AND ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Current and Projected Enrollments: Superintendent Welsh provided an overview of the current
and projected student enrollments. We have an increase of over 51 students district wide from
what was anticipated from last year due to families moving into town and Durham choice
students choosing the RSU high school. This is due to adding AP courses and laptops to our
budget last year and attracting and retaining good quality staff. Enrollments for next year will
remain relatively stable with an increase of 9 students district wide. Superintendent Welsh also
reported the current 8" grade Durham students are the last class to have choice of high schools.
Tom Ambrose — Principal of Morse Street School reported enrollments for next year will remain
stable. The addition of guidance services this year has had a positive effect. Another positive of
the RSU is the sharing of specialists across the district.

Will Pidden — Principal of Durham Community School reported enrollments for next year will
remain stable. The upcoming 3“ grade will be a large class and may need additional staff.
Pownal's 5" graders will also need to make a choice of middle schools and that could impact
Durham Community School, Freeport Middle School, or both. A meeting will be held with
Pownal parents in January with visits to each school.

Lisa Demick — Principal of Pownal Elementary School reported enrollments for next year will
remain stable. This was the first year as a PreK-5 school and things are going well. One of the
benefits of the RSU is the different Kindergarten programs. Pownal has a half day PreK, half day
K program and the other schools have a full Kindergarten program. This creates a choice for
parents.
Beth Willhoite — Principal of Mast Landing School reported there will be a loss of about 6
students next year. A large 5" grade class will be moving on to the middle school. Next year's
math classes look good with 4 classes of 20 but the upcoming 3" and 4" grades could be a
challenge.
Ray Grogan — Principal of Freeport Middle School reported enrollments for next year will
remain stable but there will be a bubble in the 6" grade with 95 students coming in from Mast
Landing School and possibly 18 from Pownal. Pownal students/parents will be making their
choice of middle schools by the end of January so we will have a better understanding of
enrollment numbers.
Bob Strong — Principal of Freeport High School reported enrollments for next year will remain
stable. Some classes will be big and some will be small depending on the class. There are
currently 57 high school students attending Region Ten Technical High School, the largest
number ever. Plato has 50 students participating and Virtual High School currently has 11
students taking classes.

8. PUBLIC COMMENTS: Geoff Dyhrberg, FHS teacher, extended an invitation to Board members to
observe his classroom, shadow a teacher or student to see first hand what is going on at the high
school.

Hank Ogilby, FHS teacher expressed his concern that the climate at FHS and district wide has an
effect on student learning and suggests surveying staff to gather information.

Nancy Drolet, FHS teacher and CEA president spoke of her concern with the culture in Durham,
Freeport and Pownal. Morale is low and suggested talking to the teachers in the buildings.

John Egan, Freeport resident spoke in support of finalizing the teacher contract.

Kristen Dorsey, Freeport resident encouraged the Board to seek teacher and parent input. Also
concerned the teaching of students is being data driven.

Arnie MacDonald, Freeport resident echoed the comments of others, negotiations have taken a toll
on everyone and there needs to be an element of respect.

Diane Whitmore, FHS teacher is concerned about the message graduates are receiving due to the
teacher contract still not being finalized.
