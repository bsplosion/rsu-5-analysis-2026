5. ADJUSTMENTS TO THE AGENDA:
6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government — Reiley Parker reported on the following: American Studies
students working on self designed service projects connected to Ben Franklin's interests and
virtues; Mock Trial class is preparing for competition; National Honor Society launching annual
food drive; seniors working with Guidance Counselors on the common application; pumpkin
carving to benefit Camp Sunshine; 32 students serving as mentors at Morse Street School;
presenters from Shalom House met with students in health class; the musical South Pacific is
scheduled November 8-11; 23 students auditioned for District III Honors Band and Chorus
festival on October 22™.

B. Freeport Middle School — Update — Phyllis Latham, Guidance Counselor and 8" grade Peer
Mentors Mackey Lipsack, Lauren Moore and Abby Arruda spoke about the FMS core values and
“Got Character” program happening at Freeport Middle School. The core values include:
respect, responsibility, compassion, courage and honesty. The Peer Leaders at the school work
with other students on stopping bullying and working with students to just being themselves.
The 6" graders are working on an Invasive Species unit; the 7" graders are working on the
Acadia unit and the 8" graders are working on a standards based computer math class.

C. KinderKonzert ~ Rachel Domin, K-2 Music teacher reported on the Portland Symphony
Orchestra's KinderKonzert that performed for all the K-2 students in the RSU at the FPAC. The
concert “Fire in the Big Top” is tied into literature. Leading up to the performance students
learned about the musical instruments and songs that were in the performance.

14. COMMUNICATIONS (Consent Agenda):

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — John Morang reported the committee met on October 10". The committee discussed
how the district music team would be distributing the $9,100 appropriated in contingency in the
2012-2013 budget cycle and the Janet Newlin donation fund money. Joseph Cuetara from
Moors & Cabot will be attending the next Finance Meeting to talk about the bonding process,
various financing options and current rates. Dennis Ouellette attended the meeting and
reviewed the updated Capital Plan he would be presenting to the Board that evening. Seth
Thompson also attended the meeting and presented his updated draft of the technology plan he
will be presenting to the Board on November 14, 2012.

B. FHS Facilities Study Advisory Committee — Peter Murray reported meetings with the architect
are ongoing. The committee met yesterday on building and site needs. All meetings are open to
the public and the next meeting is scheduled for November 8" at 4:30pm. High School students
are creating a survey on space, technology, cafeteria space, general building space. Students
will report out on results at the November 8" meeting.

C. Strategic Communications — Jane Blais reported Joe Makley attended the meeting and provided
a status report on the Work and College readiness pilot and work of the social studies teachers.
Options for this year's Community Forum were discussed. It was decided to postpone a
decision whether to hold the public forum since attendance by members of the public has been
limited. Alternative methods of dialogue and obtaining feedback from the public may prove
more effective than a one evening event. A final decision will be made in January.
