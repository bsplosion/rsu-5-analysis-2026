5. Superintendent'S REPORT:
A. Proposed 2009-2010 School Calendar and process. Superintendent Welsh presented the
proposed 2009-2010 school calendar. By law, the Board is required to adopt a school calendar
annually by the end of May. Elaine Tomaszewski explained in crafting the calendar we were
cognizant of existing practices in each district such as early release, late start, professional
development and professional compensation time. The proposed start date for 2009-2010 is
scheduled before Labor Day. The proposed calendar is posted on the RSUS5 website and feedback
can be sent to calendarfeedback@rsu5.org.

7. OLD BUSINESS:
A. Review Budget FY'10
Jim Rier, Policy and Finance Specialist with the Department of Education attended the meeting
representing the Commissioner of Education. Mr. Rier encouraged the Board to keep moving
forward with the work that needs to be done - to have a legally functioning system in place on
July 1°. It was explained there are no provisions in the law to disband the RSU. The legislature
can't make changes to the consolidation law due to the November referendum. The penalties to
districts that did not consolidate have been removed from their subsidy. If the law is repealed in
November there will not be penalty money available for the districts that followed the law and
consolidated. At this time, no decision has been made on the timing or distribution of the
penalty funds. Mr. Rier explained the annual budget meeting and Budget Validation
Referendum voting process.
Rep. David Webster explained due to the referendum in November the legislature's hands are
tied. Rep. Webster had a conversation with Commissioner Gendron regarding the RSU's cost
sharing formula. The Commissioner explained to Rep. Webster if the three towns are willing to
petition her to reopen the cost sharing formula she has the authority to do so. Rep. Webster is
working with the Commissioner and Governor's office to help find money to assist the RSU with
this difficult situation.
Due to the late hour the Board was not able to review the budget Article by Article as planned.
This will take place at the May 13" meeting. Board members spoke about areas in the budget
they might be comfortable reducing. A conversation regarding the laptops also needs to take
place. Board members provided direction to Administration regarding information needed for the
May 13" meeting and next steps. These include requesting additional aid from the State, further
investigation into both the laptop and 21‘ Century Skills initiatives and further budget
reductions.
