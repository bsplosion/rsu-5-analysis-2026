11. UNFINISHED BUSINESS:
A. Budget FY'14 — Chair Larkins reported the Freeport Town Council was scheduled to be in
attendance tonight, but because the budget was presented last night there was no time for review of
the budget. We will move them to the May 1“ meeting.
Superintendent Welsh reported the formal presentation of the budget took place last evening. We
went through an overview of the budget then article by article including the anticipated impact to
taxpayers. The overall themes are that student enrollment has increased by 3.7%, wages are at 3%
on average, insurance 4% and increased program demands in the area of Special Education. After
looking at the potential tax impact, reductions were made. Other drivers in the budget are changes
in the Governor's proposed budget to push retirement costs to the local taxpayers so it is based on
property tax instead of income tax an addition of $188,000. The other driver is choice, the tuition
we would need to pay to Charter schools is estimated at $110,000.
Superintendent Welsh reported we will be able to provide a quality program with this budget, but
we will not be able to move forward at the level we would like for next year. We have quality
people and a good foundation to work with.

A. Finance ~ John Morang reported the committee met on March 13, 2013 and listened to the top

two candidates for Financial Advisory Services. Richard Ranaghan, Jr. from Gorham Savings
Bank and Joseph Cuetara from Moors & Cabot. Both candidates have years of experience in
the Maine bonding market and with education finance. The Finance committee recommended
Joseph Cuetara from Moors & Cabot and he was appointed by the Board of Directors tonight.
The Finance Director and Superintendent reviewed the proposed budget impact numbers along
with a spreadsheet of the requests of the Administrative team for the upcoming budget year. In
addition to the 3% increase in enrollments, negotiated wage and benefits increases, and the
increased special education needs of the district, the Governor is proposing to push half of the
cost of retirement onto the local school districts. The budget was presented to the public last
evening.

B. Strategic Communications — Jane Blais reported the committee met on March 12, 2013. The
committee reviewed the final drafts of the data gathered to assess the RSU5 Strategic Plan,
including NECAP and MHSA scores, financial data, college attendance, etc. The new report
will include summaries of the action taken in response to the implications presented by the data.
The 2013 State of the Schools report will be mailed to all households during the last week in
March.

The committee discussed the format for the “Dine and Discuss” sessions which will be held in
each town to discuss the State of the Schools report, the 2013-2014 budget and the FHS
renovation project.

C. Facilities Committee Update- Peter Murray reported the committee is working on ways to
communicate with citizens in the RSU. A postcard was mailed to all households in the RSU
notifying citizens of the April 3" Informational Session at 6:30pm in the FHS café. Visit the
renovatefhs.org website for information on the project and to submit questions and comments
about the project.
