5. COMMUNICATIONS:
Brenda Kielty reported on her visit to a high school classroom and how beneficial it is to have time
with teachers in their environment. It is an opportunity to see what it is like in the classroom, what
the teacher's priorities are and bring that information back to the Board. Brenda encouraged other
Board members to visit the classrooms.
Peter Murray and Nelson Larkins shared their experience when they participated in a potluck and
book study with students from an American Studies class. Eleven students, teachers Mr. Dyhrberg
and Mr. Mellon participated in the discussion. Peter and Nelson both agreed it was good to get to
interact with students in an intellectual way and found it very valuable.
6. WORKSHOP:
Interim Report of the Freeport High School Facilities Committee: Lyndon Keck, Senior Principal
Architect with Portland Design Team reported on where they are with the process and what they
know so far. They are half-way done with the study and there are currently no cost estimates, no
floor plans and the strategic master plan is not finished. A meeting was held with the High Schoo!
Leadership team in December and all employees at the high school were surveyed. Four themes
emerged, they were: need for a more robust new IT network; improved classroom technology;
greater mix of classroom sizes and congestion in hallway to lower level of the school. The
intermediate progress report included a campus site plan analysis. Changes would need to be made
to the proposed athletic complex plan including moving the proposed track and softball field.
Moving the alternative program (portable building) into the high school was suggested. Bus loading
and unloading is unsafe and drop off and pick up by parents is congested and backs up Holbrook
Street. The intermediate report also includes a space allocation program that outlines appropriate
square footage needs per students. There is roughly a shortage of five classrooms at the high school.
Building analysis and infrastructure systems were also part of the report which included congestion
in narrow corridor serving cafeteria, gym and FPAC, the lower level rooms feel isolated, weight
room is undersized, all toilet rooms need renovation.
