7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS: OPENING OF SCHOOL

A. Curriculum Director Joe Makley spoke of the work of the curriculum office including such items
as: Teachers College Writing Workshop; cross-curricular standards for work and college
readiness; scientific practices being used at the middle level; Wellness and PE standards being
refined; and the summer institute.

B. Athletic Director Craig Sickels spoke about the fall sports season including participation
numbers and post season success. All fall teams qualified for post-season play. Sports awards
are coming up next week.

C. Director of Instructional Support Lisa Garneau reported on enrollments and new programs
throughout the district including Choices, Bridges and Functional Life Skills. Special Education
enrollment is at 303 students (16%) and ESL has 27 students.

D. Director of Adult, Recreation and Community Education Mike Myers reviewed recreation
programs for both students and adults that are run through his department. The Laugh & Learn
program has a total of 157 students. Currently 7 GED students are enrolled. The Teen Center has
between 10-20 students a day.

E. School Nutrition Director Judy Goodnow reported participation is picking up. We are at 23% for
free/reduced. A full Federal review will take place in April. New regulations for chemicals are
due in December. Durham and Pownal received the Certificate of Excellence Healthy US
Challenge last year.

F. FHS Visioning Process - Principal Bob Strong reported the high school has made significant
progress, changes and adjustments since the consolidation. Have added numerous programs and
increased staffing. These changes have been primarily through administration, faculty and
leadership teams. The goal is to start the formal visioning process this winter after the renovation
referendum is passed. Looking to bring together all stakeholders and create focus groups and
creating surveys to look at what we need for our students and what we want for a high school
going forward. As a result of recent Board and community discussions, this process will now
also include consideration of the name of the high school.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Freeport High School Renovation: Superintendent Welsh reported information on the project
can be found at rsu5.org along with the video of the October 16, 2013 public hearing. Another
informational session is scheduled for October 28, 2013 in Durham. Voting will take place on

B. Board Goals Summary: Superintendent Welsh reviewed the two month process to get to this
point beginning in August with the Board self-evaluation and then meeting with governance
officials on October 9, 2013 to hear their comments and input. Goals focus on communication,
FHS facility, financial and negotiations.

VOTE TO AUTHORIZE THE RICHARD P. ALLEN SCHOLARSHIP FUND
Be it hereby VOTED, as follows:

1. That the Board of Directors of Regional School Unit No. 5 (the “Board”) accept the
Richard P. Allen Scholarship Fund (the “Scholarship”), which has been established to
provide scholarships for Freeport High School graduating men and women whose four
high school years demonstrate a consistent pattern of scholastic achievement,
extracurricular determination, and school/community citizenship.

2. That the Board accept the Scholarship pursuant to Title 20-A M.R.S.A. § 1256, which
provides that the Board may accept and receive money, outright or in trust, for any
specified benevolent or educational purpose.

3. That the Superintendent of Schools of Regional School Unit No. 5
(the “Superintendent”) be and hereby is authorized in the name and on behalf of the
Board to execute the Scholarship Fund Agreement in form acceptable to the
Superintendent of Schools and the District's legal counsel.

4. That the Superintendent be and hereby is further authorized in the name and on behalf of
the Board to notify the Scholarship donors of the Board's vote.

Motion Seconded: I John Morang second the motion to approve the Vote to Authorize
the Richard P. Allen Scholarship Fund.

Vote: Adopted by affirmative vote of 10 _ Board members.

A true copy, attest:

Shannon L. Welsh, Ed.D., Secretary
