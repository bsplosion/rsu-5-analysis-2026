A. Report from Student Government — Reiley Parker reported on the following: On April 27" the
former Ambassador of Haiti spoke to the students about the conditions in Haiti; juniors took the

B. Donation of Paddle — Evan Kumagae and Lia Wellen spoke of the banner year the Teen Center
had with the Sitting Pretty auction. L.L. Bean donated 100 canoe paddles that were painted then
auctioned off. The auction raised $16,500. A paddle was donated to the teachers of Freeport
Middle School for all the work they do with teens.

7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. FHS Facilities Study Advisory Committee Report — Lyndon Keck, PDT Architects provided an
interim report on Master Plan work completed since his last update in April. The three
categories in the plan are repair and renovate, overcrowding and future enrollments. The two
components are the site and the building. Planning work includes drawings that show
incremental additions to the existing building. Information was shared that included different
alternatives for student enrollments with costs ranging from $4M for a 590-student enrollment to
$14M for 716 students. Next steps will be for Mr. Keck to finish the draft report and present it to
the Advisory Committee at their meeting next week for discussion. The final report will be
presented to the Board on June 13".

B. Eligibility Expectations — Craig Sickels, Athletic Director reported a committee of 11
community members has been reviewing Freeport High School's Co-Curricular Eligibility

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:
A. Professional Growth and Evaluation System Advisory Committee Final Report. Jessica Sturges,
a member of the committee, reported on the work that has been done to date on a district
evaluation system. The committee has come to consensus on a short-term and longer-term
implementation plan that will ensure the RSU meets the criteria established by the Board and the
new law enacted this year by the Legislature. The new system is based on the National Board
Certified Teacher (NBCT) Standards and Five Core Propositions. These were selected because
they met the committee's criteria of simple, understandable, tested, and familiar. Presentations
to teachers were held during faculty meetings and feedback received. The system will be
transitioned in over the next three years starting with the 2012-2013 school year. Full
implementation is scheduled for the 2015-2016 school year.
