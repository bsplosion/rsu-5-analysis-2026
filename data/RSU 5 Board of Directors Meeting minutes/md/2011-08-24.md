7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS: FIRST DAYS OF SCHOOLS

A. Tom Ambrose reported on the following: student enrollment is at 265; school's main office has
been renovated; a staff gathering was held this afternoon; leadership teams met today and will
meet again tomorrow; professional development took place over the summer on the Readers
Workshop and EveryDay Math.

B. Beth Willhoite reported on the following: student enrollment is at 263; all open positions have
been filled; the school has new yellow walls; a new Mac computer lab; the leadership team will
meet next week to plan professional development for the year with a focus on Readers
Workshop, EveryDay Math and technology.

C. Lisa Demick reported on the following: student enrollment is at 100; five staff members attended
a conference in Chicago focused on the technology piece of EveryDay Math; 35 staff members
attended a professional development day last week on EveryDay Math; classrooms have been
moved around now that Pownal is a PreK-5 school; all open positions have been hired; staff will
be concentrating on Readers Workshop blocks this year.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Celebration of First Days of School — Superintendent Welsh reported on the many professional
development activities that took place over the summer that will give teachers an opportunity to
support each other throughout the year. New Teacher Induction sessions started today with 26
new staff members. August 31‘ is the first staff day and will focus on technology. September 1“
is the formal welcome back gathering for all staff.

B. Professional Growth System — Superintendent Welsh reported on the need for one equitable
professional growth system for all teachers. Teachers are currently working under three separate
plans. Tonight the Board will vote on a Professional Growth and Evaluation System Advisory
Committee Charge as a first step toward this import work. The first meeting will be held on
September 27" at 4:15 p.m.

C. Work Plan for 2011-2012 — Superintendent Welsh reported on the draft work plan for the 201 1-
2012 school year. The plan includes the following areas: Strategic Plan Building Level Goal
Setting and Report Out; Teacher Professional Growth System; Communication Promoting the
Educational Benefits of the RSU; Budget FY13 Development and Adoption; Teacher
Negotiations; Instructional Support/Response to Intervention.

14. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on June 29, 2011. The committee voted to
transfer $25, 000 from Article 6 to Article 2 due to health insurance changes and payroll days
from the previous year that had not been accrued causing Article 2 to be over. The committee
also met on August 10, 2011. The Director of Finance reviewed financial summary sheet
handed out to the Committee. The current estimate of unexpended funds remaining from the
2010/2011 school year is approximately $800,000. Kelly also reviewed the Summary of
Undesignated Fund Balance Handout that had been previously presented to the committee
earlier in the year. This summary identified a target fund balance that was necessary to retain
the level of current revenues for next year budget cycle. The $800,000 is close to the target
range necessary to do this. The Auditors will be in the third week of September to begin the
audit process and a final review will be presented to the committee when completed. Warrants
were signed at both meetings.

B. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported negotiations with Administrators has been completed.
The support staff three year combined contract was TA'd this morning with a vote scheduled
this evening by the Board and by the CEA membership on September 1°. The Board will also
vote tonight on a one year extension of the 2009-2010 teachers contracts. The CEA
membership will vote on September 1".
