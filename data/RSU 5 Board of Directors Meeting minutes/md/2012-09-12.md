6. WORKSHOP:
A. Freeport High School Master Plan. Lyndon Keck, PDT Architects reviewed the Freeport High
School Master Plan Facilities Study he presented in June. Mr. Keck reported the High School is
a well maintained facility, but there are areas in need of renovation and areas of the building that
do not meet ADA compliance, security requirements, and safety needs. There are space needs
for programs such as art, music, science and STEM. Mr. Keck reported the enrollment study
completed by NESDEC shows 600 students in five years and 680 in ten years.

Principal Strong reported on the issues he sees at the school such as: moving of sets to
accommodate classes on the FPAC stage; no ability to add science classes due to limited lab
space; Social Worker housed in a passageway; location of guidance department; inadequate
storage; not enough space for STEM classes; one conference room; Alternative Education and
JMG housed in a portable; and front entrance congested with parents, busses, deliveries.
Kim LaMarre, representing the Facilities Study Advisory Committee, reported the
recommendations of the committee are to stay in the present location, move forward with the
concept design phase, and use the ten-year enrollment projections. Members met with a variety
of community groups and governance boards to gather feedback on the renovation project
including: the neighborhood group; FEDC; Freeport Chamber; governance committees from
Durham, Freeport and Pownal; Freeport High School staff; and parents at school open houses.
Feedback has been positive.
A roundtable discussion with the Board and members of the Facilities Study Advisory
Committee was held. The next phase in the process is the completion of Concept Design, which
will provide more detail and financial information about the project. The Board anticipates a
vote on September 26, 2012 on the expenditure of $40,000 to complete this work.

B. Goal Setting Linked to Board Self-Evaluation: Candace Decsipkes reported out on the Outreach
Group meeting. The purpose of the group was to consider what could be done as a Board and
organization to reach out to the three towns. Suggestions were made for activities ongoing
throughout the year such as: mingling middle school students within the RSU and orientation
activities for freshmen; rotating Board meetings between the three towns; meet with governance
boards twice a year and on important topics; and invitational roundtable discussions.
Suggestions were also made specific to the budget development process such as: publish budget
process in advance; continue meetings with parent groups; summarize budget development
process at the end of Board meetings and send press release; invite budget advisory and
governance body members to budget meetings; and move Annual Budget Meeting to a Saturday.
The Board reviewed the results of the self-evaluation. The overall rating of 82% reflects an
effective Board who just completed a successful year. The seven dimensions on the evaluation
were contextual 83%, educational 87%, finance 82%, interpersonal 84%, analytical 77%,
political 79% and strategic 83%. The Board discussed adding components to the goals for the
2012-2013 school year including: connecting with teachers more to the communication goal;
adding a facilities goal; and an expanded financial goal to do more outreach to the three
communities around the budget process.
