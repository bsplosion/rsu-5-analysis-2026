A. Recognition of Retirees — Retiring staff members were recognized for their many years of

B. Freeport Middle School Musical — Principal Ray Grogan explained the middle school musical is

directed the musical, Disney's The Little Mermaid, Jr. Cast members expressed their
appreciation to the high school students involved with the musical.

Nationals and attended the competition at Michigan State University. Students expressed it was a
wonderful experience and bonded with teams from other countries. The team thanked their two
parent volunteers Beth Parker and Brett Pierce.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:
A. Budget FY14 — Superintendent Welsh reported the school budget for 2013-2014 has passed by a
vote of 2,414 in favor and 1,716 against. The additional money added to the budget will go for the
half-time Assistant Principal position at Freeport Middle School. Superintendent Welsh thanked the
voters for their support.
B. FHS Renovation — Superintendent Welsh reported the Freeport High School renovations vote did
not pass with a vote of 2,028 in favor and 2,202 against. The Facilities Committee did an exceptional
job getting information out; they did everything they could.
The Board members discussed what the next steps should be in regards to the renovations.
VOTED: (2) To authorize the Superintendent, Chair and Vice Chair to work with the Architect,
pollsters, the Facilities Advisory Committee and other advisors to put together a poll, at a cap of
$5,000, and report out at the first September meeting with options. (Murray ~ Parker) (8 ~ 0)
C. District Overview — Superintendent Welsh reported out on a document that has been created to
recognizes many of the accomplishments achieved by students and staff within the RSU. The
document will be posted on the RSUS website.
9. PUBLIC COMMENTS:
Laurie Poissonnier, Durham resident asked for people not to take Durham's no vote as a vote against
education, it was the tax impact. A building doesn't make a good education, the staff does.
Susan Karnes, Durham resident asked the Board to move forward with modifications to the
renovation plan.
Kevin Nadeau, Durham resident stated it is tempting to lash out at a town over the rejection of the
FHS renovations vote. Kevin also mentioned budget documents should have a note of explanation;
people just see the numbers and think it is unfair.
Joe Migliaccio, Freeport resident doesn't think the Board should split up the project into two
questions. Also, people need to know what a no vote means.
John Egan, Freeport resident spoke of his frustration with the renovation vote and asked that the
Board keep moving forward with renovations to the High School, don't give up.
Kristen Dorsey, Freeport resident doesn't feel the project should be split into two questions.
Citizens didn't attend meetings and were not informed.
Emily Carter, Freeport resident would like to hear the reasons why people voted no. Doing a poll is
a waste of money.
Jen Maneikis, Freeport resident stated she believe the renovation project will pass if the fields are
split out. People are also talking about pulling out of the RSU.
Lindsay Sterling, Freeport resident stated she supports the Board conducting a poll and that they
should go back out in November with fields separated out. We need to pull together; all three towns
care about their kids.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):
A. Finance — Kate Brown reported the committee met on May 8"". The committee reviewed the
revised budget impact sheet that reflected the additions made at the May 1“ Board meeting.
The DOE made revisions to the ED279 that resulted in a decrease in our state subsidy by
$154,601 from the initial estimate and reported the expected full value impact of State
Retirement to be $268,750.67. The committee also reviewed the 2013/2014 warrant and
meeting schedules.
B. Policy — Superintendent Welsh reported the committee met to consider changes from Maine
School Management Association (MSMA) to Policy JIH Questioning and Searches of Students.
The Freeport Police Department may obtain a police dog and committee members discussed the
possible use of a police dog in bomb or drug searches, for example. JIH will be added to the
first policy packet in September.

C. Strategic Communications — Candace Decsipkes reported the committee met on May 30". The
committee discussed the three Dine and Discuss sessions held in each of the towns. It was
decided this format provided more opportunity for discussion than the previous format of a
single large event, so it should continue next year. Also, more opportunities should be sought to
engage the public on school issues.
The committee reviewed changes to the Strategic Framework. Joe Makley, Curriculum
Director provided status reports on two pilot projects: Work and College Readiness and Writing
Prompt at grades 7 and 8.
