6. WORKSHOP: FY12 BUDGET REVIEW:
Superintendent Welsh reported there will be a budget increase of $159,664 in Article 4 due to
Census 2010 figures recently released for Region 10. The overall Region 10 budget has a decrease
but the RSU has an increased assessment based on the Census figures for all three towns. There will
be a budget decrease of $38,126 in Article 6 because the RSU will no longer pay tuition to Region
10 for Durham students attending Brunswick High School and Maine Region 10 because they are
now included in the RSU assessment. The anticipated first bond payment of $56,106 for the fields
project will be put into contingency. The net anticipated impact of all three items to taxpayers would
be as follows:
Durham: +$15,918 = 1%
Freeport: +$99,597 = 1%
Pownal: — ($11,349) =— 1%
The Superintendent also reported on the Board's request for a list of administrative priorities. The
administrative team met and confirmed that the schools can be run with the recommended budget
and the following items will be considered in future budgets:
¢ Literacy/Instructional Support - coaching positions to support new instructional practices
¢ Professional Development Flex Time — additional time for training and professional work
¢ Student Technology PK-6
¢ Enrichment/Gifted and Talented support (positions)
¢ Intervention Supports and Students (instructional, behavioral, and programmatic)
* Continued Support for Appropriate Class Size
¢ Beautification/Improvement of our Schools and Grounds

Public Comments:
Freeport: Stephanie Paine mentioned the wonderful RSU 5/6 Grade concert last evening. She
believes the elementary schools need more music and middle school students should have more
general music education.
Kristen Dorsey supports adding additional music at the elementary level and asked about the
budget impact if FMS taught music throughout the year so it is more of the core curriculum.
David Watts talked about his concerns with the music programs throughout the RSU and the lack
of support in the budget.
Catherine Breer spoke about the short term goals a group of parents has come up with to work on
cosmetic and safety improvements to the High School. The group is asking for $9,500 to begin
the work.
