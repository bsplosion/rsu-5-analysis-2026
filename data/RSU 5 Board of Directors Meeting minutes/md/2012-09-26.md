8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Celebration of First Days of School — Superintendent Welsh reported on the many professional
development activities that took place over the summer that will give teachers an opportunity to
support each other throughout the year. There is a 3% increase in enrollment from what was
projected. There were approximately 40 openings this year with a large candidate pool. A strong
group of teachers have joined us. The challenge is new teacher salaries were budgeted at M6 and
most new hires are at a salary step of M8 or 9 and some higher, so the budget is tight. With the
increase of enrollments at Mast Landing the Finance Committee approved using contingency
money to hire an additional teacher.

B. Work Plan for 2012-2013 — Superintendent Welsh reported on the draft work plan for the 2012-
2013 school year. The plan includes the following areas: Strategic Plan Building Level Goal
Setting and Report Out; State of the Schools Report; Professional Growth and Evaluation
System; Communication Promoting the Educational Benefits of the RSU; Budget FY14
Development and Adoption; FHS Facility; and Response to Intervention.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):
A. Finance — John Morang reported the committee met on June 13, 2012 and discussed the
following: 2011-2012 Educator Contract retro pay transfers; review of Computation and
Declaration of Votes for the Budget Validation Referendum; reviewed a draft of the 2012-2013
warrant and meeting schedule; Eric Dube was thanked for his service to the Finance Committee;
and warrants were signed. The committee met on August 8, 2012 and discussed the following:
election of Chair for the committee; an outline that listed upcoming months and potential topics
for the committee to discuss; review of current/anticipated enrollments at Morse Street School
and Mast Landing School; Bookkeeper vacancy in the Business Office; and warrants were
signed. The committee met again on September 12, 2012. John Morang was voted in as Chair
of the committee. The committee discussed meetings with member communities and they will
be invited to the October 10" workshop on Capital Plan and Efficiencies. Contingency transfers
were made which included $73,226 between Article 6 and Article 11 to cover Special Education
expenditures as a result of a new child that moved into the district requiring an Out of District
Placement. The second transfer was for $500,391 between Article 6 and salary lines in Articles
1, 6, 7, and 11 to cover the movement of funds to cover teacher wages as a result of the
ratification of the 2011-2014 CEA Educators Collective Bargaining Agreement. Warrants were
signed.
