6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Council —- Emily Sturtevant reported on the following: mid-term exams are
taking place this week; guidance counselors have met with all 9" 11" grade students to review
PSAT scores; all 9" — 12" grade students have College Board accounts; 35 seniors have been
accepted to colleges; World Quest competition begins on February 6"; Model UN begins in
February; and the Winter Carnival will take place on February 15".

B. The Port Teen Center ~ Mike Myers introduced Darryn DuShane, the new Teen Center
Coordinator. Darryn worked as Freeport High School last year as an Ed Tech and Coach. Darryn
has a background in outdoor education. Moving forward Darryn is looking at community
service projects for the teens who attend the Port.

C. Impact of Proposed State Budget — Superintendent Welsh reported on the proposed State budget
that includes: flat funding for education; cost of Maine State Retirement being pushed into local
school district budgets; new casino revenues that are suppose to help supplement education
funding to reach 55% will be used by the Governor for his initiatives. The final budget probably
won't be finalized until June.

Representative Sara Gideon also spoke about the State budget. Rep. Gideon's perspective is that
legislatures are dissatisfied with the budget proposal. Budget proposals change through the
process so this isn't the final budget. Citizens should contact their representatives with concerns.

7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Special Education Programs and Enrollments - Dominic DePatsy, Director of Instructional
Support provided an overview of the Special Education programs including: 298 students
identified as of December 1“; the make-up of regular classrooms, resource classrooms and self-
contained classroom; reviewed the programs and enrollments at each school; work being done
with Becket Family Services and Connections for Kids; and needs for the upcoming school year.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Update on Professional Growth and Evaluation System — Superintendent Welsh reported the
Board adopted an initial teacher growth and evaluation system based on the National Board of
Professional Teaching Standards. Administrators and teachers are working this year to better
understand the standards and what the expectations are, what it looks like in the classroom. The
Professional Growth and Evaluation Committee has been meeting to develop rubrics and
examples to help guide teachers on levels of effectiveness. The committee will finalize the work
by spring and bring it back to the Board for adoption.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — John Morang reported the committee met on December 12, 2012. The committee
reviewed Board policies and procedures related to school finance. There were no recommended
changes at this time. The committee also reviewed the RPC funding formula including
definitions of terms and how they tied into the tax impact sheet distributed during the budget
process. The committee met again on January 9, 2013 and discussed the areas of anticipated
reductions and savings as a result of the State Aid to Education subsidy reduction of $138,193.
The committee also discussed the FHS Facilities Master Plan. If the bond passes the impact of
the first principal and interest payment is not expected until the 2014-2015 budget year. Kelly
Wentworth reported she has begun updating the 3-5 Year Financial Plan. The draft plan will be
presented at the next committee meeting, scheduled for February 13".
