5. Superintendent'S REPORT:
A. District Organizational Structure Update: Superintendent Welsh reported on the business
transition plan being worked on to ensure the RSU is a legal entity on the official start date of July 1,
2009. Job descriptions and evaluations will be created for each position in the organization. Work
has been begun in this area with Directors and Central Office Support staff. The goal is to
recommend the Central Office job descriptions to the Board at the April 29" meeting. The job
descriptions will then be used for development of evaluations.
B. Department of Education Consolidation Planning Grant Update: Superintendent Welsh reported
on work being done through an Education Planning Grant from the Department of Education for the
purpose of creating a unified system. We are in the planning stages of initiatives. The
Communications Sub-Committee is responsible for planning a Future Search discussion involving
60-70 people in the creation of a shared vision for the RSU. The second initiative focuses on
curriculum and professional development. Elaine Tomaszewski is working to organize and
implement those efforts. Elaine reported we plan to engage the services of Heidi McGinley and
possibly one additional facilitator to assist in moving the RSU to a unified system of high
expectations for all. It is anticipated the work will be completed in three phases. The three phases
are data collection, analysis of data to identify key issues, and the third is to develop a district
curriculum team.

7. PUBLIC COMMENTS:
Scott Kaplan, Pownal resident, thanked the video staff for taping the meetings. Mr. Kaplan
requested the Board stop working to reduce the budget and instead look at the organizational
structure. Mr. Kaplan believes consolidation in this manner will not work and the law is too
restrictive. He suggested the Board look at options (AOS) to be implemented immediately.
Board member Jennifer Kaplan read an e-mail she received from Mary Ann Hodson a Pownal
resident.

8. OLD BUSINESS:

A. Review Budget FY'10
Additional budget information was provided to the Board as requested at the April 8" meeting.
Administrators met and worked together to come up with a reduction of $570,808 to the budget.
In addition to cuts to the budget we are looking at curtailment funds, penalty funds and grant
funding. Legal council is reviewing the option of changing the cost sharing formula through a
referendum. Commissioner Gendron has been asked to attend the April 29" Board meeting to
help us understand what our options are to work ourselves out of this situation. Several Board
members and citizens expressed concern about the tax impact on Pownal and also their concerns
about cuts to the budget.

9. NEW BUSINESS:

A. Voted (2) To waive the requirement of competitive bidding of insurance for the policy period
beginning July 1, 2009 due to an inability to meet certain statutory deadlines established for the
bidding process as a result of a restricted consolidation timeline, which therefore creates an
emergency as defined in 20-A M.R.S. Section 1001(14) that requires the immediate procurement
of insurance. (Morang — Dube) (10 — 0)

B. Voted (3) To authorize RSU No. 5 to enter into a trust agreement, between the Maine School
Management Association Unemployment Compensation Trust Fund and RSU No. 5 dated July
1, 2009 and to make contributions to the Trust Fund on behalf of employees. Further move that
our school unit meet its obligation to provide unemployment compensation protection on its
employees, through reimbursing method, by participating in the Maine School Management
Association Unemployment Compensation Trust Fund, and further move that the Superintendent
of Schools be authorized to create a new account with Maine School Management Association
Unemployment Compensation Trust fund. The Superintendent is further authorized to enter into
an agreement to administer our unemployment compensation program with Maine School
Management Association on behalf of RSU No. 5. (Morang — Kaplan) (10 — 0)

C. Voted (4) To authorize the purchase of Advanced Data Systems financial software and related
services for RSU No. 5. (Morang — Kaplan) (10 — 0)

11. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications: Betsy Peters reported the committee met on April 6" and 13". Good progress
is being made on the goal of transparency. We have moved to using students to video the Board
meetings with the goal of investing in a technology club for students to become more involved.
The committee is in the beginning planning stage for the Future Search scheduled for June 18",
19" and 20".

B. Finance: John Morang reported the committee met on April 9"" to consider areas for action for
the RSU. The RSU has successfully attained an Employer Identification Number. Property and
casualty, school board liability and workers compensation insurance was discussed including a
request to waive the requirement for competitive bidding. Also discussed entering into an
agreement with MSMA to become a member of their unemployment pool. A financial software
system was also discussed.

C. Policy: Cori Holt reported the committee met on April 10" to review packet 09-1, no feedback
was received. Packet 09-1 will go to the Board on April 29" for a 1“ read. The committee also
reviewed packet 09-2. Packet 09-2 will be distributed on April 29" for feedback.
