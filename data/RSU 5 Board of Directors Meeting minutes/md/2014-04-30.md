Kelly Wentworth reported the budget was presented to the Board and communities on
March 25, 2014. The complete video of that presentation and all the budget handouts are
available on the RSUS website. Kelly provided an overview of the budget handout which
included the following: budget priorities; an article by article comparison to last year with
explanations; capital and fuel reserve funds; and the budget impact to towns. Two adjustments
will need to be made to the budget document due to an increase in Region 10 and health
insurance costs. Kelly also reviewed a handout that provided information on Administrative
budget requests.
Dennis Ouellette, Director of Facilities and Maintenance reported the movement of a tractor
purchase in Capital Budget FY'15 to FY' 16 in order to do the repairs to the Tennis Courts in
FY'15.
Board members asked questions about the following items: fundraising for a portion of the
money to repair the tennis courts; NWEA testing vs. Smarter Balanced; .16 English teacher at
FHS.
Public Comments on the Budget:
Karen Primavera from Durham asked about NWEA and Smarter Balanced testing.
John Primavera from Durham has concerns about the tax increases each year.
Kevin Nadeau from Durham noted citizens should talk to their legislators about school funding.
Jeff Wakeman from Durham mentioned the school budget is going up two to three times the
rate of inflation.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — John Morang reported the Finance Committee met on April 9" and reviewed the cost
sharing section of the RSUS Reorganization Plan. At the meeting Kelly Wentworth reviewed
definitions of terms and explained how they are tied into the projected tax impact sheet that is
distributed during the budget process.

The Finance Committee approved delaying the banking, audit and insurance request for
proposals process until after the Town of Freeport vote on withdrawal.

group will meet from 6:00-6:30pm then the attorney will join us at 6:30pm
