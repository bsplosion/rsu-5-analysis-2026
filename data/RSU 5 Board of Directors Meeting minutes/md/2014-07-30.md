5. NEW BUSINESS:
A motion was made by Peter Murray, seconded by Valy Steverlynck, that the Board approve the
withdrawal agreement proposed by the Freeport Withdrawal Committee, with such other updates
and technical changes as the Superintendent and Finance Manager may approve, and that the board
chair be authorized to execute and deliver the agreement in the name and on behalf of the RSU
Amendment #1: Moved by Michelle Ritcheson, seconded by Kate Brown, that the Withdrawal
Agreement presented to this meeting by the Freeport Withdrawal Committee be approved by the
RSUS School Board with the following four (4) changes, as recommended by the RSU 5 Withdrawal
Group:
First, that the enrollment availability at Freeport High School for Pownal and Durham students
(see Exhibit 1, section 6) be changed so as to be based upon the total enrollment number of 500
less (a) the number of Freeport resident students enrolled the prior year at the applicable grade
levels, (b) the number of Freeport resident students that are expected to newly enroll, and (c) the
number of students enrolled in grade 8 at Freeport Middle School.
Second, that the two different secondary tuition rates payable by RSUS5 to Freeport (see Exhibit
1, section 8), be changed to a single tuition rate for all attending students, based upon the
midpoint between (a) the tuition rate determined by the Commissioner based on F reeport's actual
per pupil costs in the prior year under 20-A MRS §5805(1), and (b) the state average tuition rate
(referred to in the statute as the “maximum allowable rate”) determined by the Commissioner
under 20-A MRS §5805(2).
Third, that the termination provision of the secondary tuition contract (see Exhibit 1, section 14)
be removed and that a provision be added so that the Agreement applies even if further
reorganization by either party occurs; and

Fourth, that Freeport reimburse RSU5 the sum of $225,000, representing approximately 26% of
the $853,000 sum contributed by Pownal and Durham for debt service on the Central Office and
Freeport High School properties that will be deeded back to Freeport by RSUS.
Amendment #2: Moved by Kate Brown, seconded by Naomi Ledbetter, to amend Amendment #1
Page 4 B.2. of the Withdrawal Agreement as follows:
Freeport agrees to serve as the school of guaranteed acceptance for Pownal middle school
students for the 10-year period following withdrawal. The tuition rate for Pownal middle school
students attending Freeport Middle School shall be determined under 20-A M.R.S. § 5804(1).
Pownal students enrolled in Freeport schools in the previous school year may continue their
education in Freeport through graduation from Freeport High School, as long as they remain
continuously enrolled in Freeport High School. The tuition rate for Pownal secondary students
attending Freeport High School shall be the midpoint between (a) the tuition rate determined by
the Commissioner based on Freeport's actual per pupil costs in the prior year under 20-A M.R.S.
§ 5805(1), and (b) the state average tuition rate determined by the Commissioner under 20-A
MLR.S. § 5805(2). The tuition rate under section 5805(1) does not include costs for special
education, career and technical education, community services, major capital outlay, debt
retirement, and tuition and transportation. In addition to the secondary tuition rate, the parties
agree that the New Freeport SAU may charge a debt service factor (“Secondary Debt Service
Factor”) pursuant to 20-A M.R.S. §5805(4) for any (i) newly incurred capital outlay and debt
service for Freeport High School; and (ii) newly incurred renovation project capital outlay and
debt service costs for Freeport High School, to the extent that such costs are not included in the
tuition rate calculated under 20-A M.R.S. §5805(1). The parties agree that the Secondary Debt
Service Factor shall not exceed 10% of the applicable legal tuition rate in any one year and must
be limited to a period of time not to exceed Freeport's repayment period for the capital outlay
and debt service. The parties agree that the amount of the secondary Debt Service Factor in any
year is that dollar amount which is proportional to the cost of the project(s) and the number of
students, that is, a percentage of the amount for capital outlay and debt service equal to the
percentage of Freeport High School students that are Pownal students. Freeport will invoice
RSUS for tuition on a semi-annual basis on or before October 15 and April 15, with a
reconciliation after the Department of Education sets final tuition rates.
Freeport has offered to educate all remaining RSUS secondary students for the 9 years following
the first year after withdrawal pursuant to the terms described in the tuition contract that is
attached as Exhibit 1. Freeport will keep this offer open for a period of 60 days following the
Effective Date.
Moved by Kate Brown, seconded by Naomi Ledbetter, to update Amendment #2 by reinserting the
