5. WORKSHOPS:

A. Building Level Strategic Plans: Superintendent Welsh reported the October 8" professional
learning day was the kick-off of the Strategic Plan. We walked through the plan. with staff. Staff
then returned to their buildings and developed Action Plans. The goal of the work on the plans is
to improve our ability to analyze student data and use it to make instructional and curriculum
decisions. At the March 18" professional learning day each school reported out on data from the
first 6 months. The State of the Schools Report was compiled and an Education Forum was held
to inform the public of our work.

Building Principals, staff and a high school student reported out on their plans that have a direct
impact on student learning. Teachers and administrators analyzed student scores on literacy
assessments and developed specific, measurable targets for this first year's timeframe of 6
months. The plans differed based on the needs and strategies at each building/team. Buildings
will now create a plan for a full year.

B. FY12 Budget Review: The Board's workshop provided time for deliberation about the pace at
which we are implementing elements of our strategic plan and the financial impact of that work
on the budget. The Board reviewed the Administrative Priorities and indicated support for
additional literacy coaching and staff professional time. The additions will help move the
district more quickly toward the attainment of our goals.

As noted previously, adjustments in the Superintendent's recommended budget have been made
because we have final information about insurance premium increases; the Region 10 High
School budget (our vocational/technical school); the anticipated fields bond payment, and
changes in debt service due to the fact that the Town of Freeport refinanced bonds which
lowered interest rates and payment amounts. The total of these adjustments along with the
additional literacy coaching and staff professional time to the recommended budget result in the
following anticipated impact to each community:
