4. WORKSHOP:
Great Schools Partnership presented a draft Strategic Plan to the Board. The Plan is divided into five
sections and an Appendix. Sections include: Section I. District Vision and Mission Statement (does
not exist at this time); Section II. Student Achievement; Section HI. District Strategies; Section IV.
Financial Efficiencies; Section V. Public Communication Commitments; and Appendix A. Glossary.
The next step in this important process is to gather input from the staff, students and citizens from
the three communities. Survey Monkey will be used to gather this feedback. Great Schools will
collect and review all feedback. The Plan will be revised based on feedback and draft targets (or a
target range) will be established for each indicator.
At the November 4" Board Meeting, the Board of Directors will receive and discuss the revised draft
Plan and targets, which will again be distributed for review. A Community Forum will be held as
part of the November 18'" Board Meeting for staff and citizens to respond to the newly revised Plan
and targets. Formal adoption of the full Strategic Plan is anticipated for December 9th.
Following adoption, administrators and staff will work together to consider school-based strategies
that support the District Plan. The 2009-2010 school year is a "base-line" year and status checks on
our growth are set for June 2011, 2012, and 2013.
5. OLD BUSINESS:
A. Legislative Process and Agenda on Consolidation.
Superintendent Welsh reported she met with members of the Department of Education on
October 13" to discuss the process following the November 3" vote on consolidation. If the
consolidation law is overturned the legislature has 45 days after entering session to take action.
There are several actions they can take and it depends on the margin of vote. A large margin will
send a clear message to the legislature and a close margin will be harder to figure out. One
possibility would be to turn the RSU's into School Administrative Districts (S.A.D.'s). This will
