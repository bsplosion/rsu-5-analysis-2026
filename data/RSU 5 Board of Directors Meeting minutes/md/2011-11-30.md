6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government — Charlie Baker, Student Council President reported on the
following: winter sports started this week; Mock Trial made it all the way to the semi-finals;
round table discussions on bullying have taken place; RSVP will be leaving tomorrow on a two
day retreat; the first all school student recognition assembly took place today; there will be a
senior financial aid night on December 8"; and seniors are hard at work fundraising for Project
Graduation.

B. Middle School Physical Education Teacher of the Year (Joe Cormier) — Postponed to another
meeting.

C. Freeport Middle School Peer Leaders Red Ribbon Week — Anne-Marie Davee and Phyllis
Latham, Peer Leaders Co-Advisors announced a drug free initiative happening at FMS called
Red Ribbon Week. It was created and implemented by the FMS Peer Leaders, a group of about
25 8" grade students given an opportunity to become leaders and make positive changes. The
key focus is drug free initiatives and building a sense of community and spirit at FMS. Every
Friday students “get their red on” to show support for being drug free. Caiden Shea and Quinton
Libsack, two Peer Leaders, played a video they made of the activities from Red Ribbon Week.

qualifications for architects was advertised and five firms were interviewed by the Facilities
Committee. Site visits to schools the firms were involved with also occurred. PDT was the top
choice and was selected to work with the committee. Architect Lyndon Keck met with Bob
Strong and Athletic Director Craig Sickels to gather information about the Athletic Fields

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:
Department of Education Draft Strategic Plan Core Priority Framework: Superintendent Welsh
reported the Department of Education has created a Strategic Framework. The framework is
closely aligned with RSUS's framework which is good news. The State's plan is to build a
learner-centered education system from the learner out, meaning it is student centered. As you
move out from the center the plan includes: effective instructional practices; great teachers and
leaders; multiple pathways for learner achievement; comprehensive school and community
supports; and an aligned, student-centered educational system. Under each of the sections of the
plan RSUS has implemented or is working on many of them already.
