A. FY14 Budget Review — Opportunity for Town Leadership and Public to Comment
Chair Larkins reviewed the budget process schedule for the next two months. Tonight's meeting
is an opportunity for the Board to hear from the Durham Selectmen and Budget Committee
members on the proposed budget. Selectmen Hall, Larrabee, Wakeman and Budget Committee
members Kevin Nadeau and Ben Love were at the meeting.
Selectman and Budget members asked questions about the percentage increase to the budget,
how valuations effect the local contribution, enrollments and upgrades to the Central Office.

Kelly Wentworth, Director of Finance reviewed the tax impact to each town (pages 12, 13 and
14 of the budget handout). The recommended budget reflects a net increase of 2.58%
($644,847) with the following anticipated impact to each community:
Durham — an increase of — $343,593 or a mil rate increase of $1.04 (7.81%)
Freeport — an increase of — $355,664 or a mil rate increase of $0.26 (1.69%)
Pownal — an increase of — $161,820 or a mil rate increase of $2.09 (6.20%)
It was also important to note that the state assessed valuation of each of our three towns has
changed and that has impacted our state subsidy. This would have changed what each town
would pay for education regardless of whether we are a consolidated system or separate
individual systems.
Public Comments on the Budget:
Durham:
Richard Johnson spoke about the increase in taxes and recommends a decrease by 5%. Also
encouraged residents to attend the Annual Budget meeting and to vote.
Alice McPeak had questions about enrollments, new special education positions and what a flat
budget would look like.
Arnold Cloutier suggested closing Pownal Elementary School and moving the students to
Durham Community School.
Tim Kegan asked if the RPC agreement should be looked at and asked a question about fields.
John Primevara spoke about voting and changing the formula.
Kevin Nadeau stated it would not be in the best interest of Durham to change the RPC
agreement
Freeport:
Joe Migliaccio stated he is in favor of the school budget and fields project.
Pownal:
Alan Bradstreet noted his taxes will go up and will vote against the budget and bond. He also
encouraged residents to vote.
Chair Larkins was asked that the Board consider changing the Annual Budget meeting to a
Saturday and moving it to Durham. This was a request from the Durham Selectmen for next
year, not this year.
