8. OLD BUSINESS:
A. Review Budget FY'10
Commissioner of Education Susan Gendron was in attendance at the meeting to explain the
circumstances prompting her withdrawal of a recent grant proposal. The Commissioner
explained she had recently offered a grant to M.S.A.D. #62 (Pownal) in the amount of $190,000.
The grant had been intended to study the longer-term financial impact(s) and potential
educational benefits of consolidation. The Legislative Education Committee expressed concerns
about providing funds to one system to study the impact of consolidation when other systems did
not have that same opportunity. These concerns prompted the Commissioner to withdraw the
grant offer.
The Commissioner stated that she does not have authorization to delay the implementation date
of the RSU. Current law requires systems to be operational by July 1, 2009. Systems found not
to be in compliance face consequences that may include the loss of state subsidy. Commissioner
Gendron offered to facilitate a meeting to search for a resolution to the current challenges facing
the RSU. The meeting was scheduled for May 18, 2008 at 7:00 P.M. in the high school cafeteria
The Board discussed the high school's participation in the State's laptop program. Grant funds,
funds from the current year's budget and from the 09-10 budget will be used for the first year of
the program. Funding for following years may come from the reallocation of budget items such
as tuition funds as more of Durham's students attend the high school.
Voted: (2) To authorize the Superintendent to enter into agreements to commit to the 9-12 MLTI
program (Larkins — Peters) (8 — 0 — 1 Kaplan)

11. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications: Betsy Peters reported the committee met on May 4" and has decided to
postpone the Future Search Conference due to the current situation.

Referendum vote.

B. Finance: John Morang reported the committee met on May 7" to continue discussions on the

fees for negotiations. There is $59,140.29 remaining in the interim budget. Bank RFP's were
received from Bath Savings and Key Bank. Deeds were also discussed and there is a small issue
with the Freeport Middle School property.

C. Negotiations: The committee has been meeting and discussing how to move forward. Meetings
have been scheduled this week with Durham support staff, Teachers and Administrators.
