7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS: FIRST DAYS OF SCHOOL & BUILDING

STRTEGIC PLAN

A. FHS Principal Bob Strong and Assistant Principal Ann Marie Barter reported on action plan
results from 2012-2013 including: increased student participation in higher level math classes
and increased participation in advanced level science classes; improvement in SAT and PSAT
scores. Goals for 13-14 include: math interventions; increased student achievement; evidence-
based practices; and standards-based curriculum.

B. FMS Principal Ray Grogan and Assistant Principal Eleanor Sears reported on the action plan
results from 2012-2013 including: successfully created writing prompt and selected anchor
papers in pilot year; 78% of students met or exceeded on 2012 NECAP writing test;
implemented advisory groups at each grade level; discussions occurred about healthy
relationships; focused on active bystander; and Got Character campaign. Goals for 13-14
include: increase student 21“ century learning skills with the iPad; and improving student
writing.

C. DCS Principal Will Pidden reported on the action plan results from 2012-2013 including:
Kindergarten students forming lower case letters correctly; grades 1 & 2 increased spelling and
word study skills by at least one spelling category; 89% of 3 graders met writing volume on a
timed on-demand piece and 67% of 4h graders met the goal; 5" and 6" graders increased their
ability to use details from test to meet the standard on written constructed response questions; 7”
and 8" graders increased % of students passing core subjects. Goals for 13-14 include: to refine

D. PES Principal Lisa Demick reported on the action plan results from 2012-2013 including: 80%

increase in the number of students meeting the benchmark on the Primary Spelling inventory;
and an increase of students' skills to self-manage their behaviors. Goals for 13-14 include: 80%
of students will demonstrate an increase of at least 1 grade level in elaboration and craft using

students meeting the benchmark on the Primary Spelling inventory.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Celebration of First Days of School — Superintendent Welsh reported that feedback received
from educators from the 3 Professional Learning Days in August shows educators enjoyed
working together before the arrival of the students. Enrollments compared to last year show a
decrease of 27 students. All schools are down with Durham seeing a slight increase. RSU5 has
an incredible staff working with our students and because of that we attract high quality
applicants for open positions.

B. Work Plan for 2013-2014 - Superintendent Welsh reported on the draft work plan for the 2013-
2014 school year. The plan includes the following areas: Strategic Plan Building Level Goal
Setting and Report Out; State of the Schools Report; Professional Growth and Evaluation
System; Communication Promoting the Educational Benefits of the RSU; Budget FY15
Development and Adoption; FHS Facility and Grounds; and Negotiations.

9. PUBLIC COMMENTS:

Charly Haverstat, Freeport resident stated a grassroots committee in Freeport has started step one of

the RSU withdrawal process by starting a petition.

Sara Tracy, Freeport Town Council member explained the withdrawal study will be posted on the

Town's website on Thursday and that it is a 1“ draft.

Kevin Nadeau, Durham resident talked about the two votes needed for withdrawal.
Rich DeGrandpre — Freeport Town Council member, stated withdrawal is more than just dividing
the assets it is like a divorce and also involves children.
