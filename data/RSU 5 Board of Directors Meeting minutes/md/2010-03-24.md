7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, reported on the first draft of the proposed 2010-2011
school calendar. Four calendar options were created and shared with Administration which was
then reduced to one. The proposed calendar reduces Pownal and Freeport's early release days,
the high school's late arrival days and adds seven early release days to Durham. The goal was to
have common professional development time throughout the district without impacting the
budget. Sarah reported the draft calendar will be posted on the website and feedback will be
accepted until Noon on April 5". Revisions will be made to the calendar, if needed, and will be
brought to the Board on April 14" for a 1“ read and April 28" for 2"! Read/Adoption.

Board members expressed concern about the inconsistent approach to early-release days in the
calendar; the impact on instructional time with students, and alternatives for professional
development time for teachers. The Board asked that the Use of Time committee meet to
consider the draft calendar in light of that committee's previous recommendations.

10. UNFINISHED BUSINESS:

A. Review Budget FY'11: Superintendent Welsh reviewed the packet of information that included
responses to questions asked at the March 10" budget meeting. Clarification was provided on
the savings from health services, library books, secretarial support, and the relocation of Pownal
6-8 grades. Stipend positions were reviewed along with the positions funded for Gifted and
Talented.

Superintendent Welsh presented the Pownal Middle School Recommendation Summary to the
Board and public. The summary explains that equity of educational opportunities had been a
concern expressed by several parents about Pownal's middle school program. Parents have
commented on a strengthened middle level program this year; however, there are limited funds
to increase student access to other offerings and opportunities. The question to consider was
how, in light of decreasing state revenues, we can continue to provide varied academic, athletic,
and co-curricular experiences to all of our students while limiting the impact to taxpayers. These
considerations led to the recommendation to replace Pownal's current middle school program
with the choice of attending either Durham or Freeport middle school with transportation being
provided to either school. Recommendation considerations include: preserving a PK-5 program
in Pownal and provide options for the meaningful use of the middle school classrooms; class
sizes at Freeport and Durham middle schools would be targeted at 20-22 students; providing
strong educational opportunities for our middle level student would be more cost effective at two
middle schools instead of three; transportation would be provided to either middle school; budget
reduction of $185,000 due to the elimination of three teaching positions and the addition of
transportation to Durham; and the use of the space at Pownal to ensure the building remains an
active and dynamic environment.

14. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications — Betsy Peters reported the committee met on March 4" and March 18".
Kristen Stetson, a professional graphic designer, from Pownal and Jeff Brazee were in
attendance on March 4" to finalize the layout of the website. A new feature of the website will
be the “Schools Delivering on the Mission”. This will be a student/school focused element of the
biweekly message. A donation button will be displayed on the website. The volunteer sub-page
has also been set up as a method of identifying volunteers who have specific skills or areas of
interest. A High School Students Campaign is in the planning stages. This campaign will focus
on retaining students and attracting others who attend a non-RSU high school. This will be
discussed further at the Communications meeting on April 8". Budget communications will be
an on-going item that will be discussed at each of our future meetings as we work toward an
adopted budget approved by the voters.

B. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on February 24" and March 17". At the
February 24" meeting the committee selected members to be included on the Strategic Planning
Financial Indicators Task Force, they were: Rod Regier, Allan Purinton, Cliff Ruprecht and Lois
Kilby-Chesley. The Task Force has met three times. The committee also briefly discussed
transportation services. Shannon reviewed the pubic budget meetings that were held throughout
the district and reviewed the areas of discussion.

At the March 17" meeting warrants were signed. The committee discussed the conversion to
natural gas. Maine Natural Gas is bringing a pipeline through Pownal into Freeport and three of
our schools would be on the line. Eric reviewed the conversion comparison sheet. The
conversions would provide us with dual fuel capabilities in these three buildings. The committee
continued discussion on the 2010-2011 budget and additional handouts for the March 24"
meeting including Pownal total savings and other options. More information will be presented
on the Pownal 6-8 choice option on the 24". The committee reviewed the work of the Strategic
Planning Financial Task Force. We are preparing the bus bid for leasing two buses for 2010-
2011, the audit bid and the insurance bids. We will be having an on-site review of our AARA
funds by the Department of Education.

C. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported several different sessions have been held with Pownal
support staff, teachers and administrators. We will be meeting with teachers in the next two
weeks.

D. Policy — Cori Holt reported the committee met on March 12". The committee did not receive
any feedback on policy packet 10-5. The packet was finalized for 2" Read/Adoption. The
committee reviewed and prepared policy packet 10-6 for draft feedback. The committee
received feedback on the use of private vehicles policy EEBB in packet 10-5. A component
authorizing the Superintendent to develop procedures for student use of vehicles and the
transportation of other students in private vehicles was added to the policy. The revised packet
10-6 was prepared for 1*' Read on March 24" and 2" Read/Adoption targeted for April 28". The
committee decided to pull the draft Superintendent's evaluation instrument from packet 10-6 and
work on that document during May. Packet 10-7 was reviewed and compiled for distribution on
March 24" for feedback.
