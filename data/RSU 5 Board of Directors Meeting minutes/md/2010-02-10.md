C. Use of Time Report Out: Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director presented the Use of
Time Advisory Committee Final Report. The committee met five times since November
19"" with Heidi McGinley from the Department of Education as the facilitator. The
committee analyzed the current RSU No. 5 calendar and the allocation of professional
development time in each town; researched various aspects of professional development
time; and looked at calendars from districts both in surrounding communities and districts
deemed “high performing.” Focus groups were convened and electronic surveys were
provide to staff, families and community members to gather feedback. The committee
reached consensus on common full in-service days for teachers in the 2010-2011 RSU
No. 5 calendar: maintain the legal requirements of 175 student days and 180 teacher
days; allocate professional compensation time for teachers in one full day and two half
days. This allocation of professional compensation time would result in four full in-
service days across all schools and towns. There is also significant majority support in
the committee to allocate the two professional compensation days as four half days.
Doing so will yield five full in-service days during the school year with no economic
impact; schedule all full in-service days on the same dates in all schools and towns. The
committee did not reach full agreement on the inclusion of early release time or late
arrival in the 2010-2011 calendar.

Draft calendar options will be developed in mid-February and will go through a feedback
process, which will result in calendar options being presented to the Board for a 1st read
at the March 24" meeting, followed by time for public comment, and with anticipated 2"
read/adoption by the Board on April 28".

D. Mast Landing Report Out: Superintendent Welsh reported this year was a pilot year at
Mast Landing School with a one principal model instead of the teaching-principal model
as in the past. Parents, staff and administrators were asked to provide us with their
thoughts regarding the effectiveness of the one-principal model as compared to the
teaching-principal model in place last year. The results of the feedback received indicate
strong support for the one-principal model. Tonight's report was for informational
purposes. Action will be taken at the February 24" Board meeting on the one-principal
model.
