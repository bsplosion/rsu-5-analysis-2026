7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Student Information System — Seth Thompson, Technology Director, reported on the findings of
the SIS Selection Team. A student information system (SIS) is a program designed to manager
student data. Currently RSUS uses three different SISs. The goal of the SIS Selection Team was
to select one SIS for district wide adoption. The team evaluated three different programs,
Infinite Campus, PowerSchool, and Web2School. Based on group discussions, webinars,
information from surrounding school districts, and individual research, the team agreed that
PowerSchool was the best choice for the RSU.

9. PUBLIC COMMENTS:
Freeport: Nicky Knighton is a member of the parent led High School renovation project

10. UNFINISHED BUSINESS:

A. Budget FY' 12 — Superintendent Welsh reported tonight is an opportunity to further discuss the
recommended FY' 12 budget that was presented last evening. The Budget Goal is to continue to
increase educational excellence while demonstrating a greater efficiency in the use of our
resources. Staff and administration have spent months looking at revenues and programming to

, stay at a flat budget. All of the Board's priorities were added to this budget. We have benefited
as an RSU by not paying tuition to other high schools and reinvesting that savings in
programming.

The Board asked questions regarding class sizes, professional development days, and asked what
items were not added.

Continued budget deliberations will occur over the next two months ending June 4" with a
three-town Annual Budget Meeting followed by a Budget Validation Referendum on June 14".
All citizens are encouraged to participate in the process.

B. Fields and Grounds Update ~ Craig Sickels, Athletic Director updated the Board on meetings
with community groups that have taken place since the last Board meeting. He has also talked
with Donna Larson, Town Planner regarding parking for the complex. Craig has also talked with
companies regarding lighting and sound restrictions in an effort to gather preliminary
information. The neighbors concerns include: traffic flow and safety; parking; lights and noise.
Mr. Sickels spoke to the fundraising effort he and possibly members of the fields and grounds
committee would take on to help with offsetting the bond payments.

Kelly Wentworth, Director of Finance discussed specifics to the $2.9 million bond including a
term of 15 years with the initial interest only payment of $56,000 due the spring of 2011-2012
and $299,377 the following year.

14. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Policy — Cori Holt reported the committee met on March 11" and received a significant amount
of feedback on policy packet 11-4. The committee reviewed the draft GCMA Instructional
Staff Planning Time policy. The policy provides flexibility so the Board can respond to
changing conditions without having to constantly change policies. This flexibility does not
change the Board's intent to provide planning time, it simply reflects the fact that the amount of
time is dependent upon the availability of staff and the budget. The committee and the Board as
a whole understand the importance of planning time and how hard our staff works to provide a
quality educational program. Policy packet 11-5 received no feedback and was finalized for 1st Read on March 23" with 2nd Read/Adoption on April 27". Packet 11-6 was prepared for
distribution on March 23". 1 Read will take place on April 27" with 2" Read/Final adoption
on May 27, 2011.

B.& C. Communications and Strategic Planning — Jane Blais reported that the committees have
met with Ken Templeton from Great Schools to continue work to review a draft of the
information for our upcoming State of the Schools Report. The report was mailed to every
household in the three communities. The committee also discussed the RSU5 Education Forum
scheduled for March 29, 2011 at 6:30 p.m. in the FRAC. Academic and financial indicators
outlined in the Strategic Framework will be used to provide an accounting of our performance
at this point in time. The Forum will provide a community discussion of what's been
accomplished, areas to celebrate, and what goals we should establish as we look to the future.

D. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met two times. At the February 16" meeting an
update on the Audit was provided, the State of Maine granted us an additional extension to
March 15". The committee reviewed the preliminary estimate for the 2011-2012 state subsidy.
As expected there is a reduction in the amount of state funded debt service as the final payment
on the Mast Landing School was made in 2010-2011. The funding level is close to the earlier
estimates as our valuation remains flat. John Morang gave the committee a status update of the
High School Athletic Field Complex and the Hunter Road complex. At the March 9" meeting it
was reported work is being done on the final research projects with the Auditors. The updated
Capital Plan document was presented to the committee. The Facilities Committee toured the
buildings in mid-October. The goal of the plan has continued to be that $400,000 per year be
raised for capital improvements and enhancements. The committee reviewed the proposed
2011-2012 budget and handouts. The Board's budget priorities have been worked into the
budget. New to the budget this year is the creation of the Capital and Fuel Reserve Accounts.
E. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported support staff negotiations will be held tomorrow night
and the first mediation session with the teachers is scheduled for March 31°. Administrators are
working on a different structure.
