7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Use of Time Update — Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, reported at the January 26" Board
meeting the Use of Time Advisory Committee presented two calendar options that both carried
significant financial implications. The Board charged the committee with developing at least
two additional calendar options, a low cost option and a no cost option. The committee met on
February 3“ and developed both of the requested options, and collapsed the two options
presented in January into a single option. Each calendar presented tonight increases student
learning time through eliminating the use of early release and late arrival time. The committee
looked at high performing schools to compare student learning time and found RSUS is on the
lower end for learning time. Calendar option | puts all of the cost in actual dollars in order to
maintain the current amount of professional learning time. Calendar option 2 allocates less of
the cost to actual dollars, but balances that out with a cost in the form of a loss of professional
learning time. Calendar option 3 puts all of the cost in loss of professional learning time. The
committee's first choice is option 1. After much discussion the Board's decision is to distribute a
calendar adding two professional learning days to the public for feedback. The feedback period
will take place February 17-March 4.

B. Fields and Grounds Update — Craig Sickels, Athletic Director, reported the fields and grounds
committee has met with the Hunter Road group and the committee recommends the following:
defer the reconfiguration of the Freeport Middle School athletic fields and the enlargement of the
High School's baseball outfield; go forward with plans for a High School event complex and
stadium, consisting of an 8 lane track, artificial turf field, lights, bleachers, press box, concession
stand, and bathrooms; and the RSU Board of Directors make this investment and ask the voters
of Durham, Freeport and Pownal to support a June 2011 Referendum.

VOTED: (2) To authorize the fields and grounds committee to enter into a discussion with the
Hunter Road group regarding potential synergies and a unified vision. Authorize the fields and
grounds committee to prepare operational budget numbers for the Board to look at what the
ongoing costs would be, including the Hunter Road facility, assuming the RSU would be taking
care of that facility. Authorize the fields and grounds committee to present to the FEDC, the
town leadership of the three communities, and any other community groups for the purpose of
communicating and implementing plans and measuring support.
(Dube — Morang) (9 — 0)

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Budget Priorities — Superintendent Welsh reported on the list of budget priorities anticipated for
the 2011-2012 school year. Over the past few months detailed presentations have been presented
to the Board. The priorities presented have been developed with input from teachers, advisory
committees, administrators, and the strategic plan. The proposed priority areas include the
following: Capital investments increase of approximately $80,000; technology investment
increase of approximately $95,000; enrolments (new positions) increase of approximately
$260,000; curriculum material increase of approximately $85,000; athletics increase of
approximately $25,000; and the addition of two professional learning days at an increase of
approximately $100,000.
