8. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

End of Year Updates:

A. Tom Ambrose reported on the following: literacy grant received for before and after school
support; work with Dr. Ross Green on collaborative problem solving; excitement of staff on
additional technology; [Pads for students bought with PTC funds; and work on strategic plans.

B. Beth Willhoite reported on the following: graduation of 89 5" graders; 84 2" graders had step-
up day last week; strategic plan work with the addition last year of literacy and library support
went well this year; leadership team work this year; emphasis this year on the Art, PE and
Music; guidance, counselors and special education teachers worked on providing breakfast for
students; and 43 students received camp scholarships this year.

C. Lisa Demick reported on the following: it has been a wonderful year in Pownal; teachers
worked on building relationships and high aspirations; a wonderful musical took place; students
went to Merrill Auditorium to see Charlotte's Web; 5'" and 6" grade students were very
thoughtful about which middle school to attend next year; we will move to trimesters next year;
some classrooms will move next year; and a new Math piece next year with online use for home
use, and assessments.

D. Will Pidden reported on the following: the new building is great and children were patient as we
learned how to best use it; continued work on science and members from the Maine Math and
Science Alliance and National Semi Conductor visited the school; staff members were asked to
present at a STEM conference; teacher leadership teams were a great addition this year; next
year we will continue work on RTI, Readers Workshop and move to trimesters to align with
other schools.

E. Ray Grogan reported on the following: students took the NECAP's in the fall and they met or
exceeded in two areas; students took the NWEA in the spring and also made very good growth;
6"" graders worked on a train study then presented the results; 8" graders worked on a Dirty
Thirties unit based on the 1930's; 7" graders working on a salt water touch tank with funds
from outside sources; and the Pownal students have been a wonderful addition to the school.

F. Bob Strong reported on the following: primary focus of leadership teams this year was
academic performance and student recognition; teams also worked on interdisciplinary PLG's,
technology and literacy; many programs happening at school including Mentoring, RSVP,
Interact Club; new courses will be added next year, AP Music Theory, Ceramics, Freshmen
Engineering; Senior Projects were wonderful; Parent Team has been very involved this year;
and the Facilities Advisory Committee will continue to meet during the summer and might
come back to the Board in August to re-look at other areas in the charge of the committee.

9. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Budget FY12 — Superintendent Welsh reported the school budget for 2011-2012 has passed and
thanked the voters for their support. The Campus Complex was defeated. The committee
documented a need but due to the economy taxpayers did not want to take on an additional tax
burden.
