6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Technology Presentation: Diane Atwood, Social Studies teacher at Freeport High School and an
online instructor, provided an overview of Virtual High School. Virtual High School is a new
program at the high school that allows students to take online elective courses. There are over
200 courses offered through Virtual High School for students to choose from. Currently there
are four Freeport High School students taking courses through Virtual High School.

B. Report from Student Government — Lucy Whitacre and Shelby Colvin reported on activities that
the Student Government have taken on. The school year started with a Spirit Day, followed by a
Halloween Costume dance this past Friday. On December 11" there will be an Arts Fair and a
Winter Carnival will be scheduled for January or February. Elections were held and Freshmen
members have joined Student Government.

7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS/EDUCATION COMMITTEES:

A. Professional Development Report Out by Curriculum Director and Principals — Sarah Simmonds,
Curriculum Director, started by reporting out on the activities that took place at the Early Release
Day today. Pownal and Freeport staff were able to meet together K-5 at Mast Landing and 6-8 at
Freeport Middle School. K-5 staff discussed opportunities to share; resources to share; and
challenges. Grade 6-8 teachers worked with the Literacy Strategist on vocabulary development.

Each building principal spoke about professional development that is taking place in each
building this school year. Common themes between buildings are technology, school culture,
Every Day Math and Response to Intervention.

B. H1N1 Update — Joan Nason, Student Services Director reported on the three vaccine clinics
taking place in Freeport, Pownal and Durham over the next week. The seasonal and HIN1
vaccines will be available. This has been a group effort between the Rescue departments in each
town, Freeport Medical, CHANS, the school nurses, the School Physician, and many volunteer
nurses and physicians.

C. Anticipated Subsidy Reduction — Diane Boucher, Finance Director, informed the Board of
possible subsidy reductions to the current school budget. We are looking at a possible reduction
of $658,000 which may be even larger, depending upon state revenues. Diane walked the Board
through the list of known savings, anticipated revenues, and anticipated building level savings.
Superintendent Welsh discussed with the Board the possible reduction to the vacant nursing
positions in Freeport and Pownal by using CNA's instead of filling the positions with nurses
which would result in a savings. Superintendent Welsh discussed next steps would be sending a
message to staff and the communities.

14. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications — Betsy Peters reported the Committee met on October 22" to discuss
communications to support the Strategic Plan process. The Committee discussed ways to
encourage public comment on the Strategic Plan at the Community Forum on November 18" by
doing the following: sending a flyer to all households in the district; sending e-mails and robo
calls to parents in the district; submitting an update to Shopping Notes and the Forecaster.

B. Drop Out Prevention — Brenda Kielty reported the Committee met on October 8" with
representatives from all grade levels, guidance and community members. There are a lot of great
things in place to support At-Risk students. Data on drop-outs is hard to read and the measures
keep changing. Freeport High School had at least 23 students between 2006 and 2009 that did
not receive a diploma. The three key issues in keeping students in school are: attendance, getting
them to come to school; attachment, a critical adult that cares; achievement, being successful at
something. The Committee will meet again in December/January.

C. Finance: Eric Dube reported the Finance Committee met on October 14". The RSU warrant was
signed. An update on the facility/capital plan was heard. A representative from each town will
be on the Facility Committee. A 3-5 year capital plan will be created and presented to the
Finance Committee. A transportation snap-shot was presented to the committee outlining
previous year expenditures and the current FY10 budget. The Committee was updated on
possible state subsidy reductions. RFP for loans for the purchase of the two new buses were
presented to the Committee. The RSU start up budget was reviewed.

On October 20" the Finance Committee met with Jim Rier from the Department of Education to
discuss the development of a 3-5 year financial plan for the district. The discussion started with
“How do you make a plan”, define the driving elements, develop ways of looking at them,
changes and adjustments. Jim reviewed with the committee how EPS (Essential Programs and
Services) subsidy allocations are determined by the state and components in EPS that will effect
it going forward. The next meeting will include information entered into the template.

D. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported negotiations are still ongoing with the three groups.

E. Policy — The Policy Committee met on October 16'". The Committee discussed feedback
received on packet 10-1 which is up for adoption. The feedback pertained to Policy IJJ —
Instructional and Library-Media Materials Selection. The Committee recommends keeping the
policy general in nature. The Committee also reviewed and prepared policy packet 10-2 for 1“
Read. A meeting will be set up for consideration of the Board self-evaluation and the
Superintendent job description/evaluation.

F. Strategic Planning — Superintendent Welsh reported the Committee met on October 8". A
summary of that meeting was provided to the Board on October 14"". The Committee will meet
again with Great Schools on November 3" to review feedback on the draft Strategic Plan.
