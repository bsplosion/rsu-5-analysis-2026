INFORMATIONAL DISCUSSION ITEMS:

Board Orientation — Superintendent Tomaszewski mentioned a Board orientation through Maine School

Management Association as a first step. The need for further information can then be determined.

DOE Grant — Vision for RSU No. 5 — Superintendent Tomaszewski informed the Board the Department

of Education is making available to RSU's non-competitive grants of $5,000 for educational planning

and the services of a facilitator for up to 18 months. The work is not limited to curriculum. A straw poll
of the Board was taken, all members indicated they were in favor of pursuing the grant.

Selection of Superintendent — Superintendent Tomaszewski reviewed the first action the new Board
needs to take which is to select a Superintendent, either an interim to serve until July 1, 2009 or a
permanent to serve immediately (as interim) and carry into the district July 1, 2009 as permanent
Superintendent.
Voted: (7) To appoint a Sub-committee to research internal v. external Superintendent selection process
and to report back to the Board at the next meeting.
(Kaplan — Poissonnier) (9 — 0 — 1 Dube)
Voted: (8) To appoint Cori Holt, Jennifer Kaplan and Beth Parker to serve on the Superintendent selection
process sub-committee. (Morang — Dube) (10 — 0)
FY-10 Budget Process — Superintendent Welsh reviewed the proposed process for developing the RSU
No. 5 2009-2010 budget. Durham, Freeport and Pownal School Committees will work on their budgets
separately. In late February/early March the three budgets will be merged into one for Board review. A
proposed timeframe for the budget process was reviewed and will be finalized at the next meeting.
Schedule of Meetings — Superintendent Welsh reviewed the proposed meeting schedule for the Board.
The proposal is to meet on the second and fourth Wednesdays of every month until July 1, 2009.
Meetings will be held at the Freeport High School Library at 6:30 P.M. and will be taped for later
broadcast in each town. The proposed schedule was reviewed and will be finalized at the next meeting.
Required work and suggested timeline — Superintendent Welsh reviewed the work needed to be done to
build a strong organization. Much of the work will be done by employees who will then bring
recommendations to the Board for action. Meetings with directors have already begun.
