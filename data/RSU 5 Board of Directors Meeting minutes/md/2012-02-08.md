B. Superintendent's Update: Superintendent Welsh reported the citizens of Durham have submitted
a petition to the Durham Selectmen to add a Warrant Article to the April Town Meeting. The
Article will ask for $25,000 for a committee to work on the withdrawal from the RSU. There
will also be a referendum vote in June which would need a majority vote of the Durham citizens
to start the planning process for withdrawal. Dr. Welsh also reported the State has set a high bar
for towns wishing to withdraw from an RSU and there is a clearly defined process for doing so.
There is another group of residents in Durham that do not want to withdraw because they see the
benefits of the RSU. We will keep our focus on the RSU as a whole.

6. WORKSHOP:

A. Use of Time Update — Joe Makley reported the committee met for a third time on February 1* to
develop final recommendations for the 2012-2013 calendar. The committee chose two calendar
scenarios with justification for each. Version | has no budget impact, and is similar to last year.
Version 2 has an additional flex day. The flex-time is proposed by the committee to provide
structured, recurring sessions of 1.5 hours of extended professional development time. An electronic
survey link will be distributed to parents, staff, community members and students. The committee
will meet again on March 7" to review the feedback. First read on the draft calendar is anticipated
for April 11" with second read/adoption on April 25".

B. Budget Priorities - Superintendent Welsh reported on the list of budget priorities for the 2012-2013
school year. Administration has begun the process of developing a budget to present to the Board.
Administrators have met with teachers and staff members to gather budget information.
Administrators then met individually with the Superintendent and Finance Director on their
proposed budget. Administrators will now come together as a team to craft a budget to bring to the
Board on March 27" and 28". The budget is still in development stages at this time so there are no
