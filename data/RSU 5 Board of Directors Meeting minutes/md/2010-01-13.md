5. WORKSHOP:
A. Consolidation Legislation discussion with Legislators from Durham, Freeport and Pownal:
RSU No. 5 Senators Gerzofsky and Simpson along with Representatives Webster, VanWie and
Austin were in attendance. Senator Alfond, Co-Chair of the Education Committee, also attended
the meeting to listen to the Board's suggested improvements to the consolidation law. Suggested
improvements to the law are in three areas: elimination of the budget validation referendum due
to the cost and it is redundant to the Annual Budget meeting; adding language to the law that
would create a process for dissolution or opt out; adding language that enables an RSU to set a
common date for Board elections at either the June or November state funded referendum.
Senator Alfond stated the Education Committee will discuss LD 570 along with other bills
dealing with consolidation on Monday, January 25"". The Board was encouraged to put their
suggestions in writing and to attend the hearing.
Freeport residents Rich DeGrandpre and Joe Migliaccio spoke about consolidation.
B. Fields/Grounds Capital Planning:
The Facility Sub-Committee presented the RSU No. 5 Capital/Facility Plan to the Board. Tours
and assessments have been completed with the exception of Durham Community School, due to
the construction of a new school. Buildings seem to be in very good repair. A 20 year life cycle
plan has been developed for the infrastructures of the buildings. The information informs us of
the replacement timelines. A 5 year Capital Plan was developed by taking the 20-year plan and

A. Update on procedure to review RSU name.
Superintendent Welsh wanted to reassure the community there will be public input for
developing a name for the RSU. There will be an outreaching of communications the same as
has been done in the past for budget and strategic planning. Round table discussions were
originally scheduled for December 3™ but were delayed. There are no specific dates at this point.
The goal is to give an identity to the RSU as an organization.
