4. ADJUSTMENTS TO THE AGENDA:
Chairperson Poissonnier noted there will be two items under New Business added to the agenda.
Item 8. C. Deed language for Freeport and 8. D. Deed language for Durham.

5. Superintendent'S REPORT:
Superintendent Welsh notified the Board the Durham Selectman met Tuesday, June 9" and approved
the deed with the clarification of the emergency shelter added. Freeport and Pownal letters have
gone out. Both the Freeport Town Council and the MSAD #62 School Board have meetings
scheduled for June 23". The Freeport Town Council requested more detailed deeds. The Town
Council also requested clarification on what would happen to the deeds if the RSU were to dissolve.
The attorneys are working on the information requested which will be shared with the Board and the
MSAD #62 Board.

6. PUBLIC COMMENTS:
Freeport resident: Joe Migliaccio asked a question regarding Maine State Retirement contributions
for support employees. Mr. Migliaccio also mentioned the Pownal Road field maintenance issue
was always brought up during budget discussions between the town and school.
Pownal resident: Tim Giddinge asked if the signs the Communications Sub-committee are making
regarding the June 16'" Annual Budget meeting and the June 25" Budget Validation Referendum
would be distributed to all three towns.

7. OLD BUSINESS:

8. NEW BUSINESS:

A. Voted: (2) To become a Participating Local District in the Maine Public Employees Retirement
System and adopt Regular Plan AC, effective July 1, 2009 for all former school support
employees of the Town of Durham who become employed by the RSU and who were members
of the Town's PLD plans immediately prior to becoming employed by the RSU, excluding all
other employees of RSU No. 5, and to authorize the Superintendent to sign the contract
between RSU No. 5 and MainePERS. (Dube — McKechnie) (9 — 0)

Superintendent Welsh explained there are currently eight support staff in Durham who
contribute to Maine State Retirement System instead of Social Security. Section 218 states
those eight employees can be grandfathered as we enter into an RSU if the Board approves this
motion. There is no financial impact to the RSU.

B. Voted: (3) To authorize RSU No. 5 to enter into a trust agreement, between the Maine School
Management Association's Unemployment Compensation Trust Fund and RSU No. 5, July 1,
2009 and to make contributions to the Trust Fund on behalf of employees. Further move that
our school unit meet its obligation to provide unemployment compensation protection on its
employees, through reimbursing method, by participating in the Maine School Management
Association Unemployment Compensation Trust Fund, and further move that the
Superintendent of Schools be authorized to create a new account with Maine School
Management Association Unemployment Compensation Trust Fund. The Superintendent is
further authorized to enter into an agreement to administer our unemployment compensation
program with Maine School Management on behalf of RSU No. 5. (Dube — Morang) (9 — 0)
Superintendent Welsh explained this item is for unemployment insurance. Pownal and Durham
are currently with Maine School Management Association and Freeport has been self funded
with the Town of Freeport. The Finance Sub-committee recommends the RSU participating in
the MSMA Unemployment Compensation Trust Fund beginning January 2010.

C. Voted: (4) Pursuant to 20-A MRSA section 1462 and the Plan of Reorganization that the
property described in the deed presented to this meeting be deemed necessary to carry out the
functions of the Regional School Unit, that the Superintendent of Schools again request that the
municipal officers Town of Freeport complete the transfer of that property by July 1, 2009, the
operational date of the regional school unit, and to the extent the deed presented to this meeting
has been amended from the deed previously approved by this board, that said amendments are
hereby ratified and approved. (Parker — McKechnie) (9 — 0)

Superintendent Welsh stated that the first deed was brief so it was revised to break out each
deed by property. The issue with the Middle School and Library property has been resolved
with the town stating they would help relocate the Middle School ropes course if the Library
was to expand. The Powal Road field issue has not yet been resolved. There is ongoing
discussion regarding ownership. The field was not part of the RPC plan so it does not have to be
resolved by July 1°.

D. Voted: (5) That the proposed deed of Durham school real estate to Regional School Unit 5
previously approved by this Board be amended to include a provision to the effect that the
Town of Durham reserves and excepts the right to use as a shelter during periods of public
emergency the portions of the building being constructed on the property that have been
proposed for use for that purpose so long as that building continues to be used for public school
purposes, and that the Superintendent be authorized to accept the deed with that change.
(Dube - McKechnie) (9 — 0)
Shannon Welsh stated it is a long standing practice in Durham to use the school as an
emergency shelter. A grant has been received for a generator for the new school. Language in
the deed was added to reflect this.

A. 09-Packet #1 was distributed for policy book update.
11. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Policy: Kristen Dorsey reported the committee met on June 5" to review feedback and finalize
packets for board consideration. Policy BE regarding Board of Directors meetings was discussed
in depth and considered the possibility of moving the RSU Board meetings to Mondays.
Feedback from the nurses regarding proposed medical policies was also reviewed and changes
were made to reflect their recommendations. The policy committee decided to prepare Policy
binders for Board members with copies available on-line. Packet 09-3 will have a First Read and
Packet 09-2 for adoption at the June 24" Board meeting.

Board members provided input on changing the meeting day to Mondays. The meeting day for
Board meetings will continue on Wednesdays with a time change to 7:00 P.M. starting in July.
This change will begin in July.

B. Communications: Kristen Dorsey reported the committee met on June 1“ to review feedback
on the lists of people who will be invited to attend the Future Search Conference. The
Committee decided to stay with the dates of August 27, 28, and 29. A component of the
conference is learning more about the 21* century skills students will need for their future. The
Board received a list of attendees. Board members are invited to the conference.

Beth Parker stated her concern with the list of attendees. Beth believes the list is heavy on
elementary and there are not enough Middle and High School attendees.
