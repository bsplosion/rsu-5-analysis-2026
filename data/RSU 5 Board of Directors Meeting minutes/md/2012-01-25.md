7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Use of Time Update — Joe Makley, Curriculum Director reported the committee has begun its
work by reviewing reports on the 2011-2012 calendar, what worked, what didn't and what needs
to be achieved next year. The absence of early release days provided more learning days for
students. The committee has developed four calendar scenarios for 2012-2013. Options | and 2
have no budget impact, and simply have different dates. Version 3 has an additional flex day
added to provide structured, recurring professional development sessions of 1.5 hours. Version 4
proposes a professional development summer institute using two additional days. The committee
will meet again on February 1* to consider Board feedback and develop final options for
presentation to the Board on February 8". After this meeting, public feedback will be collected
on final options until March 5". A special effort will be made to ensure students have had the
opportunity to make suggestions. First Read on the calendar is anticipated for April 11" and final
adoption on April 25".

B. 3-5 Year Financial Analysis — Kelly Wentworth, Finance Director reported the Finance
Committee has completed the updating of the 3-5 Year Financial Plan. Valuation and
enrollments are two major factors in determining the level of funding received by the State.

Enrollments are projected to remain relatively stable in upcoming years and the towns are

C. Special Education December 1 Count — Dominic DePatsy, Director of Instructional Support
reported the December 1* Childcount in Special Education is used for the benchmark for EPS
(Essential Program and Services) to calculate subsidy for special education for all school
districts. RSUS is at 15% identification rate which is the Department of Education's benchmark
for the targeted identification rate. The Special Education count is at 281 which does not include
students from the eight private schools or the six Brunswick High School Students. There are 49
total 504 students. This is a broad disability law, typically medical disorders. RSUS has 18
English Language Learners and 104 Gifted and Talented Students. The Gifted and Talented
application and budget were recently accepted by the DOE and we are at 5% enrollment for
gifted and talented students, which is also the Department of Education's benchmark. The GT
Advisory Committee is working on the revision to the academic and visual arts plan for the
district.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Board Goals Update - Superintendent Welsh provided a mid-year update on the Board's goals
for 2011-2012. We have met our goal area of effective communication to include the following:
Board representatives have attended/are planning to attend parent group and municipal officers'
meetings to establish communication relationships to keep people informed; the Superintendent
has scheduled meetings with parent groups in March as an open discussion regarding education
and the budget; and, the Board held informal socials in each of the three towns to provide an
opportunity for the public to speak with members of the Board. A second round of meetings in
each town has been scheduled for April budget discussions. Goal two, to provide Board training
and opportunities for members to develop and reinforce the skills required for effective policy-
making, budget planning, and communication has been accomplished as follows: new Board
members participated in orientation meetings and assigned a mentor; and, several Board
members attended the MSMA annual conference in October. Eric Dube reported that the Board
will step back from approaching each municipality on moving to a common election date for
RSU elections. In researching this issue it was determined each town would also have to change
their municipal elections dates. More research will be conducted. Goal three is to provide
leadership for securing support for the district budget with a specific plan for explaining and
defending the budget to the public. Progress on this goal is ongoing with workshops being held
that focused on programs and needs that may impact the 2012-2013 budget. Budget Priorities
will be established in February and meetings with municipality governance boards will take
place later this winter/spring.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on December 14, 2011 to discuss the updates
to the 3-5 Year Financial Plan. The committee felt that the percentage of increase between the
years could be reduced from 3% each year to 1%, 1.5% and 1.5%. Revisions were made to the
plan. Warrants were signed. The committee met again on January 11, 2012 to discuss the 3-5
Year Financial Plan. Discussion focused on the following key points: valuations and
enrollments are expected to remain relatively flat over the next three years which allows State
subsidy amounts to remain relatively flat as long as the State continues to fund education at
current levels. Once the funding level for Capital Projects is raised to the targeted $400,000 and
high school choice expires both in 2012-2013 the budget should remain stable. Any other
increases would be program changes. Warrants were also signed.

B. Negotiations — Peter Murray reported the group continues to meet but had a respite from
meetings the past two weeks due to mid-terms. Meetings continue tomorrow night and starting
next week two meetings a week for the next several weeks.

C. Facilities - Peter Murray reported the FHS Facilities Committee has been undertaking a study
of the building, fields, parking — the entire high school facility. The Committee has been
working with Portland Design Team and has been gathering data on the school. The committee
is moving through the process of holding public meetings with the three communities and town
leaders. A website has been established with up-to-date information including a video
presentation of the architects preliminary report. We encourage people to provide input and ask
questions about the project.
