3. ADJUSTMENTS TO THE AGENDA:
HINI1 Report — Joan Nason, Director of Student Services, provided the Board and public with a
time-line pertaining to the H1N1 vaccine and the clinics. Joan discussed the make up of the
Planning Committee that was formed, training that occurred and meetings that were held. On
September 8'" RSU No. 5 officially partnered with Freeport Medical Center to obtain vaccine doses.
Joan also explained the Planning Committee met on October 1*' and discussed the concern about
using the HINI Mist. The HIN1 Mist arrived on October 5". The Planning Committee met on
October 7" and at that meeting it was decided to defer the batch of HIN1 Mist and wait for the
injectable. This decision was made after taking into account that area schools were also not
accepting the HIN1 Mist, CHANS suggested the Planning Committee wait for the injectable and the
school physician, Dr. Lockwood, also said to wait for the HIN1 injectable. On October 15" the
seasonal vaccine arrived and at that time Dr. Lockwood reconsidered the decision about the mist and
clinics were scheduled for October 28, 29 and November 5" These clinics included seasonal flu
vaccine and 192 does of H1N1 vaccine. Joan and the Planning Committee's focus during this
process was the protection of our children from the flu as fast and as safely as possibly. Both
Superintendent Welsh and Joan apologized to the Board if there was any miscommunication or lack
of communication to the Board and public.
Nicky Knighton, Practice Manager of Freeport Medical Center (FMC), stated FMC's objective is to
vaccinate the children and the community. Nicky and Julie Gray did not agree with the Planning
Committee's decision not to use the doses of HIN1 mist. Since the school did not want the HIN1
nasal mist, school vaccines were returned to the state. FMC decided to hold public clinics using
HINI1 nasal mist attained by FMC for this clearly expressed purpose. All vaccinations were obtained
using FMC's pin number. FMC appropriately vaccinated patients at their public clinics. FMC
vaccinated target groups according to CDC guidelines and adjusted vaccination administration as the
guidelines became more focused. When the school changed their mind about using nasal mist,
Freeport Medical Center gave to the school FMC's excess supply for school clinics.
Julie Gray, Physician's Assistant for Freeport Medical Center, stated she wanted the public to know
that Freeport Medical Center is committed to protecting our children.
During the discussion over the HIN1 Flu Clinics there was conversation about the need to review
our policies for committee formation and communication to the Board on action items that was taken
from the committees.

4. WORKSHOP:
Great Schools Partnership presented Version 2.0 of the draft Strategic Plan to the Board.
Superintendent Welsh reported she held meetings with staff and students to obtain feedback on
Version 1.0 of the draft plan during the past two weeks. Community members were also asked to
provide feedback via an on-line survey. Taking that feedback into account, Great Schools revised
the Plan to come up with Version 2.0. The Plan now includes an Introduction and Background and a
District Vision and Mission Statement. Board members reviewed the draft Plan and made additional
suggestions for changes. One change will be the addition of a paragraph under Section IV Financial
Efficiencies explaining the Finance Sub-Committee will be involved in the process of coming up
with indicators. This work will take place December through January.
The next step in this important process is to gather additional input from the staff and citizens from
the three communities. A Community Forum will be held as part of the November 18" Board
Meeting for staff and citizens to respond to the newly revised Plan. Formal adoption of the full
Strategic Plan is anticipated for December 9th. An invitation to the November 18" forum will be
mailed to each home in the RSU during the week of November 9". Version 3.0 of the Plan will be
posted on the website, distributed to each Town Hall and will also be available at the Central Office
by Monday, November 9".
