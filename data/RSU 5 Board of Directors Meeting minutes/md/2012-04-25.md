6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Durham Community School Positive Behavioral Supports — Ryan Gleason, Assistant Principal
reported Durham Community School participated in the Maine Youth Voices On Stage Program
sponsored by the Children's Museum and the Theater of Maine. The program uses current
research and children's own words to create a solution-based model to help make schools safer,
happier and the kind of places to learn. The program focused on using improve theater to educate
and motivate students. The program included one on-site training day and one at school. Student
Ambassadors Sarah Howard and Jefferson Lane reported on survey results, their experience in
the program and what they learned about positive behavior supports.

7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Hunter Road Field Maintenance and Scheduling Proposals — Directors Dennis Ouellette and
Russell Packett reported the Town of Freeport has asked the RSU to submit proposals for the
cost of maintaining and scheduling the Hunter Road Fields. Initial meetings have already taken
place between representative from the RSU and the Town leadership. The proposal for the
scheduling of the fields was at a cost of $15,000, which included purchase of the software
module and labor. The maintenance proposal is a three year contract, $80,644 for the first year.
This proposal includes the Town of Freeport purchasing equipment and gives RSUS ownership
of the equipment. The proposal also includes additional field staff. The Town Council will
discuss the proposals at their meeting on May 1”.

date. The budget was presented to the public on March 27" and March 28". Two documents are
available, the budget itself and a packet of more detailed information such as enrollments,
priorities, reserve funds, tax impact to each town. The proposed budget has a net increase of

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on April 11". The RSU had been approached
by leaders of the Town of Freeport to submit two proposals for the scheduling and maintenance
of the town owned Hunter Road fields. The committee reviewed the two proposals, these are the
same proposals the Board heard about this evening. The Town Council will discuss the
proposals at their meeting on May 1. The committee reflected on the March 14" RSUS
member communities meeting and decided they would like to try to schedule meetings with
community leaders twice per year, one in the fall and one in the spring. The committee also
approved and signed a transfer warrant for librarian salary, bills for the FHS Master Plan and
music/drama related items. Administrator's budget priorities were reviewed. These items
would help accelerate the incremental steps that are being taken to reach our goal of providing a
world-class education to our students. A payroll warrant was signed.

B. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported the teams met last evening and excellent progress is
being made. The next session is scheduled for 7:30 a.m. Saturday morning and we are
cautiously optimistic the work can be finished on Saturday.

C. Facilities — Peter Murray reported Lyndon Keck is working on completing the final draft of the
Master Plan. The Board will hear an interim plan in two weeks and a final report at the end of
May. The committee is schedule to meet tomorrow at 4:00 p.m. at Freeport High School.
