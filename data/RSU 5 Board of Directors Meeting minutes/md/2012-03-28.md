feedback from principals on how flex-time has been used, and a breakout of teacher responses to

the feedback survey. The committee came up with three additional options for the Board to

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Professional Growth and Evaluation System — Superintendent Welsh provide a brief update on
the work of the committee. The Board formed the committee in August. Durham, Freeport and
Pownal all have different evaluation tools. The goal is to have one evaluation tool across the
RSU. Good progress is being made. The committee is looking at National Board Standards. The
Governor has proposed a bill on teacher evaluation with a timeline of 2015-2016. A full report
will be provided to the Board at the end of April.

11. UNFINISHED BUSINESS:

A. Budget FY'13 — Chair Larkins reported tonight is an opportunity to further discuss the
recommended FY' 13 budget that was presented last evening.

The Board asked what items were not added to the budget. Superintend Welsh reported the
following: Professional development time and money for training; technology funds and
curriculum materials; literacy (middle level decrease); Ed Tech hours; leadership stipends for
curriculum teams; special education at the middle level at Durham Community School; and
equipment items such as security cameras. Superintendent Welsh also reported this budget does
provide a strong program for our students and moves us forward by taking incremental steps.
Continued budget deliberations will occur over the next two months ending May 23" with a
three-town Annual Budget Meeting followed by a Budget Validation Referendum on June 12".
All citizens are encouraged to participate in the process.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on March 14, 2012. Kelly Wentworth
reviewed the audited financial statements. The auditors and Kelly had spent extra time this year
reviewing the loss in Adult and Community Education. Recommendations have been designed
and implemented to improve the financial reporting and health of this department. The Finance
Committee then reviewed the amount of undesignated fund balance followed by a discussion of
how much of those funds had been dedicated to reduce the impact of the 2011-2012 budget as
well as how much the current funds would be dedicated to help reduce the impact of the 2012-
2013 proposed budget. The committee also discussed the meeting with the municipal leaders
held on March 14". Kelly presented tax impact summaries to the committee. The summaries
included the impact on taxpayers for the initial budget requests of the Administrators, the
proposed Superintendent's request, and the 3-5 year plan impact. Warrants were signed.

B. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported negotiations are ongoing on the educators contract and
thanked the educators. The teachers have been fantastic and everyone professional during this
long, tough process. There are really good people involved in the process. The next session is
scheduled for tomorrow night.

C. Strategic Communications — Peter Murray reported the State of the Schools report has gone out
in the mail to all homes in the RSU. This is the annual report on our progress and encouraged
everyone to read it. The Education Forum is scheduled for April 2" at 6:30 p.m. in the FPAC.
Commissioner of Education Bowen will provide the opening talk about his vision for education
in the state. A dialogue with the community will take place after the presentations.

D. Facilities - Peter Murray reported Lyndon Keck presented to the Board two meetings ago.
Meetings have been held with Town Council/Selectmen in all three towns as well as public
meetings in Durham, Freeport and Pownal. Mr. Keck is currently developing a price tag for
each module and the benefits of each. The next committee meeting is scheduled for April 5,
2012. A final report will be provided to the Board in late April early May.
