5. ADJUSTMENTS TO THE AGENDA:
6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government: Brina Dillon and Mikaela Gillis reported on the following:
Rotary Interact is a new club this year; Freshman elections for President, Vice-President,
Secretary and Student Council are taking place; Spirit Day was a huge success with lots of
activities followed by sports kick-off weekend; first day of school was only for freshmen who
were matched up with upper classman mentors; there is in increase in the number of students this
year; and everyone seems to be happy to be back.

7. REPORTS FROM DIRECTORS:

A. Russell Packett, Director of Recreation and Community Education, reported on the services his
department provides including: adult education; youth recreation; The Port Teen Center; Laugh
and Learn child care; and scheduling district facility and field use. Russell also reported a grant
of $5,000 has been received from the Barbara Bush Foundation with a focus on preschool
literacy, family literacy and intergenerational activity.

B. Dennis Ouellette, Director of Facilities and Transportation, reported the district has benefited
from the old Durham Elementary School by recycling lights, ceiling tiles, and furniture. Over
1,000 maintenance work orders have been completed since July 1°. Dennis also reported on the
transportation run adjustments due to the change in school hours at Morse Street School and
Mast Landing School. Newer buses are now being used for trips furthest away.

C. Judy Goodenow, Director of School Nutrition, reported the first days of schools have been hectic
with the new kitchen in Durham and the increase in student lunches at the high school. At the
High School 420 meals a day are being served between breakfast and lunch. The Free and
Reduced application approval process has been changed this year. The Nutrition Program has
also benefited from equipment at the old Durham Elementary School.

D. Carrie Thurston, Director of Student Services, reported things in the student services area are
moving forward. We have an increase in IEP's and students with needs. The Pownal PreK-2
program is off to a good start. The Gifted and Talented team has met and will meet with an
advisory group to help frame the GT framework. The GT handbook is in draft form.

E. Kelly Wentworth, Director of Finance and Human Resources, reported she is still working part-
time for the Town of Pownal and is looking forward to start working full-time for the RSU on
October 1“. Kelly reported the finance report will be changed to report by Article. The auditors
are in this week to work on the 2009-2010 audit.

F. Craig Sickels, Athletic Director, reported on the June 8" Grade Athletic Orientation and Fall
Coaches Summit held August 24'". Sports Kick-Off weekend was held September 10" and 11"
with 350 people in attendance. Coastal Orthopedic will donate 14 days this fall for athletic
training services. Craig provided an update on the Pownal Road Field purchase & sales
agreement. The RSU 5 Recreational & Athletic Facilities Advisory Committee membership has
been finalized. The committee has met once and plans to meet again on October 29".

G. Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, reported it was a very busy summer with professional
learning activities. The major component was the Summer Literacy Institute that 19 staff
members attended. There was also a two day Every Day Math Training and two day new teacher
induction. The first early release of the school year was last Wednesday.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Strategic Plan Update — Superintendent Welsh reported the October 8" Professional Learning
Day will be the kick-off of the Strategic Plan. We will walk through the plan then staff will
return back to their buildings with three questions: 1) What does this mean for your building; 2)
What does this mean for your team; 3) What does this mean for you as an individual. At the end
of the day we will have three target sets for each building. In March we will have data from the
first 6 months, and by May a plan for a full year.

B. Substitutes — Superintendent Welsh reported the district has had three employees working as
permanent substitutes to fill in when teachers were absent. Over the summer two moved into
different positions within the district and one resigned. The Administrative team would like to
replace two of the positions to work PreK-5 and 6-12. The funds from the third position would
be used for a Substitute and Community Resource Coordinator to call/assign substitutes;
organize/train volunteers for the district; write/coordinate grant and public relations efforts for
the district.
