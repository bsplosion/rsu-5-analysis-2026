6. WORKSHOP:

A. Use of Time Update — Joe Makley, Curriculum Director reported the committee met on January 2"
and 30", 2013 to develop initial recommendations for the 2013-2014 school calendar. The
committee discussed the following: the urgent need for concentrated professional development time
to support improvement initiatives; a specific request from educators to reconsider starting in
August; an interest in eliminating the short week after the New Year holiday; an interest in
considering a switch from the current one flex day to a scheduled Professional Learning Day;
requests to avoid the months of December, May and June for Professional Learning Days; and a
new state initiative requiring formal cooperation with the CTE Region. Mr. Makley stressed the
need for professional learning time cannot be overstated. The RSU Strategic Framework outlines a
number of significant changes including a transition to a standards-based system and school-based
improvement plans.

Four calendar options were presented, all are cost neutral. Option | provides an after Labor Day
start, with two PLD's in August, one in October, one in January, and one in March. Option 2
provides an August start, and two PLD's in August, one in October, one in January and one in
March. Option 3 is similar to option 2, but changes the current flex-day to a scheduled PLD in
January. Option 4 is similar to option 1, but changes the current flex-day to a scheduled PLD in
January. Lisa Demick, Principal of Pownal Elementary School and a member of the committee,
explained a few of the reasons teachers are asking for a before Labor Day start including: additional

B. Board Goals Update ~ Superintendent Welsh provided a mid-year update on the Board's goals for
2012-2013. Goal one, Effective Communication: In October the Board hosted a meeting with
governance members from the three communities along with members of the public; a social gather
was held in each town to provide an opportunity for the pubic to speak with members of the Board;
a second round of meetings will be held in each town during budget season; Dine and Discuss the
State of the Schools sessions will be held prior to each of the second round of meetings in each
town; and meetings with parent groups for February and March have been scheduled for open
discussions regarding education and the budget. Goal two, Freeport High School Facility: the
Board approved the expenditure of $40,000 for development of a Concept Design for
improvements to the high school facility; in January, the Board held a workshop on the committee's
recommendations and voted to send the project to a referendum vote; and the Board authorized
$10,000 to fund educational programs and materials to ensure the citizens are informed in advance
of the June vote. Goal three, Financial: workshops were held over the past several months that
focus on programs and needs that will impact the 2013-2014 budget; budget priorities were

established in January; the Board budget meeting schedule has been structure to clearly
communicate opportunities for public comment and deliberations; and efforts are being made to
encourage public participation at the Annual Budget Meeting.
