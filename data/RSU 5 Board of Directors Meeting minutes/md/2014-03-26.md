A. FPAC Update — David Watts provided an update on the FPAC including: 337 calendar events

have been changed to LED and we need to start thinking about replacing bigger items.

mailed to all homes in the RSU. The report provides information on how the students are
performing in testing such as NECAP, MHSA, and NWEA compared to the state and other
districts. Both the NECAP and SAT will be replaced by the new Smarter Balanced Assessment

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Strategic Communications — Candy Decsipkes reported the committee met on March 6" to
review the State of the Schools report that Joe Makley reported on earlier tonight. Dine and
Discuss sessions will be held on April 9" in Pownal, April 30" in Durham and
May 7" in Freeport

B. Finance — John Morang reported the Finance Director presented and reviewed the 2012-2013
financial audit. There were no material weaknesses or significant deficiencies that were
identified. Once again there were a few suggestions from the Auditor for improvements within
the Business Office and schools that are currently being implemented. The school nutrition
program has a budget shortage due to new federal regulations including price increases and
healthier choices. The committee also discussed the undesignated fund balance. Warrants were
signed.

C. Region Ten Technical High School Cooperative Board — No report given.

D. Withdrawal Working Group — Michelle Ritcheson reported the group continues to meet on
Monday nights. They met with Durham and Pownal Advisory Committees on March 24", A
joint meeting with the Freeport Withdrawal Committee was held on March 19". The Working
Group met with the attorney last week. The next meeting of the Working Group will be held on
March 31°.
