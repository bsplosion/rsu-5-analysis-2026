5. ADJUSTMENTS TO THE AGENDA:
6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Sheri Gonye, Mast Landing School Guidance Counselor talked about the Aspirations Grant that
matches At-Risk 3", 4", 5" grade students with a trusted adult or teenage mentor. The grant also
allows three college trips. The students recently visited Bowdoin College and will visit USM
and SMCC.

B. Dede Bennell, Service Learning/Aspirations Coordinator, spoke about the service learning taking
place at the High School. Students from the high school spoke of the projects they are working
on including: sending supplies to students affected by Hurricane Sandy; work with Brunswick
animal shelter on spay and neuter clinic; dinner fundraiser for Darfur; partnership with the
French School in Freeport; and working with Acadia Publishing on a Local Legendary Past and
Present book. Dede also spoke about the Aspirations Partnership and that eight grants were
awarded this year.

7. REPORTS FROM Superintendent AND ADMINISTRATORS: CURRENT AND

PROJECTED ENROLLMENTS AND BUDGET PRIORITIES

A. Current and Projected Enrollments: Superintendent Welsh provided an overview of the current
and projected student enrollments. We have a 3% increase district wide from what was
anticipated from last year due to families moving into town. We projected 1874 students and on
October 1“ enrollment was at 1,933. The increase was unexpected and a teacher needed to be
added at Mast Landing School in late August. Enrollments for next year will remain relatively
stable with a projected increase of 5 students district wide. There will be 27 Durham tuition
students grades 10-12 as this is the last year for High school choice.

Bob Strong, Freeport High School Principal reported his wish list for next year includes:
additional foreign language; expansion of the math center; computer science classes; more
science staffing; in-school suspension monitor; social worker; and better technology for STEM
classes.
Ray Grogan, Freeport Middle School Principal reported enrollment at the school is up over 300
and needs for next year include: functional life skills class; new 6™ grade laptops, projection and
hardware; part-time Unified Arts increase; and administrative help for day to day activities and
discipline.

Will Pidden, Durham Community School Principal reported staffing for next year will depend on

Pownal student's decision for 6" grade. Needs for next year include: support around special

education students; and math support.

Lisa Demick, Pownal Elementary School Principal reported 15 new students enrolled in school

over the summer. Needs for next year include: literacy support; special education support; and

additional time for staff that are shared with other buildings.

Beth Willhoite, Mast Landing School Principal reported needs for next year include: special

education staffing; additional classroom teacher, classes are at 23; and math enrichment.

Tom Ambrose, Morse Street School Principal reported on special education and behavioral

needs for next year; the need for math enrichment and remediation; more professional

development; and technology in the classrooms (iPads, projectors, sound system).

Superintendent Welsh reported on the draft list of budget priorities for the 2013-2014 school

year. Administration has begun the process of developing a budget to present to the Board.

Administrators will meet with teachers and staff members to gather budget information.

Administrators will then met individually with the Superintendent and Finance Director on their

proposed budget. Administrators will then come together as a team to craft a budget to bring to

the Board on March 26" and March 27". The proposed priority areas include the following:

* Enrollments/Instructional Programs/Student Supports - RSU5 has seen a 3% increase in
enrollment this school year. Our goal is to continue to target class sizes of 20 students per
teacher and consider related services time to address the increased enrollment. Academic
support for gifted students and those who are struggling to learn are areas that need to be
addressed. There is also a need to develop new programs to support our students with special
needs. Increase math strategist support across the district for training and intervention.

* Professional Development Time and Training — Professional development time is a vital
support for educators as we continue to improve and expand our curriculum and program
offerings to students. Our goal is to fund Summer Institute trainings and an additional
Professional Learning Day for the 13-14 school year.

* Technology Investment — There is a need for increased funds this year for student technology
equipment linked to increased enrollments and new MLTI devices for grades 6-12.

* Capital Investments — This year there continues to be a major focus on capital investments.
The Capital Plan recommendations provide funds to support on-going improvements in all of
our 7 facilities and our vehicles.
