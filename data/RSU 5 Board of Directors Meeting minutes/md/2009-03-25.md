6. PUBLIC COMMENTS:
Ms. Lois Kilby-Chesley, President of the FEA and a Durham resident, asked for clarification on the
process the Superintendent Search Committee followed. Clarification questions asked included:
what was the make-up of the search committee; how were votes by the search committee counted;
were second interviews conducted; was all information held confidential; did the Search Committee
came up with a recommendation to bring to the full Board; will Board votes be weighted. The
weighted vote system was explained to Ms. Kilby-Chesley.
7. OLD BUSINESS:
None
8. NEW BUSINESS:
A. Policy development training
Cori Holt, Chair of the Policy Sub-Committee, and Superintendents Tomaszewski and Welsh
reviewed the role the Board plays in policy making; what a policy is; why policies are needed;
and the procedure to be used for policy development. A demonstration on how to navigate the
policy section on the rsu5.org website was provided.

9. COMMUNICATIONS (Consent Agenda)
None
10. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications: Betsy Peters reported the Communications Sub-Committee met on March 23™
with the Superintendents and Jeff Brazee, Freeport's Technology Systems Director. The
committee discussed website changes and processes. The goal of the website between now and
July 1 is to communicate preparations for consolidation. The committee wants to be pro-active
by posting upcoming meeting agendas, having transparency and community involvement. The
committee will meet again on April 9".

B. Finance: John Morang reported the Finance Sub-Committee met on March 19" and 25" with
the two Superintendents and two Business Managers . On the 19" the committee reviewed
expenses and discussed the upcoming budget presentation. On the 25" the committee discussed
the budget format. No decisions were made. The School Department is still waiting for numbers
from the state.

C. Policy: Cori Holt reported the Policy Sub-Committee met on March 13" with the two
Superintendents. Policy review is an on-going process. There are fifty-three state mandated
policies to be in place by July 1°.
