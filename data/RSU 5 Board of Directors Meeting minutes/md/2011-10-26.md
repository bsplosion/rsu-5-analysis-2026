7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS: OPENING OF SCHOOL

A. Joe Makley, Curriculum Director reviewed work that has been taking place, including: training
on Readers Workshop by Principals; K-8 EveryDay Math training; integration of technology
with one Student Information System; digital citizenship; teacher web pages; implementation of
Fountas and Pinnell; Work and College Readiness review; planning for March 7" professional
development day.

B. Ryan Gleason, Assistant Principal reported for Craig Sickels on the following: fall sports teams
have done exceptionally well this season with soccer and football making the playoffs; boys and
girls cross country qualified for the State competition which will be held Saturday; field hockey
lost in the quarter-finals; winter season begins on November 14" for middle school; DES fall
participation was 64%; FMS fall participation was 60%; FHS fall participation was at 43%; and
Campus Complex Committee has met to discuss public relations going forward.

C. Dominic DePatsy, Director of Instructional Support reported on the following: the department is
made up of five areas, RTI, Special Education, Gifted and Talented, ELL and 504; work is
broken down into two areas, procedural and programming; purchased Gem Care software to

F. Judy Goodenow, Director of School Nutrition, reported on the following: the year is off to a
good start; the increase in price for elementary lunch has not affected participation;
Free/Reduced participation is up 2% to 23%; new kitchen equipment is working nicely; and new
Federal regulations required one person in each kitchen to attend training over the summer.

G. Kelly Wentworth, Director of Finance and Human Resources reported on the following:
successfully adjusted staff's pay as per negotiations; encumbered all payroll so it is reflected in
financial statements; caught up with all new hires and staff transfers; implemented AESOP, an
automated absence and substitute calling software; accounts payable paying bills bi-weekly; and
cross training taking place in the business department.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:
Superintendent Welsh reported this is the week of the Maine School Superintendents and Maine
School Board's conferences. At the Superintendent's Conference today Commissioner of
Education Stephen Bowen provided a brief overview of the State's Strategic Plan. There is a
strong standards based focus at the State level and across the Nation. The Commissioner also
talked about the Common Core Standards and the importance of having a common standard
across the Nation so when looking at student performance we are comparing apples to apples.
The Common Core Standards are in Math and ELA. Our current assessments, NECAP and
SAT's, will change in two years.

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees): —

A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on October 12, 2011. Kelly Wentworth
discussed the need for transfers between Article 6 (contingency) and the remaining articles for
changes in benefits and wages negotiated after the budget was passed. The Board of Directors
voted at their meeting on October 12" to authorize the Finance Committee to make the transfers
of up to 5% of the total budget between lines for the 2012 fiscal year. Kelly also reviewed the
Capital Facility Plan that Director Ouellette presented to the Board on October 12". Kelly
presented the third draft of the 3-5 year financial plan to the committee. It was suggested to
update 2011-2012 budget information with the transfers discussed earlier regarding wage and
benefits negotiated after the budget was passed. Eric Dube requested during the budget process
there is a review with the communities of the various reserve funds that are now being utilized
to help stabilize the budget. Reports should include capital reserve funds, fuel reserve fund, and
undesignated fund balances. Warrants were signed.

B. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported Peter Murray has been working on finance
subcommittee work for negotiations. Next session of negotiations is set for Saturday,
November 5".

C. Strategic Communications — Jane Blais reported the committee met on October 6, 2011 and
discussed the timeline for creation and publication of the second annual State of the Schools
Report. The annual Forum has been scheduled for April 2, 2012 and discussion was held
regarding whether to have someone of Angus King's caliber speak on national and international
educational initiatives. The committee also discussed the possibility of student presentations.
After the more global presentation, citizens would participate in focus group discussions
regarding action that might be taken as a result of the Report, the Strategic Plan, and the
presentation. A pre-forum dinner that would benefit a group such as Project Graduation was
also discussed.

The committee discussed several communication efforts. The first one is to contact parent
groups and municipal officers at the beginning of the school year and as needed throughout the
year. Committee members Candy Decsipkes and Jane Blais attended Durham's PTA meeting.
The second element is to work toward a common municipal election date for the RSU Board.
Committee members agreed to ask municipalities to hold a June election.
