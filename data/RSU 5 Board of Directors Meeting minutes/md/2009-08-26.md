5. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Bob Strong — Opening of School: Principal Strong noted today was the first teacher day. The
high school enrollment is currently 432 which is an increase of 11 students from last year. The
high school goals for the year include: setting up Professional Learning Groups; UBD mapping;
and Technology, using laptops they way they were designed to be used.

B. Ray Grogan — Opening of School: Principal Grogan reported today was his fifth day at Freeport
Middle School. He reported the middle school goals for the year include: increasing Literacy in
the content areas starting with Social Studies and Science; UBD; interdisciplinary work; and
bullying. He explained the change from an Industrial Technology teacher to a STEM (Science,
Technology, Engineering & Mathematics) teacher.

C. Will Pidden — Opening of School: Principal Pidden thanked the Board for their support of the
Summer Institute. Teachers in grades K-5 worked with a Maine Math and Science
representative to develop a deeper understanding of the role of science language for children.
Worked on a comprehensive approach to literacy in grades 6-8. Portfolio teachers 6-8 worked
on student expectations. Mr. Gleason led the school culture action team to work on strategies for
a safe learning environment for the school community. Mr. Pidden reported student enrollment is
at 380, they ended the year at 349.

D. Beth Willhoite — Opening of School: Principal Willhoite reported the student enrollment is at
260, up by 11 students from last year. The priorities for the year include: putting an RTI system
into place; fluency and comprehension in reading; technology; health and wellness of staff and
students; and Every Day Math. Meetings with staff from each program to discuss the change in
leadership at the school were held today.

E. Peter Buckley — Opening of School: Principal Buckley reported teachers attended Every Day
Math today. He spoke about the team teaching changes taking place at the school. The teams will
be 5/6 and 7/8. Principal Buckley also mentioned he will be teaching an Algebra I class. The
focus for the year will include: technology; RTI; Every Day Math; and bullying. The student
enrollment is at 140.

F. Cheryl White — Opening of School: Principal White reported the student enrollment is at 240.
The goals for the year include: technology; Every Day Math; RTI; and Literacy and Math block
scheduling.

G. Jeff Brazee — Laptop Use and Insurance: Director of Technology Jeff Brazee reported the
district has 900 new machines. He clarified that the middle school laptops were provided by
MLTI and the district paid for 500 machines. Mr. Brazee discussed the student and staff laptop
insurance coverage. The district is looking at a self-insurance model.

6. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Celebration of First Days of Schools: Superintendent Welsh reported the opening staff day is
Thursday, August 27" starting at 7:30AM with a breakfast in the high school cafeteria. The
Superintendent and Chair Larkins will welcome all staff in the Performing Arts Center to
celebrate coming together as an RSU. We want to celebrate what we've done and share our
strengths but also talk about how do we get better. Superintendent Welsh invited all Board
members to the celebration.

B. Strategic Plan Forum: Superintendent Welsh reported on the September 14'" Community Forum.
A flyer will be mailed out to all three communities with detailed information about the forum
and an invitation to attend. Staff members will receive an invitation on the opening staff day.
Former Governor Angus King will lead the evening with a presentation on learning needs for the
21* Century. Grant funds are being used to work with Great Schools Partnership. Break out
sessions will then respond to the presentation to identify characteristics and set high level three-
year targets for student performance. Great Schools will compile the data, collate information
and we will create three-year PreK-12 targets for student performance; annual indicators that
measure progress in achieving targets; and three-year PreK-12 district strategic plan. At the end
of October another forum will be held for feedback on the plan.

C. Pownal Road Fields Update: Superintendent Welsh reported the Finance Subcommittee met
with Town Council leadership. On September 8" the Council will hear a presentation of the
field study report. It was decided a stakeholder group will be formed with the charge of
considering all community use and submit a proposal by mid-January to ensure all needs are met.
The Board is invited to the September 8" Council meeting to hear the report.

D. Workplan for 2009-2010: Superintendent Welsh reported throughout the spring and summer we
heard many issues from community members and staff so a timeline for dealing with those issues
was created. An example of the issues is a 3-5 year financial plan with a timeline of work to be
completed August — November. A meeting has taken place with the financial staff at the Department
of Education who are pulling together data. The purpose of the workplan is to show we have heard
the concerns and even though we can't get to every issue immediately there is a plan for working on
all issues. The work plan will be posted on the RSU website.
