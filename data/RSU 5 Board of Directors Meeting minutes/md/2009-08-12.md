8. PERSONNEL:

A. Voted: (5) To employ Raymond Grogan as Principal of Freeport Middle School for the 2009-
2010 school year. (Parker — Poissonnier) (9 — 0)
Superintendent Welsh explained the hiring process. The position was advertised; parents and
staff were invited to participate in the interview process; the 12 member interview committee
met to review applications and create a list of characteristics needed in a Principal; five
candidates were interviewed; two candidates were invited back for a second interview with two
Board members, a teacher and the Superintendent; references checked; final meeting with the
top candidate and Superintendent. The Superintendent and the candidate reviewed CBEA
salary comparables and looked to move closer to other Cumberland County salaries.
Superintendent Welsh introduced Raymond Grogan. Mr. Grogan has been at Sanford Middle
School for the past thirteen years, the last two as Assistant Principal. He has been released from
his contract in Sanford and will be able to begin soon. Mr. Grogan told the Board he
appreciates this opportunity and is very excited to be at Freeport Middle School.

B. Voted: (6) To employ Beth Willhoite as a one-year Interim Principal at Mast Landing School
for the 2009-2010 school year. (Morang — Parker) (9 — 0)
Superintendent Welsh explained when Holly Couturier gave her resignation and accepted a full
time Principal position in Auburn she spoke with both Beth Willhoite and Holly Couturier about
the best strategy for filling the position. Ms. Couturier immediately suggested Ms. Willhoite
take over as full time Principal. Some of the concerns include trying to hire a Teaching-
Principal in August and Ms. Willhoite teaches in the morning and it would be hard to mentor a
new Teaching-Principal. Superintendent Welsh then contacted the Board, parents and staff
about the possibility of going to a full time Principal. Superintendent Welsh received four
responses from parents, three said to go for it and one suggested doing away with the three
programs. Feedback from one staff member was concern about losing the three programs.
Superintendent Welsh and Beth Willhoite met to review salary comparables
Superintendent Welsh noted a few years ago there was a Mast Landing governance study done.
Parents indicated they wanted the three separate programs but also wanted the kids to be
together. Two years ago Beth Willhoite took over Teaching-Principal duties for the Soule and
Harraseeket Programs which helped with the effort to make connections between students and
staff. Superintendent Welsh and Ms. Willhoite have looked at performance indicators and the
possibility of a survey for feedback on how the model of a full time Principal is working. The
Board will need to decide by February.

10. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications: Superintendent Welsh reported the recommendation from the
Communications Sub-Committee and the Strategic Planning Committee is to shift the focus of
the Future Search to a focus on a three-year Strategic Plan with performance indicators. The
Great Schools Partnership would facilitate the process for the community and Board
development of the Plan and indicators. Duke Albanese, Co-Executive Director (previous Maine
Commissioner of Education) and David Ruff, Co-Executive Director (graduate of Freeport High
School) would work closely with us. On September 14" from 6:00PM-9:00PM a Board and
Community forum will be held with a special guest speaker on learning needs for the 21“
Century. The group discussion would respond to the presentation and identify characteristics
and set high-level three-year targets for student performance. Out of that information we would
create three-year K-12 targets for student performance; annual indicators that measure profess in
achieving targets; and three-year K-12 district strategic plan. A second community forum for
feedback on the plan would be held at the end of October. At the end of the 2009-2010 school
year a forum/celebration to review indicators of the one-year strategic plan will be held. All
community members would be invited to attend the forums. The goal would be to bring
information to the Board at either the August 26” or September 9"" meeting before it goes public
on September 14".

C. Finance: Eric Dube reported the Finance Committee held meetings on July 21“ and August 4.
Warrants were reviewed and signed. We have not had to use our line of credit and do not
anticipate the need to do so. The committee discussed providing a financial report to the full
Board monthly. In September we will start to look at a 3-5 year capital plan for budget in
January. Pownal Road field deed is still being discussed. The Town of Freeport is working with
Seacoast Soccer on the possibility of selling the Pownal Road fields. There has been discussions
at the Town level that if they sell the fields money should be set aside for future school fields,
but the concern is there is no land to develop. A meeting between the Finance Sub-Committee
and Town leadership has been scheduled for Tuesday, August 18'" 7:30AM to discuss the
Pownal Road fields.

Superintendent Welsh explained she was approached by Seacoast to provide school usage of the
Pownal Road fields. There is confusion around the sale of the land and what our role is. Ms.
Welsh noted the Town paid $8,000 for a field study and she has met with the group that did the
study. The process of the sale of the land has been slowed down by the Town Council.

D. Policy: Melinda McKechnie reported the Policy Committee met on August 7". Additional
feedback from recently adopted policies was considered and those revisions will be included in
the first 10-01 packet for review. Members also reviewed and prepared B policies related to
functions of the Board. They also reviewed and have recommendations regarding substitute pay
for all schools in the RSU. A schedule for the year has been completed and given to the full
Board. The plan authorizes the use of existing policies until the RSU adopts replacement
policies. The policy work plan for the year provides for a review of all existing policies and
adoption of recommended RSU policies by the end of the 2009-2010 school year. Policy packet
10-01 will be distributed on August 26". The next meeting of the committee will be held on
September 18".
