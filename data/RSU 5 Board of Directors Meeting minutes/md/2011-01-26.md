B. Use of Time Update — Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, reported the Use of Time
Committee met on December 15" to begin their work on a calendar for 2011-2012. The
committee started by identifying the elements of the 2010-2011 calendar that worked well, and
those that were not as positive. The committee met again on January 10" to review nine
calendar proposals that were developed by the members of the committee. The committee then
developed two calendar options that were presented to the Board. Both calendar options
eliminate Early Release and Late Arrival times, thus adding student learning time back into the
calendar. Both proposals adjust for the elimination of the professional development time that was
created with Early Release and Late Arrival sessions, by reallocating professional learning time
into five full days for staff members. The cost of the five days is $236,500. The committee
strongly supports continuing to provide time for professional learning as it is critical if the
district is to make progress on the initiatives identified in the Strategic Framework. Members of
the committee spoke about the reasoning behind the two calendar options.

Board members felt they needed additional calendar options due to the cost of the two calendar

choices presented. The Board directed the committee to hold on sending out the calendars for

feedback and to bring two additional options back to the Board. The two additional calendars
should be a low cost calendar and no cost calendar options.

8. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Board Goals Update — Superintendent Welsh provided an update on the Board's goals for 2010-
2011. Great progress has been made on the goal area of effective communication to include the
following: Communication Subcommittee members attended parent group and municipal
officers' meetings to establish communication relationships to keep people informed; the
Superintendent has scheduled meetings with parent groups in February and March as an open
discussion regarding education and the budget; and, the Board held informal socials in each of
the three towns to provide an opportunity for the public to speak with members of the Board.
Goal two, to provide Board training and opportunities for members to develop and reinforce the
skills required for effective policy-making, budget planning, and communication has been
accomplished as follows: new Board members participated in orientation meetings and assigned
a mentor; and, several Board members attended the MSMA annual conference in October. Goal
three is to provide leadership for securing support for the district budget with a specific plan for
explaining and defending the budget to the public. Progress on this goal is ongoing with
workshops being held that focused on programs and needs that may impact the 2011-2012
budget. Board members will select a specific budget article and take primary responsibility for
responding to questions and explaining Board decision making regarding that budget article.

14. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — Chair Eric Dube reported the committee met on December 8, 2010 and January 19,
2011. At the December 8" meeting Athletic Director Craig Sickels presented a verbal summary
of the Recreation and Athletics Facilities and Field Use Committee's preliminary findings. The
full report was presented tonight. The entire project is estimated to cost approximately
$3,600,000. Tuition student enrollments were reported to remain flat for 2010-11. Durham
tuition students will decrease by approximately 20 in 2011-12. The draft budget calendar was
reviewed. It was reported the audit is still underway. Dennis Ouellette, Director of
Transportation and Facilities provided an update on the transportation analysis. He reported
two bids were received. Three options were considered. Option (1) Freeport local, Pownal
leased buses, and contracted Durham; one bid received from BoMar. Option (2) Freeport local,
Pownal and Durham contracted; no bids received. Option (3) Freeport, Pownal, and Durham all
contracted; one bid received from STA Transportation (Ledgemere). Kelly Wentworth and
Dennis worked on the analysis and at the January 19"" meeting they reported once the full
analysis of the bid was complete for a four year cycle the cost to contract would cost the district
an estimated additional $36,000 over the next four years. Field trips and a mid day bus run was
left out of the bid. This number takes into account the revenue the RSU would receive from the
sale of the 14 owned buses. The committee was in support of keeping the transportation
services currently offered. A report will be presented to the full Board at their workshop on
February 2, 2011. Also at the January 19'" meeting it was reported the auditors have been
granted an extension by the State of Maine to February 15, 2011 to complete the audit. The
audit is taking longer than expected due to the combining of the three towns into the RSU. The
committee reviewed a draft presentation of the graphs to be included in the State of the Schools
report. Warrants were signed at both meetings.
