Durham - +$17,852 1%

Freeport - +89,067 1%

Pownal — (-$10,212) -1%

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on April 13, 2011. The Audit is complete with
minor revisions being made to the final document. A summary was provided of undesignated
fund balance which detailed how the undesignated fund balance from FY 2009-2010 is being
utilized to offset taxation in 2010/2011 and proposed 2011/2012 fiscal years. The Auditors
recommend the district target at least $350,000 to $400,000 of undesignated fund balance on
hand for prudent financial health. The committee reviewed budget adjustments due to Region
10 Technical High School's assessment increase and the addition of the initial interest payment
on the Campus Complex Bond if passed by voters. With these adjustments the communities of
Durham and Freeport will see a 1% tax increase while Pownal remains flat.

B. Policy — Karin VanNostrand reported the committee met on April 14, 2011. Policy GCRD —
Private Tutoring for Pay. Previous Policy Chair Cori Holt recommended to the Board at their
meeting on March 23" that the policy language be changed to reflect the limitation of tutoring
current students or any students that may be entering the teacher's class within one year of
being tutored by that teacher. Teachers tutoring within their assigned school must receive prior
authorization from the building administrator for each student. The Policy Committee discussed
the language, agreed to make the revision and distribute the policy immediately with packet 11-
5 for 2"! Read/Adoption anticipated on April 27”.

Policy BEDI — Board of Directors Relations with the Media. At the Board meeting on March
23" the Board discussed adding language to this policy that would explicitly state that Board
members retain the right to speak as a private citizen on any matters. The Policy Committee
discussed the language, agreed to make the revision and distribute the policy immediately with
packet 11-5 for 2' Read/Adoption anticipated on April 27".

Policy BHC — Board of Directors Communications with Staff. Feedback on this policy
suggested that the opening paragraph reflect a philosophy of open discussion of educational
issues. The policy was revised to reflect this feedback and references were added to this policy
to direct staff and the public to additional policies regarding communication and the complaint
process. This revised policy will be distributed immediately with packet 11-5 for 2"
Read/Adoption anticipated on April 27".

The policy committee made the decision to add policy KEB — Complaints About School
Personnel to packet 11-6. This policy encourages complaints to be resolved closest to the
problem but describes the complaint process if issues are unresolved. Policy packet 11-6
received no other feedback and was finalized for 1‘ Read on April 27" with 2"! Read/Adoption
anticipated for May 27". Policy packed 11-6 is the final packet for the 2010-2011 school year.

C. Communications — Jane Blais reported the committee met on April 7, 2011. The committee
discussed the communication efforts to distribute the performance information related to the
strategic plan. Participants in the Education Forum were very positive about the opportunity to
discuss educational programs, performance and financial indicators. Although there was a
smaller turnout at the Education Forum, many citizens commented on the State of the Schools
report and our outreach efforts. Next year we would like to again try to have someone of Angus
King's caliber speak national and international educational initiatives. In addition, we want to
consider having teams of teachers present the building based plans similar to what was done this
current year on the professional development day.

The committee also discussed budget communication efforts to educate the public about our
budget process and referendum vote will be made through newsletters, e-mail, phone, and
outreach to the press. The committee confirmed that we will use the phone alert system to
remind people about the Annual Budget Meeting scheduled for June 8" and the Referendum
vote scheduled for June 14". Building Administrators will be asked to keep this item in the
forefront as they communicate with parents.

D. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported support staff negotiations will be held Thursday night;
Administrator negotiations are moving forward; and mediation sessions with the teachers are
scheduled for May.
