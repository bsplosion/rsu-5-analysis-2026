D. The Board listened to public comment and considered next steps in the budget approval
process. The Board is committed to the development of a fiscally responsible budget that
continues to provide a strong educational program. The Board established the following
schedule for public input and action on the development of a revised 2009-2010 Budget.

Key upcoming dates in the budget process are:

¢ July 8, 2009 — Public input and Board action on the revised 2009-2010 Budget.
At this meeting, the Board will make their final vote on the budget to send to the
Annual Budget Meeting. This meeting will begin at 6:30 P.M. in the Freeport
High School cafeteria
¢ July 21, 2009 — Annual Budget Meeting. At this meeting, registered voters of all
three towns will deliberate on the budget approved by the Board and vote on an
Article-by-Article basis, with the option of proposing changes through
amendments on each Article. The meeting will not end until a simple majority
approves a budget to send to referendum. This meeting will begin at 6:00 P.M. in
the Freeport High School gymnasium
¢ July 22, 2009 — Absentee Ballots accepted at each of the town offices for the
referendum vote on the budget approved at the Annual Budget Meeting.
¢ July 28, 2009 — Budget Validation Referendum. Voters of all three towns will
enter a yes or no vote on the Budget approved at the Annual Budget meeting at
the voting booth in each town. A simple combined majority of the voters in the
three towns is needed to approve the budget. If the voters reject the budget, the
Board will need to reconsider the budget and work through both the Annual
Budget Meeting and the Budget Validation Referendum until the voters approve a
budget.
During deliberations on the budget, the Board considered the best approach to responding
to the feedback from the polls. The budget that failed at referendum reflected more than
$650,000 of cuts from the budgets proposed by each of the now defunct local school
boards. The Board directed administration to bring detailed information to the July 8"
meeting regarding further reductions to the proposed budget in the following areas:
¢ Salary reduction due to curriculum director vacancy;
¢ Business office reduction of position from full time to part time;
¢ Reduction of high school teaching position;
* Consideration of contracted bus services in place of purchase of two new buses;
* Consideration of contracted payroll services
