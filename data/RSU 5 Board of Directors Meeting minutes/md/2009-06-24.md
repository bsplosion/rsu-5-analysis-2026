5. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Superintendent Welsh informed the Board the meeting would not be videotaped because there
wasn't anyone available to tape. The minutes will be posted on the website and Betsy Peters
offered to put her name as a contact if a member of the public wished to have a conversation
about items discussed at the meeting.

Superintendent Welsh congratulated everyone on the budget and the great turnout for the Annual
Budget Meeting. The next step is the Budget Validation Referendum on June 25". After the
polls close on the 25" and the votes are counted the town clerks from the three towns will call
with the results. The budget passes or fails by a simple majority of the combined voters from the
three towns. An e-mail will be sent to the Board notifying them of the results. If the budget fails
the budget process will be repeated with the adoption of a budget by the Board, an Annual
Budget Meeting of the voters from the three towns, and another Budget Validation Referendum.
The district would then operate under the Board adopted budget until a new budget is approved
by the voters.

B. As of July 1* the Board will be an official Board, no longer an interim one. The July 8" meeting
will be an organizational meeting. A Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson and sub-committee
members will be elected. A list of sub-committees was provided to the Board members with a
brief explanation of the duties and time commitment of each committee. Superintendent Welsh
read through the list. In addition to the list provided there will be ad-hoc committees for Board

8. PERSONNEL:

Superintendent Welsh explained the hiring process to the Board. An interview team is created to

screen applications and conduct interviews. References are then checked and the top two

candidates have a second interview with the Superintendent and Principal. A final nomination

is then made to the Board. Hiring is a three-step process: the Superintendent nominates; the

Board approves the nomination; the Superintendent hires. Board members indicated they were

pleased there was a large pool of applicants to choose from.

A. Voted: (6) To approve a Teacher Certification Plan for the RSU.

(Peters — Parker) (10 — 0)
Superintendent Welsh held a meeting with the Certification Chairs from the three towns to discuss a
Certification Plan for the RSU. A Certification Plan is a Plan for teachers by teachers and the RSU
must have one in place for July 1st. The Certification Plans from all three towns were reviewed and
merged together by Anne-Marie Spizzuoco, Freeport's former Certification Chair. Because a Chair
must be in place for July 1* to sign off on teacher recertification applications the following wording
was added to the plan: “The Superintendent will appoint the first RSU5 certification chairperson and
the subsequent chair will be elected by the following process”. This statement will be placed in front
of the election process described in the Plan. Once the Board approves the Plan it will be forwarded
to the Maine Department of Education and a Chair can be appointed.

13. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications: Betsy Peters reported the committee met on June 15". The Committee
created signs and discussed ways to get the word out about voting on the budget on June 16" and
25"". The Committee discussed a Robocall system: Dial my calls.com. This is a message
distribution via phone. A Robocall test was done with 215 phone numbers of parents, 90% of
the calls were connected. The Communications Committee recommends adding Durham and
Pownal parents to the existing message system (School Messenger) for $1,125 for the 09-10
school year. The current use is limited to emergency announcements such as snow days, etc.
The Communications Committee would like to consider additional parameters such as absences;
school announcements; and district announcements. Parents would have the option to opt-out if
they didn't want to receive the phone calls.

The Communications Committee recommends a website framework be put in place that links the
RSU District page with the current school websites. This target date continues to be August 15,
2009. The District Technology Committee will use the district's vision for the use of technology
to create a Technology Plan and timeline for training and implementation of the new website and
other technology systems. Currently the three towns use three different Student Information
Systems. The Plan will include recommendations and expectations regarding staff utilization of
e-mail and websites as communication tools, student information and electronic grading systems.
The Plan will be completed by January 1, 2010. A second target date of March 15 was
established for implementation of these elements with the rollout of information beginning in
January.

It was explained to the Board that Superintendent Welsh would draft a process and
implementation plan for community involvement in naming the RSU. This will be presented to
the Board for consideration of action in September. The goal is to have as many people as
possible involved in the process and that is difficult this time of year.
