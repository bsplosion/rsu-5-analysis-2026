6. OLD BUSINESS:
A. Adoption of Budget FY' 10
Vice-Chair John Morang announced that the Freeport School Committee voted on Monday, June
1, 2009 to return tuition funds to M.S.A.D. #62 on the tuition premium currently paid for
students tuitioned to Freeport High School. The premium, approximately $44,310, is part of a
tuition contract between M.S.A.D. #62 and Freeport for funding for capital expenses on the high
school. Vice-Chair Morang noted that Durham students attended Freeport High School at the
state tuition rate and were not charged the tuition premium this year. The Freeport School
Committee felt it was fair to Pownal to return the tuition premium to make it equitable. The
funds can be used by Pownal as carry-forward to off-set the tax impact for the proposed 2009-
2010 budget.
Superintendent Welsh explained the Board will be voting on the budget Article by Article. The
Board could adopt the bottom line of the budget but wanted to vote on it this way to help the
public understand the process for the June 16'" Annual Budget Meeting. The public was
