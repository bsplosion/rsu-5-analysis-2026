7. OLD BUSINESS:
A. Review Budget FY'10
This was the first time since March 28, 2009 the Board and public had a chance to thoroughly
review the budget Article by Article. Superintendent Welsh indicated the biggest change in the
budget is the way we need to report stimulus funds. The Department of Education requires that
the $620,207 in Stimulus funds be pulled out of the budget and reported separately as grant
funds. The Board reviewed each article of the budget and the packet of handouts. Net budget
reductions to date total $620,465. Budgetary increases in positions and programs were discussed:
¢ Article 1 (Student and Staff Support): Increase due to moving the Technology
Director position from .75 to full time. This increase is necessary due to the
increase in responsibility of combining three towns technology departments.
¢ Article 3 — (Operation of Plant): Addition of a full time district-wide maintenance
worker due to an increase in square footage upon becoming an RSU.
¢ Article 6 (Regular Instruction): Increase of 3 FTE teaching positions at the High
School. This increase is due to large class sizes in some sections at the high
school. This will allow the high school to add more electives and lower class sizes
in grade 9. Virtual High School is an on-line program to provide students with an
opportunity to access more classes 24/7. This program will be more cost effective
then adding staff.
¢ Article 7 (Other Instruction): Stipend positions have been added due to increased
participation and additional offerings for students. An Athletic Trainer is being
added for the safety and welfare of the students. This is a standard of care that is
expected. The trainer will work with all high school, and possibly middle school,
athletes for preseason conditioning, during practices and games and with
rehabilitation after injuries occur.
¢ Article 8 (System Administration): The addition of 1 position in the Business
Office is due to the RSU taking on services previously provided by the Town of
Freeport and the added responsibilities that come with joining an RSU.
The budget timeline was reviewed with the Board scheduled to adopt the budget at a special
meeting scheduled for June 3™. At that meeting the Board will once again review the budget
Article by Article and make adjustment as needed. The Annual Budget Meeting is scheduled for
June 16" and Budget Validation Referendum is June 25". The public was encouraged to attend
all meetings.
