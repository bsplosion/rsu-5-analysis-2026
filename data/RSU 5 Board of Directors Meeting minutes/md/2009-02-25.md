5. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Business services and Technology: Superintendent Welsh provided an update on meetings
that have taken place regarding business services and technology. Technology
representatives and Superintendents from the three communities met on February 24, 2009
to discuss technology systems, software, technology plans and needs. Common goals and
threads in each community include: teaching technology skills through content based
learning; focused support for integration of technology; and connectivity — bringing the
schools to the world. The three communities have similar systems approaches to labs, cows
(computers on wheels), electronic gradebook, e-mail and websites.

The Business Managers and Superintendents met recently to discuss district needs before
July 1, 2009. They agreed on assigned work for each Business Manager and established
deadlines. Examples of financial related work to be completed include selection of a
financial software system; transfer of deeds to the RSU; transfer of Durham's
construction contracts; and retirement system/social security related issues. Mid-term
needs include changing of name and address; closing funds and establishing new
accounts; and budget work. The State will provide $25,000 for training and conversion
for a financial software system.

The Finance Sub-Committee met on February 12, 2009 and elected John Morang as
Chair of the committee. The Finance Sub-Committee has oversight responsibility for
finance operations of the RSU. Administrators will make recommendations to the
committee for their consideration and recommendation to the full Board. At their first
meeting, they considered financial policies to recommend to the Policy Committee and
reviewed information related to the proposed bus contract with Bo-Mar. The decision
was made to recommend the Bo-Mar bus contract to the full Board for approval.
On February 23, 2009 a meeting with the Commissioner of Education was held. The
discussion centered around penalty money and how it might be distributed. The stimulus
package and recommendations for use of the funds were also discussed.

B. Educational component and Special Services: Superintendent Tomaszewski provided an
update on the February 10, 2009 meeting with Bob Strong, Freeport High School
Principal, Superintendent Tomaszewski and Superintendent Welsh. The meeting
centered around what educational enhancements could be put in place at the high school
level as we consolidate. The Program of Studies, enrollments, scheduling, Professional
Learning Communities, student supports, Virtual High School and advance placement
were reviewed and discussed. Virtual High School is a wonderful opportunity for
students with over 300 online courses available. Administration has been directed to
continue to work to bring about Virtual High School as well as a credit recovery program.
Joan Nason, Freeport Special Services Director, Superintendent Tomaszewski,
Superintendent Welsh and Will Pidden, Durham Elementary Principal, met on
February 9, 2009 to discuss Pre-K to eighth grade special education. Specific areas
included what currently exists, staffing, schedules, contracted services and services that
are currently provided. Immediate needs were identified. We provide a range of services
in all three districts. Similarities exist in philosophies as well. Next steps will be to meet
again to discuss services for high school students.

6. PUBLIC COMMENTS:

Ms. Lois Kilby-Chesley, Durham resident, spoke about her concerns with the make up of the

administrator contracts under Executive Session action. Ms. Kilby-Chesley also publicly thanked

Representative Michaud, Representative Pingree, Senator Collins and Senator Snowe for helping

to bring money into the district that will protect the jobs of educators.

8. OLD BUSINESS:
Chairperson Poissonnier updated the Board on the Superintendent hiring process. The Board
Chair and Vice-Chair selected community and staff members from each town to serve on the
Search Committee. Committee members attended a training session with Dale Douglass of
Maine School Management on February 24, 2008. Surveys were compiled and the top four
qualities were identified. The search committee is in the process of developing interview
questions. Interviews will be held on March 4, 2009 with a vote by the full Board on
March 11, 2009. The search committee will use majority votes and the Board weighted votes.
