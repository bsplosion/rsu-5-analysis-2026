5. WORKSHOP: FY13 Budget Review:

A. Kelly Wentworth, Director of Finance provided an overview of the budget to date. The budget
was presented to the public on March 27" and March 28". Two documents are available, the
budget itself and a packet of more detailed information such as enrollments, priorities, reserve
funds, tax impact to each town. The proposed budget has a net increase of 1.1% overall. The
district will receive more state aid in the amount of $329,609 and we will need to raise an
additional $225,532 locally to receive the additional state aid. Ms. Wentworth also noted all
budget documents and videos of the meetings are online at www.rsu5.org
At the March 28" meeting the Board asked what priorities were not in the budget.
Administrators met and developed a list of items. The Administrators confirmed that the schools
can be run with the recommended budget, but could move more quickly with the following
items, listed by priority: an additional professional “flex” day; leadership stipends; equipment;
technology; literacy/instructional support; curriculum materials; intervention supports for
students.

Board members asked questions regarding items on the priority list and next steps for the budget
process. The Board will continue to listen to input from the public at the April 25" meeting in
Durham and decided to add an additional meeting on May 2" for the Board to consider all input
and make decisions on the budget for the adoption at the May g” meeting.
