7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Technology Report — Jeff Brazee, Technology Director, provided an update on what is
happening in technology in the RSU including: big picture inventory; changes that have taken
place in e-mail communications, financial system, networked copiers, ticketing systems for
technology and maintenance; network expansion; professional development in Google
applications, e-mail, teacher websites, Note Share; challenges and work ahead; and success
stories.

B. 3-5 Year Financial Plan Presentation — Eric Dube, Finance Committee Chair, reviewed the plan
explaining that the document presents a three — five year financial analysis, projection and plan
for the RSU. It is intended to provide a long-range financial framework to support the strategic
planning of the district. Each year the plan will be updated to better reflect the current financial
conditions, in both revenues and expenditures, while taking into consideration program
improvements and initiatives.

11. UNFINISHED BUSINESS:

A. Review Budget FY'11: Superintendent Welsh reported at the April 14'" Board meeting the
Board asked Administration to contact current 5"" 6" and 7" grade families in Pownal to consider
their preferred choice of school for their child's middle school program for next year. All three
middle schools were offered as options. This was done to help better understand the number of
students who would attend each program if three choice options were provided. Letters were
sent to families via e-mail, snail mail and brought home by the students. Responses were
received from all families and the results were confirmed by Board member Eric Dube and
Board Chair Nelson Larkins.

B. A motion was made by Blais, seconded by Morang as follows: Eliminate the Pownal Middle
School program and allow Pownal students in grades 6-8 the choice of Freeport or Durham
middle school programs with transportation provided.

VOTED: (3) To amend the motion to read as follows: Eliminate the Pownal Middle School
program and allow Pownal students in grades 6-8 the choice of Freeport or Durham middle
school programs with transportation provided in the most economical manner possible up to
$5,000. (Blais — Beaulieu) (11 — 0)

VOTED: (4) Eliminate the Pownal Middle School program and allow Pownal students in grades
6-8 the choice of Freeport or Durham middle school programs with transportation provided in
the most economical manner possible up to $5,000. (Blais — Beaulieu) (5 — 6 Dawson, Dube,
Kielty, Larkins, Parker, Peters). The motion failed 490 - 508 weighted votes.

VOTED: (5) Pownal Middle School 6" grade students would have choice and Pownal students
in grades 7 through 8 would attend either Freeport or Durham with transportation provided in the
most economical manner possible up to $5,000 for the 2010-2011 school year (Parker — Kielty)
(5 — 6 Dawson, Dorsey, Holt, Dube, Kielty, Morang). The motion failed 490 — 508 weighted
votes.
