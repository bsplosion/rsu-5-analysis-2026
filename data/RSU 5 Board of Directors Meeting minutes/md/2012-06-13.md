8. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Update on Hunter Road Field Maintenance — Dennis Ouellette reported on June 5, 2012 the
Freeport Town Council accepted the 5-year contract for the RSU to maintain the Hunter Road
Fields.

B. FHS Facilities Study Advisory Final Committee Report — Principal Bob Strong thanked the
committee members for their work this past year. Lyndon Keck, PDT Architects presented the
Master Plan to the Board members. The objective of the Plan is to provide the Board with a
planning tool that identifies deficiencies and needs and provides solutions. The report outlines
deficiencies at Freeport High School in the physical structure, in allocated space for educational
services, deficiencies in exterior facilities, and identification of building components and missing
space. The Plan goes into detail in the following categories: focus and study methodology;
building history; enrollment summary; major areas of deficiency and problems; site plan
improvements and diagrams; building improvements with renovations and additions; and
building components with costs and student enrollment impacts.

C. Building Level Strategic Plans & End of Year Update— Bob Strong and Chris L' Abbe reported
on progress made during the school year. The three goals included: to raise the number of 9"
grade students reading and writing at grade level by 10%, or up to 83% according to NWEA;
increase achievement for all students with an emphasis on decreasing freshmen failure rate; and
create a plan to implement beginning steps to address flat SAT math scores and increase student
growth.

D. Building Level Strategic Plans & End of Year Update — Ray Grogan reported on progress made
during the school year. The goals included: 80% of students reading at grade level based on
NWEA or F&P reading tests (72% were at the level last year); and 63% of students make their
growth goals on NWEA reading test;

E. Building Level Strategic Plans & End of Year Update — Will Pidden reported on progress made
during the school year. The goals included: (K) increase from 85% to 90% number of
kindergarten students who meet letter sound fluency targets; (Grade 1-2) 80% of all students in
grades | and 2 will meet the respective end of year reading benchmarks according to F&P
testing; (Grade 3-4) increase words per minute within oral reading fluency, for students below
target speed, by one level on proficiency rubric; (Grade 5-6) 60% of Sth grade students and 80%
of 6th grade students will meet the target for fact fluency; and (Grade 7-8) 7th and 8th grade
NWEA growth targets will increase by 10 percentage points to 55% and 65% respectively.

F. Building Level Strategic Plans & End of Year Update — Lisa Demick reported on progress made
during the school year. The goals included: 80% of students will make at least a year's growth in

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported all collective bargaining agreements are in place.

B. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on May 9, 2012. Kelly Wentworth, Finance
Director reviewed the revised Budget Impact sheet which reflects the additions made at the May
2, 2012 Board Meeting. The May 9, 2012 revision also includes the anticipated revenues and
expenses of maintenance of the Town of Freeport's Hunter Road Fields Complex. Kelly
reported that she had received an email from the Town of Durham that they had chosen to use
half of the available fund balance remaining from Durham School undesignated fund balance.
(Note: After further review by the Town of Durham it was discovered that the undesignated
fund balance from the closing of Durham School Department was added into the Town of
Durham's undesignated fund balance and was not available to help offset the RSU5 budget
impact in this budget year). In an effort to clarify the impact of the budget on each community
the committee reviewed and made suggestions to the impact summary sheet included in the
budget summary packet handed out at the May 23, 2012 Annual Budget Meeting. There was
much discussion of how the current cost sharing agreement impacts each community in the
district. Eric Dube suggested that the Finance Committee look at the impact of the cost sharing
formula since the creation of the RSU. Eric Dube also felt it is important for the committee to
explain the formula and it's impact to the taxpayers before the next budget process.

The draft versions of the 2012/2013 Finance Meeting Schedule and Warrant Schedule were
handed out.

C. Strategic Communications — Jane Blais reported the committee met on May 31, 2012. At the
meeting Joe Makley, Curriculum Director, joined the group to review the Work and College
Readiness materials developed over the course of this school year. The Standards have been
created in draft form and reviewed by staff members. The Standards are supported by draft
Measurement Topics and Scoring Guides. These will be finalized over the summer for Board
approval at the beginning of the next school year.

Following a review of these materials, members supported the recommended change to the
Strategic Framework to reflect that these will be piloted in 2012-2013 with baseline data
gathered in 2013-2014. The assessment measures from the work and college readiness
standards will be demonstration based instead of solely focused on exhibitions. This change to
the Strategic Framework will also be recommended to the Board for consideration.

Additional changes to the document are recommended for Fountas & Pinnell to reflect our
current practice of using this assessment in grades K-8 and in the District Writing Prompt to
reflect an implementation timeline similar to Work and College Readiness.
