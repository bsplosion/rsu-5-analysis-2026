6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government: Members of Student Government reported on the following:
Interact and JMG will participate in a wellness walk-a-thon in April; self defense class; volunteer
activities students are participating in; Poetry Out Loud competition; MLTI will use art work
from a FHS senior as their screen saver; and mid terms are currently taking place.

B. Mast Landing School Student/Teacher Update: Gayle Wolotsky and two of her students
showcased how they won $1,000 in the S.W. Cole Digging into Science contest. The class made
a video demonstrating what they learned about renewable energy and taking care of the planet.
The video provided an interview with the president of Ocean Renewable Power Company and
showed how they use the ocean for energy.

7. REPORTS FROM ADMINISTRATORS:

A. Upate on Pownal 6" Grade Options — Lisa Demick reported the past two years all of Pownal's 6"
graders attended FMS. This year all students will attend FMS unless she hears otherwise from
parents. Ten of the thirteen current 5" graders have a sibling at FMS.

8. REPORTS FROM Superintendent:

A. Update on Professional Growth and Evaluation System — Superintendent Welsh reported the
committee has been working for the past three years on the set of evaluation requirements from
the state. We have a teacher evaluation system in place using a rubric and this year we are
looking at a deeper level. The committee has set aside the student growth piece until we have
direction from the state. We have piloted student surveys at FHS with information going directly

15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Finance — John Morang reported the committee met on December 18, 2013. At that meeting the
Finance Director explained the process of competitive bidding for property and casualty, school
board legal liability, boiler and auto insurance coverages. This is required every five years and
the RSU would need to complete this in the spring. We are asking the DOE for a waiver due to
the current withdrawal process taking place. The bond representative discussion was tabled.
The committee did not meet on January 8, 2014.

B. Region Ten Technical High School Cooperative Board — John Morang reported a meeting was
held on December 16, 2013. The Board looked at the audit, hired a custodian, reported on the
Open House, and entered into Executive Session.

C. Strategic Communications — Candy Decsipkes reported the committee met on January 16, 2014.
The preliminary date for the State of the Schools report was reviewed. Both 3“ and 5" grade
students matched or exceeded the regional average in math. Grade 8 students performed well
against the state average, but somewhat below the regional average in math. On MHSA,
students continue to score well above the state averages in all subjects, and somewhat below the
regional average. The number of low-income students at RSUS increased slightly in 2013. The
High School teachers are preparing to offer their new program of studies that outlines standards

in core courses. Families of students entering 9" grade next year will be given information ;
