6. GOOD NEWS & RECOGNITION:

A. Report from Student Government — Josh Sturtevant, President of the Student Council, reported
on activities happening at Freeport High School. Mid-term exams took place last week and
second semester has begun. Many seniors have received college acceptance letters and those
acceptances are posted on the college board in the hallway. Winter sports are well underway.
The Earth Club recently received a $200 grant to purchase a composting system that they plan to
utilize at school. JMG has also implemented a milk container recycling system. Figures of
Speech and the One Acts are underway. The One Act play is student written titled Red List.
The Winter Carnival is scheduled for February 12" and on February 13" a benefit concert for
Haiti will take place at the Community Center. Josh noted that the high school has a very
involved student body.

B. Recognition of the HIN1 Planning Team — Joan Nason, Director of Student Services, reported
nine vaccination clinics were held between the end of October and the second week in January.
Six of the clinics were initial vaccinations and three were booster. Vaccinations were given to
50% of the student body. The CDC gave permission to use the left over vaccines for a staff
clinic. We will look at what went really well and what could we change for future clinics. Ms.
Nason thanked the members of the Planning Team and the volunteers who helped with the
clinics. A special thank you goes to Brenda White and Paul Conley. Dr. Welsh thanked Ms.
Nason for her leadership of the process.

C. Technology Report — Jeff Brazee introduced Gabby Bousquet, an 8" grade student at Freeport
Middle School. Gabby demonstrated how her Language Arts class incorporates technology by
using a Wiki page to post pod casts the students have created in Garage Band. The students
make their stories come to life by creating different voices for each of the characters in the story.

B. Use of Time Committee Update — Sarah Simmonds, Curriculum Director, provided an update on
the committee's work to date under their charge to consider the optimal use of time to benefit
students and staff while balancing the needs of families and the community. The Committee has
met five times since November 19" with Heidi McGinley from the Department of Education as
the facilitator. The committee has looked at the current make up of days in each town; researched
professional development; and looked at the make up of other district's calendars. Focus groups
and electronic surveys have been provided to staff and community members to gather feedback.
A recommendation on a calendar structure will be presented to the Board on February 10" with a
first read on the calendar scheduled for March 24" and action on April 28",

C. Transportation Analysis — Eric Dube of the Finance Sub-Committee reported the committee has
spent the last few months gathering and analyzing data regarding student transportation. The
goal is to provide a quality system at the most cost effective method possible. Several options
were considered including owning our own buses and employing drivers; contracting services for
drivers and buses; leasing buses while employing drivers; and options that combined these in
different ways. Full deliberation and public comment on the information will be held at the
February 10'" Board meeting with Board consideration of action anticipated at the February 24"
meeting. Mr. Dube walked the Board through the analysis and answered questions. The analysis
will be posted on the RSU website along with a video clip of the presentation in order to provide
staff and community members the opportunity to provide feedback.

9. Superintendent'S REPORT:

A. Strategic Plan Feedback: Superintendent Welsh reported the Strategic Planning Committee met
on January 19" and the Strategic Framework has been revised slightly based on feedback
recently received. Superintendent Welsh reviewed the revisions and the Strategic Framework
timeline with the Board. The goal is for the Board to adopt the Framework at their meeting on
February 24". The revised Framework will be posted on the RSU website for feedback.

B. Legislative Update — Superintendent Welsh reported the Education Committee met on January
25". The morning session was a report from the Department of Education regarding the impact
of the consolidation legislation. In the afternoon the Education Committee listened to testimony
on the law. Superintendent Welsh spoke several times regarding the three areas the Board would
like amended including; adding an opt out clause; creating one common Board member election
date; and having either a Town Meeting or Budget Validation Referendum instead of both. The
Education Committee will hold a work session on February 1* to create language for LD 570. It
is anticipated the language of the bill will be finalized by February 5".

C. Budget FY'11— Superintendent Welsh reported she hoped to have information from the State to
share with the Board this evening but unfortunately the information will not be available from

14. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):

A. Communications — Betsy Peters reported the committee met on January 7" and 21‘. At the
January 7" meeting the committee reviewed communications efforts during the past 6-8 months
and discussed plans for the future. The committee discussed what went well and areas for
improvement. One method of increasing the frequency of communication is for Superintendent
Welsh to distribute a bi-weekly update entitled “Delivering on the Mission” to keep people
informed about district news and accomplishments.

The January 21" meeting focused on redesigning the website. Kristen Stetson, a web designer
from Pownal, volunteered to help with the design of RSUS.org with a focus on clarity of
message and ease of use.

The committee also discussed the idea of communication volunteers to highlight one school per
week with a target date for the first publication by late February/early March. The committee is
working on a job description for the volunteer position. The next meeting is scheduled for
February 4" at 7:30 a.m.

B. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on January 13" and J anuary 19"". At the
January 13" meeting the curtailment for this school year of $651,872 was discussed. The
committee also discussed the anticipated state subsidy reduction of $1.8 million for next year's
budget (2010-2011). The transportation analysis was further reviewed with requests for
additional information. The January 19" meeting focused on finalizing the presentation to the
Board on the Transportation Analysis. Warrants were signed at both meetings. The next
meeting is scheduled for February 10" at 3:45 p.m.

C. Negotiations — Nelson Larkins reported two sets of negotiations are underway, Pownal support
staff and teachers. The next session with Pownal support staff is scheduled for the end of
February. A meeting was held with the teacher negotiating team to meet and greet and to
schedule sessions. The first session is scheduled for the end of February.

D. Policy — Kristen Dorsey reported the committee met on January 8'" to finalize packet 10-3 for 2
read/adoption for action tonight. A revision was made to policy DFF on how the senior class
disburses funds after graduation. The committee discussed the draft Board Self-Evaluation form
and added it to packet 10-4. Packet 10-5 was prepared for draft feedback. Included in Packet
10-5 will be the draft Superintendent's evaluation instrument. The next meeting is scheduled for
February 12" 9:15 a.m.

E. Strategic Planning — Superintendent Welsh reported on Strategic Planning under
Superintendent's Reports.
