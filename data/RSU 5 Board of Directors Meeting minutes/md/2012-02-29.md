15. BOARD COMMENTS (Reports from Sub-Committees):
A. Finance — Eric Dube reported the committee met on February 8, 2012. The committee
discussed a joint meeting with the RSUS member communities to review the 3-5 year plan and
the budget priorities approved by the Board of Directors. The meeting was set for March 14,
2012 from 6:00-7:00 p.m. A personal invitation to each Board/Council member has been sent to
their home address and by e-mail. Town Manager/Administrative Assistants were also invited.
Kelly Wentworth reported that the CBEA Superintendents and the Business Managers/Directors
had met to discuss the formation of a Health Insurance Exploration Group. Recent legislation
passed allows for districts to receive their health insurance loss ratings. This would allow for a
competitive RFP to be sent out. Members of the CBEA would explore the feasibility of this and
report findings. The Finance Committee was in support of this and the Board approved this
item this evening as well. Kelly Wentworth reported the audit has been completed. Extra time
was spent this year reviewing Adult and Community Education and recommendations have
been implemented to improve the financial reporting of this department. The Department of
Education released a revised preliminary ED279 and shows an increase in State Aid to
Education over 11/12 of $317,081. These numbers will be used in the creation of the 12/13
school budget but may be revised as the legislature works to balance the budget. Warrants were
signed. The next meeting is scheduled for March 14, 2012.

B. Negotiations — Peter Murray reported the group continues to meet but had a brief break from
meetings over school vacation. We are currently focusing on some of the bigger issues
outstanding and continue to make progress. The tone is generally good. Meetings start up again
next week with two meetings scheduled each week for the next couple of weeks.

C. Strategic Communications ~ Jane Blais reported the committee met on January 24, 2012.
Significant work has been done with staff on development of Work and College Readiness
Standards. A discussion draft has been developed that articulate the standards as well as rubrics
to guide curriculum, instruction, and assessment. Heidi McGinley, consultant, is meeting with
groups of staff to facilitate this work. The goal is to test the rubrics with existing opportunities
to learn and demonstrate these skills. Staff will then begin to identify and develop additional
opportunities. The expectation is that these will be considered by the Board for adoption by the
end of this school year. The Strategic Framework targets 2012-2013 as the baseline for this
assessment data. The Committee discussed the fact that it may take longer and will need work
to determine what we will gather and report for this element. We will continue to work and
