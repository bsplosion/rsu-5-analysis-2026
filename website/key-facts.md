## About the Proposals

**"Is PES being closed?"**

RSU 5 administration has presented scenarios that convert PES to either a preschool center or a 6th grade school. Both eliminate Pownal's K-5 elementary and bus children to Durham. No formal Board vote has been taken yet, but the only scenarios on the table other than status quo involve converting PES.

**"Does the early childhood mandate require this?"**

No. The mandate requires special education services for roughly 25 preschool-age children with disabilities. It does not require consolidating all 128 PreK students from three towns into one building. That consolidation is a policy choice, not a legal requirement.

**"Would the conversion save money?"**

No. The preschool scenario produces a net cost of $87,000 to $161,000 per year. The 6th grade scenario does not address the early childhood mandate at all.

## About Independence

**"Could Pownal operate independently?"**

Yes. At FY28 projected rates, the worst case carries a small net cost (~$79K/year, ~$124/household). Under favorable assumptions, Pownal saves money. All cost estimates with low/high ranges are published in the [full analysis](analysis.md).

**"What is the withdrawal process?"**

A 22-step statutory process under Maine law (§1466), used successfully by 43 towns since 2012. It includes a town authorization vote, a negotiation period, state review, and a final referendum where every Pownal voter decides.

**"Would this hurt Durham and Freeport?"**

Pownal students continue attending Freeport's middle and high schools. Tuition revenue stays with the district. The financial impact on Durham and Freeport is approximately budget-neutral when tuition revenue is accounted for.

**"What about Year 1 costs?"**

Year 1 carries one-time transition costs: uncapped secondary tuition rates required by statute, startup expenses, and the withdrawal process itself. These are partially offset by Pownal's share of the RSU 5 fund balance. Full Year 1 details are published in the analysis with low and high ranges.
