# RSU 5 Budget Analysis: Cross-FY Overview

## Budget Trajectory (FY17 → FY27)

RSU 5's operating budget has grown from $31.0M (FY17) to $44.5M adopted (FY26), with FY27 proposed at $47.4M. This represents a cumulative increase of 43.4% over the decade, or approximately 4.1% annually.

| FY | Adopted | Increase | % Change |
|----|---------|----------|----------|
| FY17 | $31,008,672 | $1,518,469 | 5.15% |
| FY18 | $32,310,685 | $1,302,013 | 4.20% |
| FY19 | $33,058,025 | $747,340 | 2.31% |
| FY20 | $34,192,295 | $1,134,270 | 3.43% |
| FY21 | $34,984,700 | $792,405 | 2.32% |
| FY22 | $35,714,863 | $730,163 | 2.09% |
| FY23 | $37,223,151 | $1,508,288 | 4.22% |
| FY24 | $39,080,569 | $1,857,418 | 4.99% |
| FY25 | $41,612,460 | $2,531,891 | 6.48% |
| FY26 | $44,455,929 | $2,843,469 | 6.83% |
| FY27 | $47,357,441* | $2,901,512 | 6.52% |

*FY27 is the superintendent's proposed budget (March 2026); not yet adopted.

Growth has been accelerating since FY22, with annual increases rising from 2.1% (FY22) to 6.8% (FY26). This acceleration is driven by:

- **Salary and benefits growth** (Articles 1-2): Contractual obligations, health insurance, and state EPS requirements
- **Transportation costs** (Article 9): Rising fuel, maintenance, and contract costs
- **Special education mandates**: Federal/state requirements including EC (Early Childhood) mandate effective FY28

## Key Board Decisions and Proposals by Year

### FY22-FY24: Moderate Growth Period
Budgets grew 2-5% annually with relatively modest debates. Board-adopted budgets closely tracked superintendent recommendations.

### FY25: Acceleration Begins
The 6.48% increase ($2.5M) marked a shift. Reductions were proposed to limit the increase, but most were deferred.

### FY26: Continued Acceleration
6.83% increase ($2.8M). The superintendent's handbook included 26 reduction items, establishing the tiered reduction framework (M, N, R).

### FY27: Three-Tier Proposal (Current Cycle)
The superintendent has proposed three distinct budget scenarios:

1. **With All Cuts (T1+T2)**: $47,357,441 — 6.52% increase
   - 13 Tier 1 positions cut (certified staff reductions)
   - 7 Tier 2 positions cut (support staff: ed techs, instructional strategist)
   - Superintendent's recommendation

2. **T1 Only (Restore T2)**: ~$47,768,300 — ~7.45% increase
   - 13 Tier 1 positions cut
   - T2 support positions restored (~$410,859)

3. **No Cuts**: $49,118,453 — 10.49% increase
   - Full maintenance-of-effort budget
   - No position reductions

The board is expected to adopt one of these scenarios in March 2026, with the Annual Budget Meeting in spring.

## Forward Outlook

### FY28 Projections
The superintendent projects FY28 at:
- $49.4M with continued reductions
- $54.2M without reductions

FY28 is the year when significant structural changes take effect:
- **EC Mandate**: Early Childhood/ECSE program becomes mandatory
- **PES Decision**: Whether to preserve or close Pownal Elementary

The PES preservation cost analysis (see FY28 workbook) estimates the annual cost of keeping PES open vs. closing it, considering staffing, transportation, facility, and community impact factors.

### FY29 Projections
- $52.9M with continued reductions
- $58.0M without reductions

The 4-year trajectory from FY26 ($44.5M) to FY29 ($52.9M-$58.0M) implies 6-9% annual growth, far outpacing enrollment trends and typical inflation.
