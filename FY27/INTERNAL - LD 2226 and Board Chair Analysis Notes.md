# LD 2226: Analysis and Open Questions

**March 2026** | Working notes

## Background

LD 2226 ("An Act to Amend the Essential Programs and Services School Funding Formula"), HP 1503, was introduced in the 132nd Maine Legislature, Second Regular Session. Referred to Education and Cultural Affairs on March 3, 2026. Committee voted March 19, 2026: divided report, 11-2 Ought to Pass as Amended (majority). Not yet reported to the floor.

**Sponsor:** Rep. Kelly Murphy of Scarborough (committee chair).

**Origin:** Reported out by the committee pursuant to Resolve 2025, chapter 84, section 2, subsection 4. The committee's printed disclaimer states it "has not taken a position on the substance of this bill" and is reporting it solely "for the purpose of having a bill printed that can be referred to the committee for an appropriate public hearing and subsequent processing."

---

## What LD 2226 Does

LD 2226 is a **statewide EPS formula update**. It changes how the state calculates allocations and contributions across all Maine school administrative units. Key provisions:

1. **Income-adjusted fiscal capacity** (Sections 19-20, amending §15688(3-A)): Adjusts each municipality's property fiscal capacity by an income index based on the percentage of economically disadvantaged students. Municipalities with more economically disadvantaged students would see their fiscal capacity adjusted downward, reducing their Required Local Contribution (RLC) and increasing their state aid. This operates at the state level across all 189+ districts.
2. **Variable poverty weight** (Section 6, amending §15675(2)): Changes the weight for economically disadvantaged students from a fixed value to a range of .15 to .35, based on an index developed by MEPRI and updated at least every 5 years.
3. **Regional cost-of-living adjustment** (Section 18, new §15682-A): Replaces the old regional adjustment with one based on a nationwide cost-of-living index, aligned to the teacher salary matrix.
4. **Transportation cost reform** (Sections 5, 16): New predicted transportation cost model averaging a pupil density model with the most recent year's actual costs adjusted for inflation. Caps predicted costs at 105% of the most recent year's reported net transportation expenditures.
5. **Special education changes** (Sections 13-15):
   - Prevalence threshold increased from 15% to 17%
   - New 80% cap on maintenance-of-effort special education expenditure adjustment
   - Elimination of midyear funding adjustments for out-of-district special education placement costs
6. **Other**: Removes per-pupil professional development cost component, adds instructional staff support component. Charter school payment clarifications.

**Scope:** LD 2226 changes state-level EPS parameters. It does not change any RSU's internal cost-sharing formula. RSU 5's Additional Local Monies (ALM) formula (Durham 21.42%, Freeport 65.98%, Pownal 12.60%) is a local Board decision under §1481-A, separate from the EPS formula.

## Independent Analysis of LD 2226 Impact on RSU 5

Modeling the LD 2226 provisions across the range of plausible parameters (poverty weight .15 to .35, different MEPRI index assumptions, transportation cap scenarios), the results were consistent:

- **All three towns saw similar rate increases** under the EPS changes, regardless of which formula variant was modeled
- The bill is **distributionally neutral** for RSU 5: it raises the overall cost floor but does not meaningfully shift the split among the three towns
- RSU 5, as a relatively affluent southern Maine district, would likely see a net reduction in state funding under all three parameter sets. This is consistent with the bill's statewide purpose of redirecting aid toward higher-poverty districts.

## Numerical Verification

### Method

Under the current ALM formula (fixed percentages), a state funding reduction of $X increases the ALM pool by $X. Each town absorbs its fixed share. The chair's "tax impact" percentages appear to use each town's total tax commitment (RSU + county + town) as the denominator.

Estimated total tax commitments:

| Town | Taxable Valuation | Mil Rate | Estimated Total Tax |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Durham | $389,647K | 33.58 | ~$13,085K |
| Freeport | $2,613,679K | 13.85 | ~$36,199K |
| Pownal | $392,398K | 15.30 | ~$6,004K |

### Predicted incremental impacts under current ALM

| Town | ALM Share | Absorption Factor (share / total tax) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Durham | 21.42% | 0.001637% per $1 |
| Freeport | 65.98% | 0.001823% per $1 |
| Pownal | 12.60% | 0.002099% per $1 |

| Scenario | Durham (predicted) | Freeport (predicted) | Pownal (predicted) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| $200K | 0.33% | 0.36% | 0.42% |
| $644K | 1.05% | 1.17% | 1.35% |
| $1.44M | 2.36% | 2.63% | 3.02% |

### Comparison

| Scenario | Durham (stated / predicted) | Freeport (stated / predicted) | Pownal (stated / predicted) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| $200K | 0.31 / 0.33 | 0.35 / 0.36 | 0.40 / 0.42 |
| $644K | 1.05 / 1.05 | **1.35 / 1.17** | 1.35 / 1.35 |
| $1.44M | 2.36 / 2.36 | 2.63 / 2.63 | 3.02 / 3.02 |

### Observations

**Proposals 1 and 3** are approximately consistent with the current ALM formula. The small deviations (e.g., 0.31 vs. 0.33) are within rounding and the uncertainty in total tax commitment figures.

**Proposal 2 ($644K)** shows a discrepancy for Freeport: the stated incremental impact is 1.35%, but the current ALM formula predicts 1.17%. That gap represents approximately $65K of additional burden on Freeport beyond what the current formula would allocate.

**Possible explanations:**

1. The three LD 2226 parameter scenarios may produce different RLC distributions. If the $644K scenario's specific income adjustment hits Freeport's RLC harder than the other two scenarios, the difference could appear even at current ALM percentages. Verifying this would require the specific MEPRI poverty index values for each town.
2. The Proposal 2 calculation may use different ALM assumptions than Proposals 1 and 3.
3. Rounding or calculation error, though the magnitude ($65K on a $644K reduction) would be unusually large.

## Two Methodological Questions

### 1. Terminology: "re-allocation of the funding formula"

LD 2226 adjusts statewide EPS parameters. It does not change the RSU's internal cost-sharing formula. The distinction matters:

| | LD 2226 (statewide EPS reform) | ALM formula reform (local RSU 5 decision) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Scope** | All 189+ Maine school administrative units | RSU 5 only |
| **Mechanism** | Updates state-level EPS parameters | Changes how the ALM pool is split among towns |
| **Authority** | Maine Legislature | RSU 5 Board vote under §1481-A |
| **Effect within RSU 5** | Distributionally neutral (similar rate impact across towns) | Directly shifts costs between towns |

The chair's description of her analysis as a "re-allocation of the funding formula" could refer to the statewide EPS reallocation (accurate at the state level) or to a local ALM formula change (a separate Board-level decision). Clarifying which was intended would resolve the ambiguity.

### 2. Direction of any formula adjustment

The Finance Committee has historically recommended **enrollment-weighted** ALM reform (85/15, 65/35), which would increase Durham's share and decrease Freeport's and Pownal's. LD 2226 introduces a different concept: **income-adjusted fiscal capacity**, which accounts for the fact that property values do not always reflect ability to pay. If this concept were applied to the ALM formula, it would move in the opposite direction from the Finance Committee's recommendations:

| Direction | Durham Effect | Freeport Effect | Previously Recommended |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Enrollment-weighted (FC proposals) | Pays more | Pays less | Yes (2016, 2019, 2023) |
| Income-adjusted (LD 2226 concept applied locally) | Could pay less | Could pay more | Not previously considered |

The Proposal 2 discrepancy, where Freeport absorbs more than the current formula predicts, is consistent with the income-adjusted direction. Whether this reflects a deliberate methodology choice or a different explanation (see Possible Explanations above) is an open question.

## Context

The ALM formula has been a recurring topic within RSU 5. Key facts for context:

- The current ALM percentages (21.42 / 65.98 / 12.60) were set in 2008 and have not been adjusted
- The Finance Committee recommended enrollment-weighted reform in 2019; the Board voted it down 450-548 (weighted)
- A change to the ALM formula requires a Board vote under §1481-A
- Durham's ALM share (21.42%) is approximately 14 percentage points below its enrollment share (35.13%); Freeport's ALM share (65.98%) is approximately 11.5 points above its enrollment share (54.47%)
- When state aid is included, total per-town contributions are closer to proportional (omnibus Section 5.1)
