## Three Budget Tiers Under Consideration

The Superintendent presented three tiers to the Board, ranging from maximum cuts to full maintenance of effort:

| Tier | Total Budget | % Increase | Positions Cut | Description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **With All Cuts (T1+T2)** | $47,357,441 | 6.52% | 20 positions | Superintendent's proposed budget |
| **T1 Only (Restore T2)** | $47,768,300 | 7.45% | 13 positions | Restores 7 T2 ed tech/support positions |
| **No Cuts** | $49,118,453 | 10.49% | None | Full maintenance of current programs |

The proposed budget ($47.36M) already includes $1,715,649 in cuts from the Superintendent's initial request of $49.15M. These are real losses -- every elementary school lost a teacher or ed tech, FMS and FHS lost multiple positions, and the district-wide ESOL educator and instructional strategist were eliminated.

## What PES Contributes

- **Enrollment is growing.** PES is the only elementary with positive 4-year enrollment growth (+16 students, from 89 to 105). MSS (-42) and MLS (-17) are declining. [^1]
- **Class sizes are small by design.** 13-18 students per single-section grade.
- **Special education costs are below average.** PES spends $463/student *less* on special education than the district elementary average. [^2]
- **The building has room.** Designed for ~180 students, currently serves 105.
- **Staffing is the leanest in the district.** PES is the *only* school whose total staffing declined over the past decade (-8.0%), while every other school grew (+3.9% to +36.5%) and central office positions grew +34.6%. [^45]

## How Each Tier Projects to FY28

Whatever FY27 budget voters approve becomes the FY28 baseline. Applying the 3-year average growth rate (6.10%) plus the EC mandate cost (~$514K net):

| FY27 Tier Adopted | FY27 Total | → FY28 Projected | FY28 Increase |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| With All Cuts (T1+T2) | $47,357,441 | ~$50.8M | ~$3.4M |
| T1 Only | $47,768,300 | ~$51.2M | ~$3.5M |
| No Cuts | $49,118,453 | ~$52.6M | ~$3.5M |

Each tier carries forward into FY28 with compounding effects. Higher cuts in FY27 produce a lower FY28 baseline but may require rehiring positions to meet enrollment needs. The FY27 choice directly constrains FY28 options.
